# ZAO Stock 2026 - Guest Experience Narrative

## The Day (as a story)

You drive into downtown Ellsworth on a Saturday morning in October. The leaves are turning. Every car heading to Acadia passes through here but most never stop. Today they will.

You see the Parklet before you hear it. Franklin Street is closed off, picnic tables are out, and there's a DJ warming up on a small stage between Main Street and Store Street. The sound carries across the block. You park on a side street and walk toward it.

At the entrance there's a check-in table. Scan a QR code with your phone - you just got a digital collectible marking that you were here. No wallet needed, no crypto jargon. Just a thing you'll have forever that proves you showed up on day one.

The first thing you notice is the smell. Fogtown's wood-fired pizza is right around the corner. A food truck is set up on the edge of the Parklet. Someone hands you a cold water. You grab a coffee from Precipice down the street.

The first artist takes the stage at 12:30. Twenty-five minutes of original music. No covers. No backing tracks. Just an independent artist performing their own work to a crowd of people who actually came to listen. Between sets, the DJ keeps the energy moving. No dead air. No awkward transitions.

By the third artist you've met someone new. You're sitting at a picnic table with people from three different states. One of them found this through Farcaster. One lives two blocks away. One drove from Bar Harbor after seeing a flyer at Vinyl Vogue - the record shop literally next to the Parklet.

There's a merch table. ZAO Stock t-shirts. The logo is clean. You buy one because you want to remember this.

The artists are real. You can talk to them between sets. They're standing right there, drinking the same coffee you are. No VIP section. No barriers. This isn't a festival that separates performers from people.

At 6pm the last artist finishes. Zaal gets on the mic for thirty seconds. Thanks the crowd, thanks the artists, thanks Heart of Ellsworth for the venue. "Walk thirty seconds that way for the after-party at Black Moon."

You walk to Black Moon Public House. Steve Peer's bar. Live music continues. The energy shifts from outdoor festival to intimate pub. Craft beer, flatbread, and a room full of people who just shared something together.

On your drive home you check your phone. There's a livestream replay already posted. Photos from people you just met. The digital collectible is in your wallet. You text a friend: "you need to come next year."

## What Makes ZAO Stock Different (for the pitch)

1. **No barriers between artists and audience.** Artists hang out at the same picnic tables.
2. **Built by the community, not a promoter.** 14 team members, 4 teams, weekly standups for 6 months.
3. **Onchain transparency.** Every dollar in and out is visible. Artist payments via 0xSplits are instant and automatic.
4. **Part of something bigger.** Art of Ellsworth, Maine Craft Weekend, statewide promotion. Not an isolated event.
5. **Year 1 of a multi-year commitment.** Zaal lives here. This isn't a one-off. The team is building infrastructure that carries over.
6. **Virtual + IRL hybrid.** Livestream built into the platform. Global audience watches while local audience experiences.
7. **90+ weeks of governance.** The community that built this has been meeting every Monday for almost two years. This isn't a startup - it's a movement.
