## Draft

Today I built something I've been thinking about for a while: a voice calibration loop.

Before ZOE generates any social content, she now asks me for 1-3 example posts in my actual voice — things I've recently written, or anything that sounds like me right now. She studies them, extracts the patterns, then applies them to whatever she's drafting. Ask-then-learn instead of assume-then-generate. The output is already better. When you train the model on your actual words instead of a description of your words, something different happens. It's the difference between a ghostwriter who read your work and one who just read your bio.

This came out of Doc 358 — a brand voice analysis I ran on the ZAO OS corpus. Turns out I have some consistent patterns: short declarative sentences. Specific numbers. Named people. Technical + personal in the same breath. "The fractal is live and it costs $0.50 to stake" is more ZAO than "we're building decentralized governance." I know this intuitively but seeing it written down made it actionable. The /socials skill now bakes it in.

On the ZAO Stock side: the weekly standup structure is live. Team rosters, partners page, the whole thing. Shawn is on Music (not Sean — fixed that too). The standup model dropped attendance tracking in favor of team reports — less friction, more signal. The right move. What matters isn't who showed up; it's what moved.
