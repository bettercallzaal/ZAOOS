## Draft

Thursday evening we deleted 20 repos. That sounds dramatic but it was the right move — 20 legacy ZAONEXUS versions had accumulated over months, each one a slightly different attempt at the same thing. The new ZAONEXUS is a single repo with a canonical structure: community routes, ecosystem routes, 35+ ZAO brands organized by the same taxonomy we use internally. The BCZ website now redirects `/nexus.html` to `nexus.thezao.com/ecosystem`. The archive is preserved in the research library (doc 624). The repos are gone. That's what graduation looks like — not adding, subtracting.

While the NEXUS consolidation was wrapping up, we also finished the architecture for the ZABAL POIDH airdrop program (doc 626). The headline: Empire Builder PULLS from BCZ, not the other way around. We publish a public JSON feed at `bettercallzaal.com/poidh-leaderboard.json` — a list of wallet addresses and scores based on who has submitted to ZAO POIDH bounties. Empire Builder polls that URL on its own schedule, applies ZABAL boosters, and distributes. Once we hand yerbearserker and Adrian the BCZ URL, our ongoing maintenance burden is zero. The page is live at `bettercallzaal.com/poidh.html`. The bounty playbook (doc 625) has 18 ready-to-post templates across ZAOstock, WaveWarZ, Fractal Mondays, COC Concertz, and BCZ. The infrastructure is ready. Now it runs on action.

One thing the weekend surfaced: the research library is at 626+ docs and the Bonfire corpus is still empty. ZOE has been @recall-blind since the Bonfire Bridge PR (PR #465) sat unmerged. The Bonfire memory backfill (135 files) and 8 corpus drafts are the next unlock — not a build, just a batch operation. Once that runs, ZOE can recall from the entire research library in natural language. That changes how every morning session starts.

## MINDFUL MOMENT

Three days of no commits. The lab was quiet while the week's work settled.

There's a pattern in this week's docs that I want to name: doc 622 (Ehrlichman's Impact Networks) describes the ZAO as something closer to a network of practice than a product company. The insight that landed hardest was this: in an impact network, the convener's job isn't to push — it's to create the conditions where the network moves itself. The Fractal is a condition. The research library is a condition. The Bonfire corpus will be a condition. The POIDH bounty playbook is a condition. None of these are output. They are infrastructure for other people's output.

The blockers that have been open for 27 days aren't just technical debt. They're missing conditions. When ZabalConviction deploys, the agents can start. When AgentMail opens, ZOE can see. When PR #465 merges, the loop closes. When the corpus populates, memory works. Four conditions. Four items. One Monday.

Monday intention: set the conditions. The network takes it from there.
