## Draft

There's a moment in most projects where the direction stops being theoretical and starts being locked. This week had one of those moments. Adam and I sat down and aligned on ZAOstock's commercial model — leaderboard reset mechanism, revenue structure, what the product actually is for the people who run festivals. That conversation is now doc 599, and it changed how I'm thinking about the graduation timeline. ZAOstock isn't just graduating to its own repo. It's graduating to a real business. The commercial model conversation gave it a spine.

The ZABAL Games design crystallized this week too, in a different direction than I expected. It started as a Claude Code hackathon. It's now a Farcaster vibe-coding challenge — Season 1, open to anyone building on Base, judged by ZAO members holding ZABAL. That shift matters because it means the competition lives where the ZAO community lives. Farcaster is where the signal is. And we now have zao-transcribe — a new script that pulls audio or podcast URLs and returns a clean transcript — which becomes one of the tools available to participants working on audio and music projects. We built the tool the community would need before the competition started. That's the right order.

Two other things landed this week that felt quieter but aren't. The first is SIDEQUESTZ — a design spec for a new ZOE layer that tracks goals across sessions. ZOE already sends nudges from the task queue; SIDEQUESTZ would give those nudges longer memory. You tell ZOE what you're working toward in a week or month, and it surfaces relevant captures and incomplete tasks in that frame. The second is the Jadyn Violet + UVR research (doc 600) — a DEEP tier dive into vocal stem separation tech and how it connects to the summer cipher release. Jadyn is in the ZAO orbit and has serious music production knowledge. That connection is worth developing.

## MINDFUL MOMENT

We had a conversation this week about what it means for something to "graduate." In ZAO OS, graduation means own repo, own domain, own database. Code deleted from the monorepo. No drift. The graduation is also a statement: this thing is ready to exist without me watching it every day.

ZAOstock is close to that. But the Adam conversation revealed something: graduation isn't just a technical milestone. The commercial model conversation was about making ZAOstock legible to a festival director who has never heard of Base. That's the real graduation — when the thing can explain itself to a stranger and they get it.

There's a research doc from earlier this year — I think it was the Emily Sundberg / Feed Me Open Tab deep dive — about solo media businesses. The thesis was that the bottleneck in solo media isn't content, it's the infrastructure to convert attention into relationships into revenue. ZAOstock is the ZAO's first infrastructure-first product. That's why it's graduating. Not because it's finished, but because it's ready to do that conversion work on its own.

Intention for the week ahead: make ZAOstock graduation irreversible. Lock the commercial model in writing. Ship the ZabalConviction contract. Let the agents start trading.
