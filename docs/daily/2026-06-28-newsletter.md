## Draft

**ZOE closed the loop.**

We've been building ZOE as a tool for a year. But there's a difference between a tool you use and a pipeline that runs. This week we crossed that line. ZOE can now receive a voice memo, transcribe it, research the context, write a durable doc, and open a PR — without asking for permission at each step. Eight features shipped in a single Saturday session. The most important one isn't any single capability. It's the auto-dispatch: ZOE no longer asks "should I do this?" for single research tasks. It just does it.

The research queue is the proof. Eleven open PRs as of tonight: docs on ZABAL Gamez legal structure, the ZAO agent-stack gaps, free Farcaster casting for ZOL (turns out Snapchain now has zero-cost signers — $0 per cast instead of $0.01), an SEO audit of the whole ZAO web presence, a risk register for the season. ZOE researched and wrote all of them. None of them were on a list this morning.

That's the shift: the intelligence is in the routing, not the execution. When "do the research" becomes as easy as sending a Telegram message, the bottleneck moves — from "can we research this?" to "what should we actually build?" That's a better problem to have.

## MINDFUL MOMENT

The Bonfire labeling run is 13 days overdue. Everything downstream — the 135-doc memory backfill, the cross-bot shared knowledge graph, ZOE's recall vectors — is blocked on one admin action.

ZOE can now ship eight features in a day. It still can't do the one thing that requires a logged-in human in the Bonfires dashboard.

There's a version of this that's frustrating. There's another version where it's exactly right: some things should require a human. The knowledge graph that all the agents will read from — the thing that shapes what ZOE remembers, what it surfaces, what it recommends — probably shouldn't be labeled by an automated process running at 2am. The curation is the whole point.

The overdue notice isn't a failure. It's a reminder that the most important inputs are still the ones you choose.
