## What fractal governance is (plain language)

- **Peer-evaluated**: every week, members share what they built or contributed. Their small group ranks each other. No committee. No algorithm. Peer accountability.
- **Respect-based**: the ranking produces Respect — a non-transferable, earned record of contribution. You cannot buy it. You cannot inherit it. You earn it by showing up and doing real work.
- **Self-organizing**: groups of ~6, each making its own evaluation. As the community scales, it nests — more groups, same structure — without losing the peer dynamic.
- **Composable**: Respect lives onchain (Optimism). Any project can read it, build on it, use it as a reputation signal.

The lineage comes from Eden Fractal and Optimism Fractal (Dan SingJoy + Tadas Pela). The ZAO adapted it specifically for a music/creator context, becoming the first music-focused Fractal to run this model at sustained scale.

## WaveWarzAfrica: the fractal expansion case study

WaveWarzAfrica is the first planned Fractal expansion outside of North America — standing up an African creator community with its own Fractal governance structure, inside the ZAO network.

**Why it matters as a model:**
- Proves Fractal governance is culture-portable (not just a North American / web3-native model)
- Gives African artists a genuine stake (Respect, governance influence) rather than a regional marketing page
- Creates a local-first structure: African members earn Respect for African contributions, evaluated by African peers

**The plan:**
1. Onboarding videos from core community members — trust layer for outreach
2. Artist outreach to anchor artists using videos as social proof
3. ZABAL Games as a lighter-weight entry point for non-artist contributors
4. Plain-language education: what WaveWarZ is, what a Fractal is, why it matters for African artists' earnings

Any region, any genre, any creator community can follow this template: stand up a Fractal, define local contribution metrics, connect to the global ZAO Respect/prize infrastructure.
