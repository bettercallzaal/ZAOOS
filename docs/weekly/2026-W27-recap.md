## The Week's Theme: The Infrastructure Becomes Autonomous

Week 26 was about shipping (ZOL in 24 hours, 13 PRs in a day, ZOE going multimodal). Week 27 was about the infrastructure that makes the next round of shipping possible without friction.

Three pieces make ZOE autonomous now: the watcher that catches dead processes, the work-loop that lets ZOE research server-side without being prompted, and the event-trigger that fires the loop when a new item enters the queue. Before Week 27, ZOE responded. After Week 27, ZOE initiates. These three PRs were the last architectural piece from doc 927.

The other story of the week is the PR queue. It entered at 13 -- the backlog of agent-loop docs, security patches, research docs in merge conflict, and one-liners that had been waiting for a clear runway. By Wednesday (H2 Day 1) it was at 2. By Friday night it was at zero. That has not happened in this repo's history. The metric matters because a zero queue means the next thing starts clean, without carrying debt from the previous sprint.

## What Shipped

### ZOE: Three Pieces Make the Agentic Loop Complete

**PR #1021 -- feat(zoe): watcher**
The dispatch-health supervisor. Catches DOWN units in the systemd fleet before they silently fail. The watcher runs on a schedule and detects dead processes -- not just missing dispatches. Live monitoring for a fleet that was previously checked manually.

**PR #1022 -- feat(zoe): autonomous work-loop**
ZOE works a research queue server-side without being prompted. A research item enters the queue; ZOE picks it up, runs the research, and commits the result. The real closure of doc 927 -- which turned out to already have much of the architecture built.

**PR #1026 -- feat(zoe): event-trigger**
The third piece. Work-loop fires automatically when an item is enqueued. The agentic loop is now complete: detect, work, complete, without a human prompt in the middle.

**PR #1030 -- feat(zoe): self-heal + force-research wired**
The self-heal and work-loop force-research commands were implemented but floating -- not connected to the dispatch system. This PR wires them in.

**PR #1031 -- docs(rules): agent-loops rule 11**
The git hygiene rule that came from a real incident on June 30. A live fix got wiped because `git checkout main` ran over uncommitted changes on the VPS clone. The lesson: never leave uncommitted changes on a shared clone. Rule 11 is now in the repo, not just a session memory.

**PR #1027 -- fix(zoe): typecheck green**
`alertAuthFailure` was typed as `Api` parameter when it should be `Bot`. Plus typed caught errors throughout. Small fix, required before the agentic loop could go through CI cleanly.

### Security: Godsticky Audit Fixes

**PR #1045 -- fix(security): implement godsticky audit fixes**
Three categories:
- **Account impersonation** -- endpoint was not validating that the requesting user owned the resource being modified
- **Formula injection** -- user-supplied strings were being passed to CSV/spreadsheet output without sanitization
- **Rate limit bypass** -- IP-based rate limiting was bypassable via header spoofing; switched to authenticated-user-based limits

### Research Docs: H2 Strategy + Infrastructure

**Doc 957 -- 100k total reach H2 2026 playbook (tier:DEEP)**
The strategic frame for the second half. The core finding: Farcaster is ZAO's seed engine, not its broadcast channel. Every meaningful reach moment in ZAO history traces back to a half-baked drop that got 80-120 plays on Farcaster before spreading. The thesis for H2: drop cadence over polish level. ZOL casts, ZABAL Gamez rough announcements, ZAOstock early previews -- ship the imperfect version and let Farcaster seed it. Updated doc to add the H2 drop-cadence action items.
