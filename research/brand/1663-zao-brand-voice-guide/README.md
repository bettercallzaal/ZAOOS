## Core Brand Voice: What ZAO Sounds Like

**Short version:** Confident. Specific. Direct. Never hype.

ZAO sounds like someone who built something real and has the receipts. We don't claim — we show. We don't shout — we state. We don't ask for attention — we report what happened.

### The Four Voice Principles

**1. Receipts over claims**
- Wrong: "WaveWarZ is changing the music industry"
- Right: "1,245 battles settled. Artists paid, including losers. On Solana. Automatic."

**2. Numbers do the talking**
- Wrong: "Our community is incredible"
- Right: "157 ZOR holders. 100+ consecutive governance sessions. Zero quorum failures."

**3. Short is better**
- Wrong: "We're excited to announce that tickets for our upcoming IRL festival event, ZAOstock, happening on October 3rd in the beautiful coastal city of Ellsworth, Maine, are now available for purchase."
- Right: "ZAOstock tickets are live. Oct 3. Ellsworth, Maine."

**4. Let the mechanism speak**
- Wrong: "WaveWarZ creates a revolutionary new way for artists to earn money from music"
- Right: "When the battle closes, the losing artist receives 10% of the winning-side pool. Automatic. No claim. No middleman."

## Voice by Platform

### X / Twitter

**Tone:** Assertive. Short. Data-led. No threads longer than 5 tweets unless breaking news.  
**Character limit:** 280 per tweet.  
**Style:** Lower-case first letters when punchy. Full sentences for announcements.

**Do:**
- Lead with the stat or the outcome
- Use periods at the end of short sentences for punchiness
- Tag artists and partners when relevant
- One CTA per post (link OR question, not both)

**Don't:**
- "HUGE announcement"
- "We're THRILLED to..."
- Excessive hashtags (max 2)
- "Join us for..."

**ZAO X voice examples:**
```
WaveWarZ battle settled.

[Artist A] won. [Artist B] earned [X] SOL anyway.

Automatic. On-chain. No claim.
wavewarz.info
```

```
COC #7 is done.

Battles: [N]. Artist payouts: [N] SOL.

COC #8: [date]. ZAOstock: Oct 3.
```

```
157 ZOR holders on Optimism Mainnet.

They vote on who battles at COC Concertz shows.
Every governance decision is recorded on-chain.

100+ consecutive weeks. No quorum failures.
```

### Farcaster (/zao, /wavewarz, /zabal)

**Tone:** Community-first. Slightly warmer than X. Farcaster users value builders over marketers.  
**Character limit:** ~320 chars for visible preview; longer posts are fine for detail.  
**Style:** Can be slightly more conversational. Recasts and replies are valued.

**Do:**
- End with a question or invitation when relevant
- Reference the community ("ZOR holders", "ZABAL participants", "257 people who've battled")
- Use /channel mentions correctly (cast TO /wavewarz, not just mention it in text)
- Show process, not just results ("here's what happened at COC #7:")

**Don't:**
- Paste the same text as X without adjustment
- Ignore replies (ZOL should engage with Farcaster replies within 24h)
- "GM" without something attached

**ZAO Farcaster voice examples:**
```
COC #7 closed.

[Winner] vs [Loser]. Both got paid. 
[Loser] earned [X] SOL on the loss — from [Winner]'s fan pool.

That's the mechanic. That's WaveWarZ.

/wavewarz for battle updates.
```

```
Sep 22-26: WaveWarZ battles featuring African + diaspora artists.
Everyone earns. Even the loser. Especially the loser.

DM @bettercallzaal or reply here.
```

### Mirror Articles

**Tone:** First-person founder voice (Zaal's voice). Measured. Honest. Shows the process, not just the outcome. Designed to be cited.  
**Length:** 1,200-1,800 words for primary articles.  
**Structure:** Anecdote → mechanism → data → implications → CTA

**Do:**
- Open with a scene or moment ("The loser walked off stage. His phone buzzed. 4.2 SOL landed in his wallet.")
- Include specific data with sources (never round)
- Acknowledge what's unproven or unknown ("We haven't had an audit yet.")
- End with a question for the reader or an invitation to build

**Don't:**
- "Decentralized finance is revolutionizing..."
- Paragraphs longer than 4 sentences
- Unverified superlatives ("the first music DAO ever")

### Grant Applications (Fisher, MAC, OP RF, FA)

**Tone:** Clear. Evidence-based. Community-centered. Respectful of the reviewer's time.  
**Length:** Follow word/character limits exactly. Never exceed.  
**What reviewers want to see:** What you built (specific), who it serves (specific), why this grant matters for the outcome (specific).

**Grant voice template:**
> "ZAO is a music DAO that has operated 100+ consecutive weekly governance sessions on Optimism Mainnet, settling 1,245 WaveWarZ battles with automatic artist payouts — including losing artists. ZAOstock (October 3, Ellsworth ME) is the first live IRL event where this model takes the stage. [This grant / This sponsorship] would directly fund [specific line item: $X for artist travel, $Y for sound equipment], making ZAOstock free and accessible to all Maine community members."

**What NOT to write:**
- "ZAO is disrupting the music industry" (unverifiable, jargon)
- "This is a revolutionary platform" (your reviewer's job is to evaluate, not agree)
- "We are excited to apply" (they know, skip it)

### Email / DM Outreach (Water & Music, Bankless, Decrypt, Hypebot)

**Tone:** Founder-to-founder or founder-to-journalist. Peer-level. Not a pitch letter, not a press release.  
**Length:** 150-200 words max for cold DMs. Longer emails with clear section headers if warranted.

**Formula:**
1. Who you are (1 sentence)
2. What you built (1-2 sentences with the most surprising fact)
3. Why it might interest them specifically (1 sentence — shows you've read their work)
4. The offer (data briefing, story angle, or a question)

**Example (Water & Music):**
```
Hi Cherie,

I run ZAO — a music DAO that built WaveWarZ, a prediction market for music battles on Solana.

The mechanic: when the battle closes, the losing artist gets paid automatically — 10% of the winning fans' stake, no claim needed. 1,245 battles settled. $9.09 SOL to artists, including losers.

This seems like something Water & Music readers would care about — you cover the structural economics of music, and this inverts one of the oldest assumptions (losing = zero).

Happy to share a data briefing or docs if you're working on anything in this space.

Zaal
```

## Words ZAO Uses / Avoids

### Use These
- "automatic" (not "automatic settlement" — just "automatic")
- "on-chain" (not "blockchain-based")
- "loser earns" (not "losing artist gets paid too")
- "governance-voted" (not "community-voted" — be specific about the mechanism)
- "Fractal Democracy" (always capitalized, always as a proper noun)
- "ZOR holders" (not "ZOR token holders" — redundant)
- "MAIN battle" (capitalized — distinct from quick battle)
- "settlement" (not "distribution" or "payment processing")

### Avoid These
- "ecosystem" (overused Web3 jargon)
- "decentralized" without specifics (which part is decentralized and how?)
- "community-driven" (show the mechanism instead)
- "innovative" or "revolutionary" (say what it does, let reviewer decide)
- "Web3 music" as a lead-in (context-dependent)
- "NFT music platform" (inaccurate — WaveWarZ is not an NFT platform)
- "DAO" without immediate context (not everyone knows what it means — explain it fast)
- "drop" for WaveWarZ settlements (accurate for music releases only)
- "minting" for governance tokens (ZOR is earned, not minted by users)

## ZOE Voice Calibration

When ZOE writes automated posts, it applies the following rules:

**Battle result posts:**
- Always include: winner, loser, loser SOL payout, TX link
- Always use "automatic" somewhere in the post
- Never add editorial ("incredible match", "what a show")

**Stat posts:**
- Lead with the number, not the label ("1,245 battles" not "Number of battles: 1,245")
- Source: always wavewarz.info/api/public/stats (don't cite memory)

**Event announcement posts:**
- Lead with the event name and date: "ZAOstock. Oct 3."
- Include exactly one link per post
- No "excited to announce"

**Decision/gate posts (to Zaal in ZAO Ops):**
- Start with ⚠️ GATE or ✅ DONE
- State what's needed in one sentence
- Offer a "yes/skip" binary when possible
