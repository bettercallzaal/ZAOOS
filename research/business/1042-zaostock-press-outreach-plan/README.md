### 3. A realistic staggered timeline, not one press release

Sourced guidance for a first-year, free, single-stage community festival suggests three touchpoints rather than one release:

- **~10-12 weeks out**: Initial announcement to regional/monthly-lead-time outlets (BDN, Down East Magazine, Maine Monitor) - festival founding, founder story, date/location, HoE/Maine Craft Weekend tie-in
- **~5-7 weeks out**: Lineup/programming release once artists are locked - targets weekly papers and radio (Ellsworth American, WERU, Maine Public) that can turn around coverage faster
- **~1-3 weeks out**: Final logistics push + a direct, personalized note to any journalist who showed interest earlier, with day-of press logistics (where to park, who to find on-site)

Down East's monthly production cycle genuinely needs the longer lead time; the Ellsworth American's weekly cycle doesn't, and waiting to pitch them until close to the date is fine. One release sent to everyone at once under-serves both ends of that range. [FULL - per subagent research on small-event press timelines, cross-referenced against each outlet's publication cadence]
