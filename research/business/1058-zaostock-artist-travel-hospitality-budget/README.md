# 1058 — ZAOstock Artist Travel & Hospitality: A Real Budget-Risk Finding

> **Goal:** ZAOstock's stated goal is "cover flights + lodging for traveling team members," and two artists are already confirmed traveling - AttaBotty (Jacksonville, FL) and FailOften (Kansas City). Nobody has ever priced what that actually costs, or checked it against the real budget (corrected 2026-07-12: ~$5K target, ~$1.5K on hand - not the retracted $20K figure prior docs used). This doc does that check, and the answer is a real risk, not a comfortable margin.

## Key Decisions

| Recommendation | Why |
|---|---|
| **Book flights for both artists by August 15, 2026 - this is a real deadline, not a suggestion** | October is the cheapest month on both routes (Jacksonville-Bangor and Kansas City-Bangor), but that pricing erodes the closer to the date it's booked. Waiting costs real money on a budget that has almost none to spare |
| **Book hotel rooms now, this week, not later** | Ellsworth in early October is peak fall-foliage season - rates run 30-70% above off-season, and rooms are already filling for October 1-5 as of this session (mid-July). This is the single most schedule-sensitive line item in the whole budget |
| **Confirm right now whether AttaBotty and FailOften expect a performance honorarium beyond travel coverage** | This is the single biggest unknown that could break the budget. The $1,230-$1,620 estimate below covers travel/lodging/basic hospitality only - if either artist also expects a cash performance fee, that's a genuinely new, unbudgeted cost on top |
| **Pool both artists into one shared green room, not individual riders** | Sourced small-festival practice treats this as standard, not a downgrade - it's the difference between an $80-150 hospitality cost and a much higher one. This also matches ZAOstock's existing no-frills, artist-first-not-lavish model |
| **Confirm neither artist is bringing a tour manager or crew member** | Every cost in this doc doubles if either artist travels with someone else. This hasn't been asked yet |

## Findings

### 1. Flights: real, sourced ranges for both routes

- **Jacksonville, FL -> Bangor, ME (AttaBotty):** $350-$400 round-trip. October is the lowest-cost month on this route per multiple booking-platform sources.
- **Kansas City, MO -> Bangor, ME (FailOften):** $350-$400 round-trip, with an October average around $358 per sourced data.
- **Combined flight subtotal: $700-$800.** [FULL - per subagent research, cross-referenced against KAYAK/Expedia/Kiwi fare data]

### 2. Ground transport: Bangor airport to Ellsworth (~40 min, per existing research)

A shared rental car for the visit (3 days) runs roughly $192-$243 at October peak-season daily rates ($64-$81/day); a shared shuttle, where available, would likely run $80-150 though specific published pricing wasn't found. **Realistic planning range: $150-$250.** [PARTIAL - rental rates sourced, shuttle pricing estimated since no specific published rate was found]

### 3. Lodging: real, sourced hotel rates, and the actual foliage-season constraint

Using the 4 real Ellsworth hotels already listed on the live `/ellsworth` page: Hampton Inn rates start around $308/night, Comfort Inn runs $177-267/night, both reflecting the 30-70% fall-foliage premium over off-season rates. **For 2 artists over 2 nights: $300-$620,** with the wide range driven entirely by which hotel and how close to the date it's booked - foliage-season demand in this specific corridor is a real, sourced constraint, not a generic "book early" platitude. [FULL - per subagent research, cross-referenced against the hotels already on the live `/ellsworth` page]

### 4. Hospitality: real practice for small volunteer festivals is minimal, if pooled

Sourced small-festival hospitality-rider guidance converges on a shared green room (not per-artist riders) as standard practice at this scale: water, towels, basic snacks, coffee/tea, one shared meal, a quiet rest space. **For 2 artists sharing one green room: $80-150.** This is genuinely achievable within ZAOstock's existing no-frills model and doesn't require anything ZAOstock isn't already planning to have on hand (per doc 1032's existing volunteer/hospitality infrastructure). [FULL - per subagent research]

### 5. The honest total, checked against the real budget - this is a real risk

| Scenario | Flights | Transport | Lodging | Hospitality | **Total** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Conservative | $700 | $150 | $300 | $80 | **$1,230** |
| Realistic (mid-range) | $750-800 | $200 | $450-500 | $120 | **$1,520-1,620** |
| High-end | $1,000 | $250 | $600 | $150 | **$2,000** |

Against the real ZAOstock budget confirmed 2026-07-12 (~$5K target, **only ~$1.5K actually on hand**), the realistic scenario ($1,520-1,620) is **more than the entire cash currently on hand**, and even the conservative scenario ($1,230) consumes roughly 80% of it. This is not a comfortable margin - it's a real constraint that determines whether ZAOstock can afford anything else (the $300-600 insurance policy from doc 1045, the $325 ATEM Mini Pro from doc 1030, permits, sound) before more funding comes in. Doc 1013's Key Decision already flagged that "most of the target is still unraised; funding would come via crowdfunding + sponsorships" - this finding makes that fundraising timeline directly load-bearing for whether the confirmed artists can actually travel as planned, not just a nice-to-have goal.
