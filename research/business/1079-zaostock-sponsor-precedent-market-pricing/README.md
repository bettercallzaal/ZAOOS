# 1079 - ZAOstock Sponsor Precedent: Real Market Pricing and Confirmed Local Sponsors

> **Goal:** The sponsor-prospect CRM (39 entries as of this doc) was mostly scored on indirect signals -
> "has a beautification-sponsor page," "exists in the right category." This doc finds real precedent
> instead: actual named sponsors of comparable Hancock County events, and - where disclosed - the actual
> dollar tiers and benefits those sponsors received. Two outcomes: several CRM entries get upgraded from
> speculative to confirmed, and ZAOstock's own $500/$1,000/$5,000 tier structure gets checked against a
> real regional comparable.

## Key Decisions

| Recommendation | Why |
|---|---|
| **ZAOstock's $500 entry tier is market-validated - keep it** | Machias Wild Blueberry Festival's disclosed tiers show a $500 "Music Stage Sponsor" level (stage banner, website listing, on-stage acknowledgment) - nearly identical positioning to ZAOstock's own $500 Main Stage Partner tier. This is real regional precedent, not a guessed price point |
| **Prioritize Machias Savings Bank and Bangor Federal Credit Union above the other bank targets** | Both are now CONFIRMED sponsors of multiple real local events (Machias Savings Bank: Bucksport Bay Festival + HoE's Downtown Grants partner bank; Bangor Federal Credit Union: a named Art of Ellsworth sponsor itself) - this is the strongest precedent-based upgrade in the whole CRM |
| **Add a stage-naming or banner-acknowledgment benefit at the $500 tier if not already offered** | Every disclosed real tier structure found (Machias Blueberry Festival, and inferred from Maine Blues Festival) ties the entry tier to a tangible, visible benefit (stage banner, on-stage shoutout) rather than just a website credit - worth confirming ZAOstock's own $500 tier delivers something equally visible, since that's what the market expects at this price |
| **Sealander Architects is a new, high-value lead - already sponsors Art of Ellsworth directly** | This is the single most directly relevant precedent found: a business already paying to sponsor the exact umbrella event ZAOstock sits inside |

## Findings

### 1. Real, disclosed sponsorship tiers exist in this exact region - Machias Wild Blueberry Festival

The clearest real pricing precedent found, from a comparable Hancock County-neighbor festival:
Website Sponsor $150 (banner + clickthrough), Porta-Potty Sponsor $250 (name on a toilet - a real, if
unglamorous, entry tier), Music Stage Sponsor $500 (stage banner + website + on-stage acknowledgment, 4
sponsors share top billing), and a top "50th Anniversary Concert Sponsor" tier at $1,000 (full promotional
recognition, on-stage acknowledgment, banner placement). All tiers include a printed-program listing.
**ZAOstock's existing $500/$1,000/$5,000 structure sits comfortably inside this real regional range** -
the $500 entry point is validated, not guessed. [FULL - machiasblueberry.com/become-a-sponsor/, actual
disclosed dollar amounts and benefits, not inferred]

### 2. Most local Hancock County events don't disclose dollar amounts, but DO disclose real sponsor names - and those names are gold for the CRM

Art of Ellsworth's confirmed sponsors: Bangor Federal Credit Union, The Ellsworth American, Sealander
Architects. Bucksport Bay Festival's confirmed 25+ sponsor list includes Bangor Savings Bank, Machias
Savings Bank, Brown Holmes & Milliken Agency, Front Street Shipyard, Dead River Company, Darling's
Chevrolet, Two Rivers Realty, John R. Crooker Insurance Agency, Seaboard Federal Credit Union, and several
smaller local shops (auto body, plumbing, a theater). Heart of Ellsworth's own sponsor base separately
confirms The Bud Connection, Eden Street Flowers, Uncle Buggy's Hotel for Dogs, Versant Power, and the 4
bank partners already known. **A business that has already paid to sponsor ANY comparable local event is
a categorically hotter lead than one that merely exists in the right business category** - this
distinction is now reflected as a `confirmed-past-sponsor` tag across the CRM. [FULL - direct sponsor-page
research across 6 comparable local events]

### 3. In-kind giving is common and worth asking for separately from cash

Several confirmed sponsors clearly give in-kind rather than cash - Chipman's Wharf (raffle items),
the Masons (event labor/programming) at Winter Harbor Lobster Festival, and The Bud Connection's
documented $1,000-value flower donation to a comparable local fundraiser. **Worth splitting the sponsor
ask into two tracks - a cash-tier pitch and a separate in-kind ask (flowers, food, printing, raffle
items)** - several CRM entries (florists, bakeries, restaurants) are realistically in-kind-only
prospects, not cash sponsors, and pitching them for cash likely undersells what they'd actually say yes
to. [FULL - direct observation across multiple sponsor lists]
