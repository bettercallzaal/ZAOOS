## Part 2 - Detailed Analysis of Top Pick

### Option 1: "Why You Own Your Masters on Web3"

**The Win-Condition (for BAR section)**

A musician (or producer, beat-maker, label) reads your submission and thinks: "Oh. I could own my masters on-chain instead of giving them to a label. That's mine forever. I keep the royalties. The ZAO community could help me reach fans directly."

If your submission doesn't create that thought, it misses. If it does, it converts.

**Why This Is the Right First Bounty**

1. **Evergreen (not event-tied).** Can re-run this every month with a new angle: "TL;DR version," "artist success story version," "label-free economics version." One bounty template, infinite iterations.

2. **Teaches, not just promotes.** Every submission is an artifact that educates someone. The winner is useful content Zaal can repost 100+ times. Compare to a clipping bounty (next tier) where the output is promotional; explainers ARE the product.

3. **Low barrier = high participation.** Meme, reel, song drop, thread mockup, illustrated poster - creator picks the format that plays to their strength. Participation > ~7 valid submissions.

4. **Solves a real ZAO onboarding problem.** Right now, musicians don't get why web3 matters. They see "crypto" and think "gambling." A good explainer in the format they consume (TikTok clip, Twitter meme, song snippet) changes that overnight.

5. **Viral-able.** Good explainer content gets shared 100-1000x. Even if only 1 submission wins the bounty, the 5-10 honorable mentions will be shared by their own networks. ZAO gets 50-100x reach per bounty.

6. **Community voice.** The submissions are FROM creators, TO creators. It's not Zaal explaining web3 (authority voice); it's peer explaining peer (trust voice). That converts better.

7. **Bounty Is the Pipeline.** Once this works, ZAO can batch explainer bounties: "Why You Own Your Publishing," "Why Royalties on Web3 Matter," "Why Collab Splits Work on Chain." Each one teaches a different layer of the value stack.

**Practical Mechanics**

- Prize: 0.005 ETH on Base (roughly $10-15 at current prices)
- Duration: 10 days (cast Mon, deadline next Fri)
- Judge: ZOL reviews for clarity + persuasiveness; Zaal final pick
- Winner cast: Repost winner + runner-up honorable mentions (builds perception of bounty quality)
- Follow-up: Add winner + top submissions to a `bettercallzaal.com/why-web3-matters.html` page so they become a searchable archive

**Expected Submissions & Quality Curve**

- Likely: 7-12 submissions (meme/explainer is low-barrier format)
- Good entries: 2-4 (clear explanation + production value)
- One winner: The one that makes someone GET IT the fastest

**Why $5 Works**

$5 (0.005 ETH on Base) at current prices is ~$10-15. That's small money, but:
- It's enough to cover gas + show good faith (>0.001 ETH threshold)
- It's NOT about the prize size; it's about the exposure
- A good meme gets shared 500x; each share is a new pair of eyes
- Participate in a ZAO bounty = instant credibility in that creator's network

**Why This First (Before Clipping or Poster Bounties)**

The dependency chain:
1. **First:** Explainer bounties (teach people web3 exists + why it matters)
2. **Then:** Clipping bounties (reuse content that already exists; assume people know context)
3. **Then:** Poster/design bounties (brand refinement; assumes awareness already high)

If you start with clipping bounties, you get submissions about a topic people don't understand yet. Start with teaching first.
