## The Intersection: Who Combines Both Axes?

### Direct Finding: EMPTY CORNER

**Result:** No living platform combines (A) 95%+ artist payout AND (B) social-capital governance.

#### Closest Attempts (Both Failed)

**1. Ampled (2019-2024) - The Cooperative Model**
- **What it was:** Multi-stakeholder cooperative with artist-owners, worker-owners, community-member-owners. 10+ supporters triggered co-ownership.
- **Payout:** High (exact % not disclosed publicly, but positioned as artist-friendly)
- **Governance:** Off-chain cooperative voting + on-chain staking (hybrid, not pure social-capital)
- **Why it failed:**
  - Burnout: "We do not have the resources or bandwidth to responsibly continue." (Board statement 2024)
  - Operational unsustainability despite mission-driven model and cultural fit
  - Quote: "This shouldn't be misinterpreted as a sign that cooperative platforms can't be successful" (Translation: they *can* be, but Ampled couldn't scale it)
  - Lesson: Contribution-based governance doesn't generate revenue; platform still needs to pay workers.
- **Status:** DEAD
- **Sources:** Ampled public statement 2024, Billboard coverage

**2. Nina Protocol (2021-June 15, 2026) - The Grassroots Model**
- **What it was:** 100% payout to artists, grassroots independent music community, implicit social-capital focus (contribution-based, not financial speculation)
- **Payout:** 100% to artist (0% platform extraction)
- **Governance:** Community-driven, no formal token governance; operated like a DAO in spirit
- **Why it failed:**
  - Sustainability: "We saw no viable path to sustainability at current scale"
  - Scale trap: Needed "10% of Bandcamp's daily volume" just to break even at 0% extraction margin
  - Adoption friction: Crypto wallet requirement created barrier for non-crypto creators
  - VC contradiction: Funded by venture capital, which requires returns; near-zero extraction + venture = insoluble equation
  - Metrics: 37,000 total releases, but 77% were uncollected (no sales); 97th percentile of releases = 5 collections
  - Venture timeline: Expected to reach Bandcamp-scale in 5 years with VC funding; Bandcamp took 20+ years
- **Post-mortem quote:** "It was contradictory thinking to believe economic equity for artists was ever going to come through venture capital involvement."
- **Status:** SHUT DOWN June 15, 2026
- **Sources:** Nina Protocol shutdown announcement (DJ Mag, The Fader, Nina's own blog)

#### Why These Platforms Failed: The Economic Trap

The intersection is empty because of a fundamental structural incompatibility:

1. **Social-capital governance generates ZERO revenue**
   - It distributes voting power based on contribution/reputation (non-transferable)
   - This creates no transaction fees, no marketplace taking a cut, no mechanism to fund operations
   - Example: Coordinape (DAO compensation tool) can only work when the DAO *already has* capital to distribute; it doesn't create capital

2. **Near-zero artist extraction (95-99% to artist) = near-zero platform revenue**
   - Bandcamp survives at 15-18% extraction because that funds infrastructure, user acquisition, R&D
   - Spotify/Apple Music at 30% extraction for the same reason
   - At 0-1% extraction, a platform generates: $0 if it has 0 volume, $1k if it has $100k volume
   - User acquisition in music costs $3-10 per user (proven by Spotify/Apple spend)
   - **The math:** To reach 100k active users at 0% margin requires ~$300-1,000k burn; at 1% margin on $1k annual per-user volume, it takes decades to reach profitability

3. **Contribution-based governance does NOT scale with revenue**
   - A contributor-earning DAO (Coordinape) needs external funding to operate
   - A music platform with 0% extraction has no external funding (no revenue)
   - Result: Operational stall + eventual shutdown (Ampled, Nina)

4. **The venture capital contradiction**
   - To fund a 0-extraction platform with no revenue model, you need VC
   - But VC requires returns (equity, token appreciation, or eventual exit)
   - This creates pressure to either: (a) take a cut (violates the promise), (b) grow at massive scale in 3-5 years (unsustainable economics), or (c) issue tokens and hope for speculation
   - Nina and Ampled both chose (b) + token hedging; both ran out of runway

4. **The scale requirement is real**
   - Nina: 37k releases, 77% uncollected. Max revenue: near-zero volume at near-zero margin

   - Scaling a 0% extraction platform requires 10x+ the initial capital, 10x+ the runway

## Why This Corner Remains Empty: The Honest Diagnosis

### It's Not Underexploited; It's Unsustainable

| Reason | Evidence | Implication |
|--------|----------|-----------|
| **Revenue model breaks** | Ampled + Nina both had product-market fit but ran out of cash | You cannot bootstrap this model without external capital |
| **Venture capital is incompatible** | Nina's post-mortem: "It was contradictory thinking to believe...equity...through venture capital" | If you take VC, you MUST extract more or hit unrealistic scale |
| **Contribution-based governance doesn't generate revenue** | No implementation of SourceCred/Coordinape in music context | Governance is a cost center, not a revenue center; someone must fund it |
| **User acquisition costs are real** | Music platforms spend $3-10/user in CAC; proven by Spotify/Apple | At 0-1% margin, you cannot afford paid growth; must rely on organic/community |
| **Scale requirement is steep** | Nina: needed 10x Bandcamp's daily volume; achieved 0.1% of target | Organic growth to necessary scale takes 10-20 years (Bandcamp's timeline) |
| **Operating costs are fixed** | Infrastructure, support, moderation, legal still cost ~$50k-100k/month | At near-zero extraction, you need 5-10 years runway funded externally |

### The Bandcamp Control Group

Bandcamp proves that **sustainable creator economics require 15-18% extraction.** This is not because creators don't want a better deal; it's because:

1. Infrastructure costs scale with volume (not with revenue percentage)
2. User acquisition costs are per-user, not per-transaction
3. Curation/moderation cost labor
4. Legal (IP, licensing) requires experts
5. Payment processing (Stripe, etc.) takes 3-5%

**Bandcamp's formula:** 15-18% extraction = sufficient for all of the above + modest reinvestment. Drop below 5%, and you cannot fund these categories.
