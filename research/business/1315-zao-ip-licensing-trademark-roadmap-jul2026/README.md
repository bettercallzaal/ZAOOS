## Section 2: What to Protect First (Priority Stack)

### Priority 1: WaveWarZ trademark — USPTO filing NOW

**Why:** WaveWarZ is the primary revenue-generating product, has 1,245 verified battles and $39K+ in volume, and the name is distinctive enough to register. If someone else files first, ZAO loses the brand.

**What to file:** 
- Class 41 (Entertainment services, online gaming)
- Class 35 (Business services, competition organization)
- Class 9 (Computer software, apps)

**Timeline:** USPTO examination takes 12-18 months. Filing now means registration by early 2028 — but protection begins AT FILING DATE, not approval date.

**Estimated cost:** $350 per class (TEAS standard) = ~$1,050 for 3 classes. Greg handles attorney fees separately.

**Action:** DECISION NEEDED — Zaal calls Greg (Autonomous, doc 951) and authorizes USPTO filing for WaveWarZ in 3 classes.

---

### Priority 2: The ZAO + ZAOstock trademarks — File within 90 days

**Why:** ZAOstock is growing toward IRL events. Event brands attract copycats and domain squatters. "The ZAO" is unique enough to register.

**What to file:**
- "The ZAO" — Class 41 (Entertainment/Cultural services), Class 35 (Community organization)
- "ZAOstock" — Class 41 (Live music event/festival)

**Estimated cost:** ~$700 total (2 classes each)

---

### Priority 3: Register the "loser-earns" mechanic as a provisional patent

**Why:** WaveWarZ's core innovation — that losing artists still earn a percentage of battle volume — is structurally novel. No other music platform uses this mechanic. A provisional patent costs ~$320 and holds your priority date for 12 months while you decide whether to pursue full patent.

**The claim:** "A system and method for distributing compensation to both winners and non-winners in a music prediction market, wherein participants on both sides of a binary competition receive algorithm-determined payouts from a shared prize pool."

**Action:** DECISION NEEDED — Greg files provisional patent for "loser-earns" mechanic.

Every artist who plays WaveWarZ, COC Concertz, or ZAOstock needs a brief agreement covering:

## Section 7: What NOT to Rush

**Don't patent WaveWarZ platform code:** Code patents are expensive ($15-50K), take 2-4 years, and are hard to enforce. Focus on the mechanic (provisional) and the brand (trademark). Open-sourcing selective components (wwtracker) already provides a strategic moat through adoption.

**Don't over-license immediately:** The first year should be about getting documents IN PLACE (artist agreements, license templates) — not about actively selling licenses. Focus 2026 on protection, 2027 on monetization.

**Don't let IP costs derail ZAOstock:** If budget is tight, prioritize WaveWarZ USPTO filing first (most critical), everything else in Q1 2027 after ZAOstock closes.
