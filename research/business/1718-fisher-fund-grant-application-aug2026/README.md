### Section 3: Project Description (500 words or less)

```
ZAOstock is a free, all-ages public music festival taking place October 3, 2026 in Ellsworth, Maine. The festival features live music performances and a demonstration of WaveWarZ — a music technology platform developed by The ZAO where audience members can support artists in real time.

WaveWarZ is unique in Maine's music scene: it's a music battle platform where both the winning and losing artist earn automatically when a battle closes. Audience members stake a small amount of cryptocurrency (Solana) on the artist they believe in. When the battle ends, the software automatically pays both artists — the winning artist earns from the losing pool, and the losing artist earns from the winning pool. No label. No intermediary. No waiting period. Both artists are paid within minutes of the performance ending.

ZAOstock will feature four Maine-connected artists in supporting sets, culminating in a MAIN battle on stage where the audience watches the vote count live on a screen — and sees the artist payout announced in real time. For many attendees, this will be their first exposure to how on-chain music economics work.

The event is intentionally free. ZAO's mission is artist economic equity — not gatekeeping music access by ticket price. We have budgeted for costs including venue, sound, production, and artist compensation without charging admission.

The ZAO is a music DAO (Decentralized Autonomous Organization) that has operated with weekly community governance sessions for over 100 consecutive weeks, with zero governance failures. Our platform has settled 1,245 music battles on Solana, with over $1,800 in direct artist payouts — including artists who lost their battles.

ZAOstock is our first IRL event in Maine. It is a direct extension of the community we have built online: a group of music fans and artists who believe in a model where everyone who shows up gets paid something.

Fisher Fund support would directly offset ZAOstock's production costs, enabling us to maintain free admission and pay artists fairly for their performances. Any grant received would be managed through our Fractured Atlas fiscal sponsorship, which provides full 501(c)(3) accountability.
```
