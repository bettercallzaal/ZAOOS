## Deep Dives

### 1. Committed Coaches - Fitness Mentorship ($4.9M/mo)

The 2025 breakout Whop success. 12-13K members at $4,995 one-time (reported; some sources cite recurring tiers as well). $4.9M/month implies ~$58.8M annualized - the highest known single product on Whop.

The differentiator is human coaches (Payton, Brenda, Karen named publicly) providing personalized emotional + strategic support, not generic fitness content. Members consistently cite the coach relationship as the value driver. There's friction in the app itself (per Trustpilot reviews) but it doesn't matter - the relationship overrides the product UX.

Fitness creators on Whop average $1,170/mo. Committed Coaches outperforms by ~4000x. Pure product-market fit + retention via human attachment. People stay when a real person is invested in their outcome.

### 2. Iman Gadzhi - Monetise + Whop Co-Owner

Built Monetise to 6K+ members and ~$10M+ lifetime via courses + community + creator reward program. April 2025 announced as a Whop **co-owner and strategic investor** (not just creator) - this is unusual and signals Whop's bet on his model.

Creators in his orbit earn up to $2K per video via his reward program. Daily coaching calls, "Mania Competition" (90-day contest with monthly leaderboard + prizes), graduation events. Quarterly cohort launches. Annual NYC bootcamp.

Sticky because (a) co-ownership signals platform validation, (b) members access proprietary tactics from someone who built an 8-figure digital biz, (c) cohort + competition drives daily engagement, (d) other 6-7 figure creators are in the room (network effect).

### 3. Stock Hours (Nour Atta) - Day Trading Signals (~$62M/yr est)

Founded 2018 by Nour Atta (@nourtrades). 26K+ members at $199.99/mo base or $224.99/mo VIP - the largest trading signal community on Whop. At median membership scales to ~$62M annualized.

Stickiness = radical transparency. Members watch real trades execute with brokerage statements shown daily. 3-5 weekly live lessons + pre-market ideas + trade alerts.

Survived early-2025 fraud accusation (alleged copying signals from another Discord). Doubled down on transparent execution and retained members. The lesson: in trading-signal niche, the *ritual* (live sessions, identity, real-time content) matters more than the *commodity* (winning picks).

### 4. Hamza Ahmed - Adonis School ($2M+ ARR)

1.5-2.4K members at ~$247/mo. Hyper-targeted at young men optimizing health, finance, dating, mindset. Built off 2M+ YouTube subscribers funneling into paid community.

Stickiness = identity + results + scarcity. "Adonis Brotherhood" is aspirational, members self-select. Hamza actively manages churn (experiments with "2nd month bonuses") and has publicly pivoted away from degen marketing that diluted core message.

Important note: Adonis School is on Skool, not Whop. Included here because the playbook is Whop-applicable and Hamza is a frequent Whop case study reference. The pattern (tight identity + YT funnel + price discipline) maps directly.

### 5. The Whale Room - Crypto Trading Signals

$159/mo. Member count not public; pricing implies $150K-$500K+ annualized. Daily signals + charting analysis + livestreams.

Stickiness = signal consistency + lab-partner ethos. Publishes win/loss records (transparency = credibility in a category that punishes inflated claims). Crypto traders are alpha-sensitive; signal quality is non-negotiable.

### 6. Korvato - Napoleon AI Trading Software

$20K software entry + ongoing consulting. 30-100+ paying customers estimated -> $600K-$1M+ ARR. Combines traditional trading wisdom with proprietary AI engine built on DeFi principles.

Stickiness = technical moat. The Napoleon AI is proprietary software, not a copyable Discord signal bot. $20K entry filters for serious capital ($100K+ portfolios). Solves implementation problem (traders need algorithms, not just ideas).

### 7. Justin Welsh - Creator MBA

LinkedIn Operating System + Content Operating System + Creator MBA. Total creator business $8-10M. Creator MBA slice on Whop ~$1M+/year.

Stickiness = personal brand moat. Justin is the thought leader for solopreneurs in the creator economy. His "no team, no pivot" positioning resonates with creators tired of scaling playbooks. Members pay because they trust him to "have already figured it out."

### 8. Bravo Six Picks - Sports Betting Picks ($551K/month)

Whop's top sports product per WhopTrends category breakdown. Daily picks Discord + analysis + community. Operated by one creator + 2 mods + AI FAQ bot.

Content cadence: daily Discord drops, 2x daily TikTok clips, daily email pick, weekly YouTube breakdown. Sticky via daily ritual + aggressive community engagement, not unique alpha. Survives in a churn-heavy category by content treadmill discipline.

### 9-10. AI Trading Bots + Custom GPT Creators (2026 wave)

Newer cohort hitting $100K-$500K ARR via $500-$5K/mo subs + $5K-$20K setup fees. Marcus Webb case study: 800 email subs -> $4,200 in first 30 days launching paid AI agent without ads.

Pattern: shifting from B2C "passive income" (selling premade GPTs in Whop's GPT Store) to B2B managed services (building internal AI agents for companies). GPT Store revenue share collapsed in 2026 due to saturation; managed consulting picked up. Stickiness = corporate switching cost once deployed.

## Cross-Cutting Findings

**Revenue concentration:**
- Top 1% earn 57% of platform GMV.
- Only 258 sellers crossed $1M lifetime (June 2025).
- Just 105 products cross $100K/month.
- The 8-12 case studies above represent the elite ~0.05% of 183K+ active sellers.

**Category leaders:**
- Trading dominates (~$20M/month) - more than next 3 categories combined.
- Fitness creators average $1,170/month (~2x trading average per-creator, lower volume).
- Software products average $3,180/month.
- Coaching + courses average $3,110/month.

**Subscription vs one-time:**
- Recurring earns 89% more than one-time on average ($3,982/mo recurring vs $2,105/mo one-time per Whop's data).
- Every top creator above uses recurring as primary.

**Stickiness patterns (across all):**
1. Real-time signal/result delivery (Stock Hours, Whale Room, Korvato, Bravo Six)
2. Personal coach relationships (Committed Coaches)
3. Proprietary methods or tech (Iman's playbook, Napoleon AI, custom GPTs)
4. Community identity / aspirational positioning (Adonis, Justin Welsh)
5. Track record + thought leadership (Iman as co-owner, Justin's $10M empire)

**2026 trend:**
- AI tooling shifting B2C -> B2B managed consulting.
- Music / DJ / production creators are notably underrepresented on Whop vs trading/fitness/business. **Potential blue ocean for ZAO if we ever go music-first on Whop.**
