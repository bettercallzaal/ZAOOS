# 714 - The Arena: ZAO's Best-Use Playbook

> **Goal:** Decide and state the single best way for ZAO to use The Arena app. This is the capstone of the 15-sub-doc Arena deep dive (Doc 708) - no new research, just the decision: given everything we found, exactly how Zaal should use the profile he already has.

## The answer in one paragraph

**Use The Arena as a free option, not an investment.** It is a contracting platform (trading volume down ~85-95% from its mid-2025 peak), so do not pour time or money into it. But it is also free, Zaal already has a profile, and it has one genuinely valuable empty space: there are essentially **no prominent musicians on The Arena**. So the best use is narrow and cheap: keep a live presence by **repurposing content ZAO already makes** (do not create net-new content for Arena), make **one Arena-native bet - music Stages (live audio)** because that is where a music brand actually has an edge, and **set a 90-day kill date** so a dying platform cannot quietly drain effort. Minimal recurring time, one real bet, a clear exit. That is the best way.

## Why this is the best way (the strategy)

Three facts from Doc 708 set the whole strategy:

1. **The Arena is past its peak and shrinking.** Volume off ~85-95%, $ARENA market cap off ~92% from mid-2025. So effort must be small - this is not a platform to build a strategy on.
2. **It is free and Zaal is already on it.** The cost of a minimal presence is near zero. A free option with a small payoff and a capped downside is worth holding.
3. **The music lane is empty.** Across all 15 sub-docs, no prominent musicians were found on The Arena. ZAO is a music brand. Being "the music corner" of the dominant Avalanche SocialFi platform is cheap, ownable positioning even while the platform shrinks.

Put together: the best use is a **lean, music-flavored presence with a hard exit** - capture the cheap positioning, make one real bet where ZAO has an edge, and refuse to let a dying platform pull effort that belongs on Farcaster (where ZAO actually lives).

The mistake to avoid is the opposite of all three: treating The Arena as a growth bet, creating dedicated content for it, or chasing ticket price. That is pouring effort into a contracting venue.

## The setup (one session, ~30 minutes)

Do this once, logged in:

1. **Complete the profile.** Clear photo, a one-line bio that says music + ZAO, and a link to `nexus.thezao.com` (the ecosystem hub). Confirm the X account (@bettercallzaal) is linked.
2. **Read off the numbers.** Ticket price, holder count, follower count. The Arena hides these from logged-out view - this is the only way to know the starting point. Note them; they are the baseline for the 90-day review.
3. **Pin a post** that says who ZAO is in one line and links out.
4. **Grab the referral code.** The referral loop pays 1% of every referred trade, permanently - it is the one piece of Arena economics worth using. Put the referral link where ZAO members will see it.
5. **Suggested bio:** "ZAO - decentralized impact network for music. Building in public. nexus.thezao.com" (adjust to taste; keep it one line).

## The weekly operating rhythm (lean - the floor)

The whole recurring commitment is small and built on repurposing:

- **Cross-post, do not create.** When ZAO publishes a build-in-public update, a ZAO announcement, or a Cipher update on Farcaster/X, drop the same thing on The Arena. No Arena-only content.
- **A few minutes of replies.** Tip and reply in music-adjacent corners. Tips are 100% to the recipient and are the cheapest way to be seen.
- **That is the floor.** If a week is busy, the floor is just the cross-posts. Do not feel obligated to do more on a contracting platform.

Optional, to remove even this load: deploy the **Arena Agent bot** on ZAO's VPS (708l, ~1-2 hours of setup) so the profile auto-posts ZAO content and stays alive without Zaal's daily attention.

## The one real bet: music Stages

This is the single Arena-native move worth real intention.

- **Host an occasional Stage** (Arena's live-audio room) - a ZAO or Cipher listening session, a track premiere, an artist conversation. Monthly is plenty.
- **Why this is the edge:** Stages are live audio, The Arena's content is overwhelmingly memecoin trader chatter, and there is no music presence. A recurring ZAO music Stage is a genuinely differentiated thing on the platform, and it costs only the time of the session itself.
- **Tie it to what ZAO already does.** A Cipher release, a ZAO Fractal recap, a ZAOstock build update - all of these can be a Stage with zero net-new work.

## The team move: Cipher artists (after ~30 days)

Once Zaal's own profile has run about a month and there is something to learn from:

- Bring **2-3 Cipher artists** onto The Arena.
- Have them and Zaal **cross-hold and cross-promote** - this is where the referral loop (1% of referred trades, forever) actually compounds, and where ZAO's 188-member community becomes a real seed holder base.
- Keep it staggered and small. Do not onboard the whole roster into a contracting platform on day one.

## What to ignore and never do

- **Never launch a token through the Arena launchpad.** Regulatory risk and it contradicts the settled position that $ZAO is soulbound Respect, never a tradeable token (Doc 695). Permanent no.
- **Never buy or hold $ARENA.** If Arena ever gives an airdropped governance allocation, accepting it passively is fine; never purchase.
- **Do not chase ticket price.** Ticket price is not a goal. The goal is presence, tips, referrals, and relationships.
- **Do not spend real money** beyond gas-level amounts.
- **Do not let The Arena pull effort from Farcaster.** Farcaster is ZAO's home and far healthier. The Arena is a side-channel, always.

## Success metric and the 90-day kill criteria

Set the exit up front so a contracting platform cannot quietly drain effort.

**What success looks like at 90 days** - at least one of:
- Meaningful tip + referral income (not ticket-price paper gains - actual received value).
- A handful of genuine new relationships or ZAO-relevant connections made through The Arena.
- A music Stage that drew a real, engaged audience.

**Kill criteria** - if at the 90-day review none of the above is true, **archive the profile and stop.** Leaving a dead profile up is fine; spending more time is not. Write the decision down either way.
