> **Goal:** One entry point for everything ZAO researched about The Arena (arena.social). This brief consolidates the 15-sub-doc deep dive (Doc 708) and the best-use playbook (Doc 714) into a single read: the bottom line, every decision, an index of all 17 Arena documents, and the playbook in brief. ## The bottom line (read this first)

Across 17 documents and 15 research agents over three waves, the picture is consistent:

**The Arena is a real platform, past its peak, in a fragile category - and ZAO should use it as a free option, not an investment.**

The Arena is the dominant SocialFi platform on Avalanche. It is a genuine product (a social feed, tradeable creator "tickets", its own DEX, a token launchpad, live-audio Stages, token-gated Groups, an iOS app) that survived a near-fatal start - it launched as Stars Arena in September 2023, was hacked for ~$2.88M eight days in, recovered, rebranded, and rebuilt under a new team. It has ~200K registered users.

But two facts decide the strategy: it is **contracting** (trading volume down ~85-95%, the $ARENA token down ~92% from the mid-2025 peak), and SocialFi as a category is a graveyard (friend.tech, the model it copied, collapsed). The Arena is the biggest fish in a shrinking pond.

The one genuinely valuable opening: across every wave of research, **no prominent musicians were found on The Arena.** ZAO is a music brand. The music lane is empty.

So the best use is narrow and cheap. Zaal already has a profile. Keep a lean presence by repurposing content ZAO already makes, make one Arena-native bet (music Stages, where a music brand has a real edge), bring 2-3 Cipher artists on after 30 days, and set a hard 90-day kill review so a dying platform cannot drain effort. **Minimal recurring time, one real bet, a clear exit.** Never launch a token via the launchpad, never hold $ARENA, never let it pull effort from Farcaster (ZAO's actual home).

## The best-use playbook in brief (full version: Doc 714)

**Setup (one session, ~30 min):** complete the profile, log in and log the baseline numbers (ticket price, holders, followers - they are hidden from logged-out view), pin a post, grab the referral code (1% of referred trades, forever).

**Weekly rhythm (the floor):** cross-post content ZAO already publishes - do not create Arena-only content. A few minutes of tips and replies. Optionally hand the posting to the Arena Agent bot on ZAO's VPS.

**The one bet:** host an occasional music Stage (a Cipher listening session, a release premiere) - the differentiated, Arena-native move where a music brand actually has an edge.

**The team move:** after ~30 days, bring 2-3 Cipher artists on; cross-promote so the referral loop compounds; ZAO's 188 members are the seed holder base.

**The exit:** a hard 90-day review. If it is not producing real tips/referrals or real relationships, archive the profile and stop. The exit is set up front so a contracting platform cannot quietly drain effort.

## Do / Do not

**Do:** complete the profile; repurpose ZAO content onto it; host music Stages; bring Cipher artists on after 30 days; use the referral code; run the 90-day review.

**Do not:** launch a token via the launchpad (regulatory risk + contradicts soulbound $ZAO, Doc 695); buy or hold $ARENA; chase ticket price; spend real money; let The Arena pull effort from Farcaster.
