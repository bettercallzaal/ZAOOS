## The framework (3 concepts, from the essay)

The piece (Jamie / Sound of Fractures + Maarten Walraven, MUSIC x, 2026-06-04) builds on Nadia Asparouhova's **Antimemetics** (real 2025 book: some ideas structurally resist viral transmission because the dominant infrastructure is built for virality and can't carry them). Applied to audiences:

1. **Dissolved fandom** (a process) - the point where a person's love of music stops being *platform-legible*. They get older/busier, stop performing taste publicly, move recommendations to private channels (a WhatsApp link to one friend). They still buy vinyl + tickets, but stop generating continuous signal. Metrics read this as "engagement declined." Reality: engagement changed shape.
2. **Ambient fandom** (the condition that follows) - after Brian Eno's "ambient = works whether actively attended to or blended into environment." These people are often MORE knowledgeable, MORE willing to spend, and better at bringing others into genuine contact with music than the romanticized superfan - but their fandom is atmosphere, not signal. They don't live in Discord/WhatsApp fan communities; their "group" is just their friends. They don't cluster, so the industry doesn't know where to look.
3. **Off-protocol behaviour** (the mechanism) - the industry protocol = release to streaming -> data -> algo recs -> discovery + TikTok/IG signals -> playlist/press -> tickets/merch. Every stage is instrumented. What it CANNOT read: a band at a wedding converting guests to lifelong listeners; a track in a Signal group that sells 4 vinyl over a month via dinner-table word of mouth. The protocol optimizes for **reach**; off-protocol is where **depth** happens.

Plus the **dark forest vs ambient fan** distinction: Maarten's prior MUSIC-x argument was "artists should build their own dark forests" (deep private communities). The essay's refinement: most people aren't in anyone's dark forest and have no time to lean into a scene - they "experience life, with music woven through it." ZAO note: ZAO's community model is a dark-forest play; the bigger opportunity per this essay is serving the ambient fan who will never join a Discord.

## Findings

- **This is a naming exercise, not a solution** - the authors are explicit: the vocabulary is "the step that has to come before solutions." That is exactly where ZAO can move first: build the thing the framework implies before the majors do.
- **It pushes against the industry's superfan obsession** - MIDiA's own analysts corroborate the gap: Tatiana Cirisano's "All eyes, no ears: why virality is not building fandom," and MIDiA's "more is more could backfire" (Q1 2025 survey: only ~25% engage with their favourite media multiple times/day; ~30% of Charli XCX superfans listen daily - but the same ~30% listen to Drake + Billie daily too, i.e. even superfan attention is fragmented). The industry is doubling down on measuring + monetizing the visible superfan while the essay says the value sits with the invisible ambient fan.
- **Contradiction worth flagging:** MIDiA is building a *data-driven superfan definition* ("you cannot monetise what you cannot measure"); the essay argues the most valuable audience is precisely the one you *cannot* measure with platform data. ZAO sits on the essay's side - but ZAO's edge is that contribution credits (ZOLs), IRL events, and on-chain Respect can make off-protocol activity measurable WITHOUT the streaming protocol. That is the synthesis: not "unmeasurable," but "measurable by a different instrument."
- **Direct ZAO artifacts referenced-adjacent:** the essay's transmission examples (wedding, Signal group, dinner) are the ZAOstock / house-concert / Telegram-drop motions ZAO already runs.
