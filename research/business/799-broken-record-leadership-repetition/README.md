## The principle (Tim Martinez, "A Broken Record", 2026-06-04)

Tim "The Inside Man" Martinez (business/leadership Substack, 74 likes on this post) argues: **if something is truly important, it needs to be repeated** - and he demonstrates by repeating that sentence three times in the post. Telling someone once and being disappointed they didn't internalize it is "a losing formula." Be a broken record about the things that matter most to the business; that's OK. The diagnostic question: *if you asked anyone on your team what you repeat constantly, would they say it's something core to the ethos of the business?* "We're all broken records about something - is it the right thing?" He closes with a CTA to run the test this week. (Adjacent data point in the post: a poll where 82% of his readers said they're actively modernizing their business.)

## Findings (the principle is backed by management research)

The "broken record" instinct is the popularized **Rule of 7 / 7x7 rule**:

- **People need to hear a message ~7 times before it sticks** (rooted in marketing's "7 exposures before a purchase" + George Miller's chunking research). In change leadership, make it ~10. (Megan Reid; Josh Holtzman; SmartBrief.)
- **"Leaders get tired of repeating the message long before employees get tired of hearing it"** - the trap is assuming repetition is overkill when it's the requirement. "If you're not blue in the face from repeating it, your people probably haven't heard it enough." (Holtzman.)
- **Deliver it 7 times through 7 different channels** - all-hands, 1:1s, email, departmental meetings, visible reminders - and reinforce with fresh stories so it doesn't go stale. (SmartBrief's 7x7 rule; Productive Flourishing's multi-mode point.)
- **VeriFone CEO Hatim Tyabji's Irish Setter poster** (11 blocks the dog ignores "sit," block 12 it sits, "Good dog"): "Leaders must be clear, consistent and repetitive. Keep repeating the message until it sticks."
- **EOS / Lencioni "overcommunicate organizational clarity":** you know your core values have taken hold when anyone in the org can recite them and explain how they're lived. Then you're "just getting started" - new people arrive, old ones forget.

Contradiction worth noting: pure literal repetition ("like a broken record") risks tuning the team out. The synthesis the sources agree on - repeat the *message* relentlessly, vary the *delivery* (angle, story, channel). Tim's "broken record" is the hook; "7 ways, 7 times" is the method.
