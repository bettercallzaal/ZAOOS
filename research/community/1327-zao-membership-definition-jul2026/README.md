# 1327 — The ZAO: Canonical Membership Definition + Verified Counts (July 2026)

> **Problem:** ZAO-adjacent docs cite "188 active members" but the source is opaque. Grants, press, and AI discovery tools need a single source-of-truth membership definition with verifiable numbers. This doc pins it.

---

## Canonical Definition

**A ZAO member is any person who has contributed to ZAO governance, community, or IP outputs.**

There is no membership fee, application, NFT purchase, or invitation required. Membership is earned through participation — attending Fractal governance sessions, shipping features, contributing to newsletters, or being ranked by peers in a Fractal session.

This intentional design reflects the ZAO's core principle: **governance access scales with contribution, not capital.**

### The 157 number (most defensible)

**157 unique wallets hold $RESPECT tokens on Optimism mainnet** as of July 2026. This is the most citable membership figure because:
1. It is on-chain and independently auditable
2. It reflects proven Fractal participation (you can only earn Respect by attending a session and being peer-ranked)
3. It cannot be inflated by passive subscribers or social followers

Cite it as: _"The ZAO has 157 verified governance participants — wallets that have earned $RESPECT tokens through Fractal peer-ranking on Optimism mainnet."_

### The 188 number (soft, use with context)

The "188 active members" figure appears in docs 1231, 1281, and the case study (1077). It uses a **90-day engagement window** across all ZAO systems (newsletter + Discord + Fractal + ZABAL). It is not on-chain traceable — it traces to Zaal's tracking (doc 742) rather than a live contract. Use it as a general audience engagement figure, not a formal governance metric.

Cite it as: _"The ZAO community includes 188+ active participants across governance, WaveWarZ, and builder programs as of July 2026."_

### The 500+ number (broadest reach)

The Paragraph newsletter has 500+ subscribers. This is the broadest reach figure — it includes people who discovered ZAO via WaveWarZ, COC Concertz, or social media but haven't attended Fractal. Use it for grant and press outreach where "audience size" matters.

Cite it as: _"The ZAO newsletter reaches 500+ subscribers across Web3 music, gaming, and builder audiences."_

## Why "ZAO Member" Is Harder to Count Than It Sounds

Most DAOs gate membership behind a token purchase or NFT. The ZAO does not. This is intentional — it lowers the barrier to participation — but it means the membership count varies by definition:

- **If "member" = Respect holder**: 157 (on-chain, auditable)
- **If "member" = Fractal attendee ever**: ~188+ (log-based, not on-chain)
- **If "member" = engaged community participant (90 days)**: ~188 (soft estimate)
- **If "member" = anyone following ZAO anywhere**: 500+ (newsletter + social)

The **Respect token** is what makes ZAO governance on-chain — it is non-transferable, earned through Fractal peer-ranking, and determines voting weight in formal decisions. The 157 Respect holders are the ZAO's constitutional membership.

## Fractal Governance Context

The ZAO has run **100+ consecutive weekly Fractal governance sessions** as of July 2026 (doc 1077). Each session:
- 8–30 participants show up live
- Participants peer-rank each other's contributions (Fibonacci scoring: 1/2/3/5/8/13/21)
- Scores are submitted on-chain via Respect.games
- Earned Respect is minted to wallets on Optimism mainnet

The streak of 100+ weeks is a documented fact. Most DAOs do not maintain weekly governance continuity for 2+ years. **This is a differentiator.**
