## Issue Structure (Consistent Format)

Every issue follows the same 5-section structure. ZOE fills sections 1-4; Zaal fills section 5:

### Section 1: The Number (1 stat)

One WaveWarZ or governance stat that changed since last issue. Pull from `/stats`.

Example: "Since our last issue: **78 new battles** were fought on WaveWarZ. The losers earned a combined **2.1 SOL** ($420 at current price). That's $420 that would have been zero if WaveWarZ didn't exist."

### Section 2: The Event (What just happened or is coming)

One event update — recap of the last thing that happened OR preview of the next big thing.

Example (Issue 2): "ZAOstock Lineup is Here — 200 tickets, Oct 3, Baltimore. Here's who's playing…"

### Section 3: The Community (One person or project)

Spotlight on one ZAO community member: ZABAL participant, WaveWarZ artist, governance contributor. ZOE drafts from public data; Zaal confirms.

### Section 4: The Governance (What happened in the DAO)

One governance session highlight: who ranked highly, what proposals passed, what came up for discussion. Pull from ZAOOS governance session docs.

### Section 5: From Zaal (Personal)

1-2 paragraphs. Zaal's voice, current state of mind, something real that happened this week. This is NOT drafted by ZOE — Zaal writes this in 5-10 minutes. It's the human element that makes the newsletter worth reading.

## Content Principles

1. **One stat, one event, one person, one governance note, one Zaal voice.** Never more.
2. **No jargon without a one-line definition.** "OREC (the ZAO's on-chain voting contract)" not just "OREC."
3. **Honest about where we are.** If only 15 tickets sold by Issue 2, say 15, then explain what the plan is. Readers trust honesty.
4. **Every issue has one call to action.** Ticket link, ZABAL signup, WaveWarZ trial, governance invite. One, not six.
5. **ZOE drafts; Zaal authenticates.** The personal voice section is non-negotiable — it's the thing that makes a 100-person newsletter feel personal.
