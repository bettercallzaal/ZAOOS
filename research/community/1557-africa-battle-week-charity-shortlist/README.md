# 1557 — Africa Battle Week: Charity Shortlist Research (Jul 2026)

> **Context:** Africa Battle Week (doc 1498) is ZAO's Sep 26 community WaveWarZ battle — US veteran vs. West African artist — where 100% of the SOL payout goes to a charity voted on by ZOR holders. Nominations open Jul 20, vote Jul 24–25. This doc pre-populates the shortlist so ZOE has 3–5 options ready on Jul 24 morning even if community nominations are sparse.

---

## Selection Criteria (from doc 1498)

| Criterion | Must-Have |
|-----------|-----------|
| Active in music, arts, or youth education in Africa or the African diaspora | ✓ |
| Publicly verifiable (website, social presence) | ✓ |
| Can receive crypto (wallet/ENS) or PayPal | ✓ |
| Onchain presence | Nice-to-have |
| Prior ZAO/WaveWarZ connection | Nice-to-have |

**ZAO's preferred transfer method:** USDC or SOL to a public wallet. PayPal is the fallback for orgs without onchain presence.

---

## Shortlist: 5 Candidate Charities

### Option 1 — Music In Africa Foundation (MIAF)

**What:** Pan-African music industry development organization. Founded 2013, based in Johannesburg. Supports African musicians with training, tools, grants, and global visibility.

**Why it fits:** Music-specific, pan-continental (not one country), youth-empowerment focus. Runs the ACCES (African Creative Economy for the Sustainable Development Goals) program and African Music Development Programme.

**Online presence:** musicinafrica.net — active website with projects, news, annual conference.

**Receiving crypto:** No confirmed crypto wallet found. PayPal-capable (receives international grants). ZAO would send PayPal or USD bank wire.

**Onchain:** Not verified as of research date.

**ZAO fit:** High — directly aligned with music artists and ecosystem building. Well-known in African music circles (would resonate with a West African artist collaborator).

---

### Option 2 — Afrobeats to the World / Motherlan

**What:** Motherlan is a US-based diaspora organization that promotes African music, arts, and cultural exchange — concerts, artist tours, youth workshops. Afrobeats-adjacent audience, reaches both US and Africa.

**Why it fits:** Bridges the US↔Africa dynamic that Africa Battle Week embodies. Strong social media presence, diaspora audience, youth focus.

**Online presence:** Active Instagram and TikTok presence. Verifiable.

**Receiving crypto:** Depends on the specific org. Diaspora nonprofits in this space have increasingly added Coinbase Commerce or Gitcoin as options. Research needed before committing.

**ZAO fit:** Medium-High — the US↔Africa framing is perfect thematically. Familiarity in the ZAO community depends on whether any member has a prior connection.

---

### Option 3 — Fela Kuti Foundation / New Afrika Shrine

**What:** Foundation preserving the legacy of Fela Kuti, founded by his son Femi Kuti. Runs the New Afrika Shrine in Lagos — a live music venue and cultural center that serves as a free community space for Lagos youth.

**Why it fits:** Legendary connection to African music history. Lagos-based (West Africa). Free community concerts, arts education, youth engagement. Aligns with ZAO's values around accessible music culture.

**Online presence:** felakutimuseum.com + Femi Kuti's verified socials. Highly verifiable.

**Receiving crypto:** Not confirmed. PayPal or USD wire likely required. Femi Kuti has a public Ethereum address from prior NFT releases — worth verifying if active.

**ZAO fit:** High cultural resonance. A ZAO battle payout going to the Fela Kuti Foundation would make for a compelling Farcaster story ("WaveWarZ battle payout goes to the birthplace of Afrobeat").

---

### Option 4 — Diaspora Arts Connection (DAC) / similar US-based organizations

**What:** Organizations in the US specifically supporting African diaspora artists — artist residencies, grants, showcases. Multiple organizations fit this profile (DAC, African Artists Foundation, etc.).

**Why it fits:** The "African diaspora" framing from doc 1498 criteria. US-based = easier to verify, more likely to have Venmo/PayPal/bank account. Good for ZOR voters who may be US-based and familiar with diaspora orgs.

**Crypto:** More likely to accept crypto than Africa-based nonprofits. Some diaspora arts orgs have Gitcoin grants profiles.

**ZAO fit:** Medium — thematically correct but less "on the ground in Africa" than Options 1-3. Better fit if ZOR voters want to keep the donation in the diaspora community.

---

### Option 5 — Community Nomination (ZOR Holder Chosen)

**What:** Intentional placeholder for a community-sourced nomination. Keep one of the 3 shortlist slots open for the highest-upvoted community nomination.

**Why it fits:** Governance legitimacy — the community should own the process, not have it pre-decided. If a ZOR holder nominates an org with personal connection (West African artist's home community foundation, etc.), that nomination carries weight.

**ZAO fit:** Always high — the community choosing their own nominee is the point of the vote.
