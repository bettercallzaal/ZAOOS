## Origin Manifesto (from impactfulgiving.org, quoted verbatim)

> Most nonprofits don't have a volunteer problem - they have a consistency challenge. People start strong, then life gets busy, and even the best intentions fade.
>
> I've spent years researching one question: what actually keeps volunteers engaged. The research kept pointing to one insight: people don't need more time - they need a better way to show up.
>
> And that way is Contribution Circles.
>
> Small groups of four to six people - where everyone feels recognized and leaves energized.
>
> New and experienced volunteers come together for just eight weeks. One hour a week. Online. You share what you've been working on or thinking about, support each other, and actually feel glad you showed up.
>
> Eight sessions is ideal for building momentum - but if you miss a week, you're still part of the group. Every bit counts.
>
> After eight weeks, volunteers can continue if they want - but those eight weeks alone are a meaningful contribution.
>
> And nonprofits gain a flexible system they can use however it fits best - weekly, monthly, or when needed - without adding cost or work to their team.
>
> If you lead or support a nonprofit and want more consistent, engaged volunteers - or you've been meaning to give back but haven't found something that truly fits your life - let's talk. Visit ImpactfulGiving.org to learn more.
>
> People don't need more time to make an impact - they need a better way to show up.

## Why This Fits ZAO

The ZAO's participation pattern mirrors the nonprofit consistency problem:
- Members show up big for events (ZAO-CHELLA, ZAOstock, WaveWarZ battles)
- Then drift between events
- Fractal (weekly Monday 6pm) is the closest analog to a Contribution Circle, but it's 25+ people and optimized for Respect consensus, not intimate small-group support

Contribution Circles fill a different slot: **4-6 person pods, 8-week commitment, 1 hr/week, peer accountability + celebration**. Lower stakes than Fractal. Higher intimacy than #general. Perfect for artists working on specific projects (album, performance prep, brand build) and for partner nonprofits who want a steady volunteer cadence without building infrastructure.
