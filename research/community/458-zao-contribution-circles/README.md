## Origin Manifesto (Zaal's voice, pre-meeting framing)

> Most nonprofits don't have a volunteer problem - they have a consistency challenge. People start strong, then life gets busy, and even the best intentions fade. Research kept pointing to one insight: people don't need more time - they need a better way to show up. And that way is Contribution Circles.
>
> Small groups of four to six people - where everyone feels recognized and leaves energized. New and experienced volunteers come together for just eight weeks. One hour a week. Online.
>
> Eight sessions is ideal for building momentum - but if you miss a week, you're still part of the group. Every bit counts. After eight weeks, volunteers can continue if they want - but those eight weeks alone are a meaningful contribution.
>
> Nonprofits gain a flexible system they can use however it fits best - weekly, monthly, or when needed - without adding cost or work to their team. People don't need more time to make an impact - they need a better way to show up.

## Eric's Framework Mechanics (verbatim from meeting)

### Recruitment Script (what converts)
Eric's logical journey that has landed two groups in the last week:
1. "Most nonprofits don't have a volunteer problem - they have a consistency challenge."
2. "People start strong, life gets busy, and the best of intentions fade."
3. "Research shows consistency is not about time. People don't need more time - they need a better way to show up."
4. "That better way is Contribution Circles - 4 to 6 people, 1 hour a week, online, for 8 weeks."
5. "Miss a week, you're still part of the team. Every bit makes a difference."
6. "After 8 weeks, you can continue. But 8 weeks alone is a meaningful contribution."
7. "You (the nonprofit) are left with a simple, repeatable, easy system to customize however fits - weekly, monthly, quarterly."

### Session Format
- 4-6 people per group, randomly assigned by Eric's app
- 1 hour per session, 8 sessions (weeks) per cohort
- Each person gets 3 minutes to share thoughts / activities / ideas
- Group votes / levels each share 1-6 (was "level six" / "level one" but rebranded because "six worse or should I be level one confused the crap out of everybody" - Eric now uses named levels instead of numbered)
- Everybody at the same level across all groups gets the same points (Eric's research finding - "worked out better")
- Core session runs in about 30 min; points phase intentionally fast

### Platform (current state)
- Zoom breakout rooms (app auto-assigns to Zoom Room 1 / 2 / 3 etc)
- App handles everything: volunteer IDs, informed consent, breakout assignment, point collection, motivation test, email for point delivery
- Eric considers moving video conference in-app in v3 but not there yet

### Research Compliance (current binding constraint)
- Eric is a grad student with university IRB oversight
- During the 8-week research window:
  - Every participant must sign an **informed consent form** (2 minutes, online)
  - Anonymous **volunteer IDs** issued; email is optional and separate from data
  - **Pre and post motivation test** (online, "tells you a lot about yourself")
  - Zoom required for breakout consistency (or whatever platform supports his data pipe reliably)
- After 8 weeks: the research constraints lift, ZAO owns the flow, can migrate to Discord, swap the software, run it however.

### Mission Framing per Session
Eric's weekly template for each host:
1. Here's the mission (30 seconds, clear, reiterate every week)
2. Here's the big things our circle has come up with so far (events, newsletter, book release, potluck, etc - show momentum)
3. Now we run the session (shares + points)

Example outcomes from Eric's last cohort: members organized an event, started a newsletter, co-wrote a book, planned a potluck. Nobody assigned tasks - members just said "I can help distribute that" once they saw what was forming.

### Eric's Post-Cohort Hub
He is building a "nonprofit hub" that graduates get 3 free months to - a shared community for nonprofit leaders. Career Catalyst Collaborative is the next cohort (students + schools + businesses - "students graduate getting paid for their passion"). This is the path from an 8-week circle into ongoing support.

## Sam's Contribution to the Discussion

Sam surfaced a product angle: adapting **$1 NFT projects for nonprofits** as a Web3 on-ramp / fundraising tool. Referenced **Poly Raiders** — a nonprofit ZAO has done benefit battles with that sells $50 NFTs to fund sanitary pads for girls in Nigeria.

Value framing Sam articulated:
- The amount of money that reaches a charity via peer-to-peer NFT is massively higher than via traditional charity infrastructure after overhead
- Class-sized projects (100 NFTs, not 10,000) teach metadata, ownership, wallet-creation without overwhelming
- "Don't call them NFTs. Call them digital assets."

Eric's response: "You're burying the lead. The lead is: if you want to be a well-funded nonprofit today and tomorrow, this is where funding is going. Fear-of-loss framing, not feature framing." That's the pitch ZAO can take to partner nonprofits after the 8-week pilot proves the circle model.

---

## Why Eric Thinks Nonprofits Need This

From meeting: "86% of nonprofit founders have huge levels of imposter syndrome." Most founders know the cause (health, education, elder care, cancer). They do NOT know funding, workforce development, IT. They bluff in front of boards and feel horrible about it. The circle format provides peer support without exposing the skills gap publicly.

ZAO is different - we are a web3-native, tech-confident group. But we have the same consistency problem, just for different reasons. The circle format is still the right tool.
