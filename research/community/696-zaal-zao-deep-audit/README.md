# 696 - Zaal Panthaki + The ZAO: Deep Audit

> **Goal:** The full, synthesized audit of Zaal Panthaki and everything he has built - person, history, ecosystem, people, tech, money, the graveyard, his voice, the market around him, and an honest strategic read. Built from a 22-agent, three-wave audit on 2026-05-20/21. This is the deep reference that doc 695 (the ZABAL Games context prompt) draws from, and a standing strategic profile in its own right.

## Headline

Zaal Panthaki is a solo founder running a real artist-economy ecosystem on extraordinary velocity. In roughly three years The ZAO went from a talent-agency idea to a 188-member network on Base with a live incubator, weekly onchain governance, three IRL events behind it, a flagship festival ahead, a 5-bot agent stack, and a ~790-doc research library. The model is proven small (WaveWarZ) and the vision is clear (primitives for any digital creator). The risk is not the idea or the execution speed - it is that one person is in every loop, the velocity leaves graveyards and half-finished surfaces, and the operation has sprawled faster than it has been consolidated. The single highest-leverage move is to honor the cleanup and graduation discipline already written down.

## 1. The person

Zaal Panthaki builds publicly as **BetterCallZaal** (`@zaal` on Farcaster, `@bettercallzaal` on X and YouTube, plus Twitch build streams). He came from engineering - an automation/robotics engineering career (PCC Structurals) before web3 - and discovered blockchain during the 2022-23 bear market as the tooling that moves ownership, not just information.

The origin that explains everything: in college (2018-19) Zaal was close to an independent musician known as **JANGOUU FOREVER**. Watching a genuinely talented independent artist struggle to capture the value of their own work set him on building systems that return the profit margin, the data, and the IP rights to creators. The ZAO is that idea, carried forward.

How he builds (from an 84-repo survey, doc 649): **velocity plus ruthless abandonment** (ship constantly, kill in 1-3 months when the pain surfaces); **build for hand-off, not control**; **monorepo as lab** (prototype in one repo, graduate to own repo/domain); **funnel-first** (every surface wired for distribution); **build in public** (daily newsletter, podcast, livestreamed sessions). His schedule is disciplined - early wake, focused DND build blocks - and he prefers long async research documents over meetings.

## 3. What The ZAO is

The canonical pitch: **a decentralized impact network focused on bringing the profit margin, data, and IP rights back to artists, using emerging technology like blockchain and AI.** Not a "music community" - broader, and the technology is the means. Internal priority order: **music first, community second, technology third.** Web3 is invisible infrastructure.

It is an **incubator**: a member pitches or builds, The ZAO incubates, Zaal plus a community cofounder back it with shared infrastructure. WaveWarZ was the first and is the proof. The product is organized in **four pillars** - Artist Org (social + music), Autonomous Org (governance + agents), Operating System (tools), Open Source (research + a forkable template). The 12-month vision is ecosystem primitives that let **any digital creator** scale a brand, not musicians only.

## 4. How it governs itself

The ZAO runs **fractal governance** - a weekly peer-evaluation session, 90-plus weeks running, Mondays 6pm EST in Discord, facilitated by Dan and Tadas. Members split into small breakout groups, each shares their week's contribution, the group ranks them, and higher ranks earn **Respect** on a Fibonacci curve (13/8/5/3/2/1 in a group of six). Respect is earned, never bought, and non-transferable. Two Respect contracts exist (OG, ERC-20; ZOR, ERC-1155) on Optimism, executed through the ORDAO/OREC contract set. The lineage runs from the Optimism Fractal and Eden Fractal experiments. Contribution is measured by peers, in public, every week - that is the heart of how The ZAO decides.

## 5. The ecosystem

Brand architecture, top to bottom: **BCZ Strategies LLC** (the Delaware legal entity) -> **ZABAL** (Zaal's umbrella brand + the `$ZABAL` token on Base) -> **The ZAO** (the impact network) -> graduated and incubated projects. A "ZABAL project" is Zaal solo; a "ZAO project" is incubated with community cofounders; graduates leave the lab for their own repo and domain.

The portfolio includes WaveWarZ (music battles, incubated), ZAO Festivals + ZAOstock (events), COC Concertz (metaverse concerts, graduated), ZAO Music (an artist-support collective, not a label), Zlank (a no-code Farcaster mini-app builder), the agent stack, FISHBOWLZ (audio rooms, paused), Empire Builder and POIDH (distribution and bounty rails). Live web properties: zaoos.com, zaostock.com, thezao.com, zaofestivals.com, wavewarz.com, cocconcertz.com, bczyapz.com, zlank.online, and bettercallzaal.com with /nexus, /kit, /zabalgames.

## 7. Tech + agent stack

ZAOOS is the product and lab: Next.js 16, React 19, Supabase (RLS), Neynar, XMTP, Wagmi/Viem, Tailwind v4. It began as a gated Farcaster social client and grew into a monorepo lab. The agent stack runs on one shared pattern (Hermes): **ZOE** (the concierge), **Hermes** (the locked automation framework, fix-to-PR pipeline), **ZAOcoworkingBot** (team tracker), **Bonfire** (the knowledge graph the bots read and write). The framework was locked 2026-05-05 after 18 months of experiments; doc 684 specs the consolidated bot-fleet architecture.

## 8. The economy + how it is funded

**BCZ Strategies LLC** is the legal and agency hub. Zaal's primary financial pillar is his day job - transitioning from Jackson Labs to Riverside Group full-time June 2026 (Cameron is the contact). The agency model (ZAO community as workforce) is architecturally sound but operationally dormant - no material confirmed revenue yet. **ZAO Music** is pre-revenue (first release, the Cipher, targeted for summer 2026; BMI + DistroKid + 0xSplits). **ZAOstock** is pre-funded - a $7K-25K budget, sponsorships not yet closed, Fractured Atlas providing fiscal sponsorship. The **ZAO Fund** on Artizen lets the community allocate capital, not Zaal. The honest read: this is a founder with a day job funding a build-in-public ecosystem, bootstrapped, no VC, runway tied to employment. The profitability bets (ZAOstock, ZAO Music, the agency) are real but unproven.

## 9. The graveyard

Velocity has a cost, and the graveyard is where you read it. Killed or deferred, mostly in one disciplined sweep around 2026-05-04/05: the **openclaw** container and its 7-agent squad, the **Composio AO** orchestrator, the **Agent Zero** migration, the **10-bot branded fleet** (never built), and a long lineage of agent-framework experiments (eliza forks, custom ElizaOS wrappers, nine monthly fractal bots, newsletter bots, a Recoupable fork). **FISHBOWLZ** was paused when a partner (Juke) solved the same problem better. The meta-pattern is genuine decision discipline: Zaal builds a thing, sees the complexity cost, benchmarks alternatives, and locks one choice in a single reasoned session (doc 601 is the template). The cost is real too - 18 months of framework churn produced one keeper (Hermes), and the abandonment habit leaves half-finished surfaces behind that someone has to clean up.

## 10. Zaal's voice

Spartan, concrete, direct. He leads with the specific named thing ("Kenny from POIDH at 2pm", not "today's recording"). No aphorisms, no hype, no "disrupting", no metaphor-as-brand ("the machine", "the rooms"). Sentences vary in length; closes land on something concrete about now or next, never a universal truth. Recurring themes: friction and systemizing it, autonomy, quiet work, supporting artists, building in public, **coordination over ownership** (he builds systems other people own). Phrases he reaches for: "music first, community second, technology third", "show, don't explain" Web3, "provide infrastructure, not ownership". No emojis, no em dashes. To write as Zaal: be concrete, name real things, skip the inspirational close.

## 11. The competitive landscape

Web3 music in 2026 is a field with a visible graveyard. **Sound.xyz** entered maintenance mode and pivoted (Vault.fm); **Catalog** is effectively dead - the NFT-drop-only model did not sustain. Still alive: **Audius** (decentralized streaming), **Zora** (now a social layer with Attention Markets), **Royal** (fractional royalty NFTs, a16z-backed), and **Coop Records** (a $10M artist-as-startup fund - the closest reference comp to The ZAO's incubator thesis). Market sentiment is cautious realism: hybrid models survive, NFT-only models died, VC-subsidized zero-commission is unsustainable. The ZAO's real differentiators are the incubator model, IRL festivals, the long-running operational fractal governance, and being Farcaster-native rather than a bolted-on marketplace. Its exposures: single-protocol dependence on Farcaster, an unproven monetization path at scale, the broader web3 market timing, and regulatory ambiguity around creator-coin economics. (Sources: Water and Music sentiment scan, Zora and Audius 2026 updates, Coop Records and Royal coverage - see Sources.)

## 12. The research-library fingerprint

The ~790-doc research library is itself a fingerprint of how Zaal thinks. The two largest topic folders by far are dev-workflows (~122) and agents (~119) - together ~30% of the library. He invests far more in **how to build** (engineering leverage, agent autonomy, orchestration) than in any single feature. The arc is visible across the number range: early docs explore possibilities (agent memory, sub-agents, platform redesigns); recent docs (660s-690s) are audits, integration, and operational hardening - "we have the pieces, now make them work together". He writes patterns, not one-offs, and reuses docs as a compounding knowledge graph. The tell: as of mid-2026 there are no new products and no new tokens in the recent docs - just consolidation.

## 13. Honest assessment

**Strengths.** Proven velocity and clean iteration. The incubator model is validated by WaveWarZ. A genuinely rare, actively-maintained ~790-doc institutional memory. A real 188-member community with 90-plus weeks of live governance. The agent framework finally locked after 18 months of churn. A tight, non-hype vision.

**Weaknesses.** Solo-founder dependency at the operational level - Zaal is in every critical loop, and recurring corrections (branch discipline, PR state, uncommitted code) show mistakes compounding under load. Ruthless abandonment leaves graveyards and half-finished surfaces (dead code still live, graduations past their deadline). Operational sprawl - multiple bots, two VPSes, ~58 doc-number collisions, stale headline numbers, a real security-dependency backlog. ZAOstock's graduation has stalled past its date.

**Opportunities.** Two finished but unlicensed open-source tools could open the "any creator" audience cheaply. ZAOstock on October 3 is the proof point that turns the infrastructure into a sellable model. ZABAL Games is a clean on-ramp for non-ZAO builders. The partner network (SongJam, Bonfires, failoften, Juke) is real and underused.

**Threats.** Founder burnout from being spread across a day-job transition, the codebase, the bots, festival production, and consulting. Web3-market headwinds and creator-coin regulatory ambiguity. Key-person risk on incubated projects. The research library tipping from asset to bottleneck. No written answer yet for how governance scales past a few hundred members.

## 14. The one thing to fix first

**Honor the cleanup and graduation discipline already written down.** Kill the zombie surfaces (dead code from paused projects), move graduating code out of the lab, set a real ZAOstock spinout date, and patch the stale headline numbers. It is two to three days of unglamorous work. It is the highest-leverage move because the audits keep finding that operational sprawl compounds faster than velocity resolves it - and because doing it is the proof that "graduate or delete" is a real rule, not a pattern admired in retrospect. Velocity is not the problem. The follow-through after the velocity is.
