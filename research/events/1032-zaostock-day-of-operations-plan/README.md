# 1032 — ZAOstock Day-of Operations Plan (Site Logistics, Not the Stage Program)

> **Goal:** Doc 428 already locks WHAT happens on stage, minute by minute. This doc covers everything else on Oct 3 - load-in, volunteer check-in, safety, signage, parking, load-out - none of which existed as a written plan before this doc.

## Key Decisions

| Recommendation | Why |
|---|---|
| **Load-in starts at 6:00am, doors at 12:00pm, load-out complete by 10:30pm** | This is a real, sequenced timeline (site prep → stage → sound → vendors → volunteer check-in), not a guess - see Finding 1. Six hours of load-in for a 6-hour show is normal for this scale |
| **Staff the day in three distinct crews, not one continuous team** | Load-in crew (10-12 people, 6am-12pm), event-run crew (8-12 people, 12pm-6pm), load-out crew (12-18 people, 6pm-10:30pm). Total headcount overlaps are fine - most people work more than one shift - but planning by crew, not by person, makes the ~20-26-person roster actually cover the day |
| **File the mass-gathering permit path NOW, not closer to the Aug 19 deadline** | City of Ellsworth Ordinance Chapter 14 requires 45 days' notice to the Police Chief plus a corporate surety bond ($200-500 for this scale). Whether ZAOstock qualifies for the city-sponsored exemption (via Art of Ellsworth) is still an open question per doc 1007/1031 - resolve it before Aug 19, because the standalone-applicant path has a hard deadline the exemption path doesn't need to worry about |
| **Get one dedicated First Aid lead with current certification, not an ad-hoc "whoever's around"** | 500 people is exactly the scale where industry standard calls for 1 certified first-aider on-site (not necessarily a full EMS unit) plus a confirmed mutual-aid call to local EMS beforehand. This is a genuinely new line item - nothing in prior ZAOstock planning docs names a first-aid owner |
| **Site Lead and First Aid Lead: Zaal answered both as himself (2026-07-12) - this needs a second look, not a clean close** | Both roles run full-day, in parallel, through the 12pm-6pm show (Site Lead owns the weather go/no-go call; First Aid Lead needs current certification and stays at the medical station). One person can't be in two places for the full show - confirm either a real coverage plan for the overlap, that Zaal is actually certified, or name a second person for one of the two roles before Oct 3 |
| **Skip a shuttle, skip street closures, skip dedicated traffic control** | At 500 people in downtown Ellsworth, existing street parking + 1-2 public lots + directional signage is enough - this is roughly 1/24th the scale of the Maine Lobster Festival (12,000+), which is the only comparable event in the region that actually needs shuttles. Don't over-build logistics the event doesn't need |
| **Pre-write the weather decision tree and name who makes the call, before Oct 3** | This doc gives specific wind/lightning thresholds (Finding 3) - the missing piece in every prior ZAOstock doc was never the thresholds, it was that nobody had been named as the person authorized to actually pause the show |

## Findings

### 1. Full load-in / load-out timeline

Doors are locked at 12:00pm-6:00pm (per doc 428/the live `/program` page). Everything around that fixed window:

**Load-in (6:00am-11:30am, sequential by dependency):**
- 6:00-7:00am - site prep (ground, generator, access) - 2-3 people
- 7:00-8:00am - stage structure assembly - 4-6 people
- 8:00-9:30am - sound + lighting install (parallel tracks) - sound tech + assistant
- 9:30-10:30am - final power/mic checks, remaining lighting
- 9:30-11:00am - staggered vendor load-in windows: heavy/AV vendors 9:30-10:00, food vendors 10:00-10:30, craft/sponsor tables 10:30-11:00 (each vendor gets their specific window by email/text 7-10 days out, with a site map)
- 10:30-11:30am - volunteer check-in opens, briefings, final walkthrough

**Load-out (6:00pm-10:30pm, also sequential):**
- 6:00-6:30pm - exit + grounds sweep
- 6:30-7:30pm - backline, cables, merch, banners
- 7:30-8:30pm - lighting, LED, sound console
- 8:30-9:30pm - stage deconstruction (heaviest labor - 4-6 people)
- 9:30-10:00pm - portable toilets serviced/marked for pickup, signage removed
- 10:00-10:30pm - final sweep, trash, generator shutdown, site returned to normal parklet condition

Total: ~5.5 hours load-in, ~4.5 hours load-out. [FULL - sourced from festival load-in/strike planning guides, cross-checked against this event's specific 12pm-6pm window]

### 3. Safety and emergency plan (this did not exist in writing before this doc)

**First aid:** industry standard for 500 attendees is 1 certified first-aider/EMT on-site, plus a roaming assistant trained in basic first aid, plus a pre-confirmed mutual-aid call to local EMS (5-10 minute response acceptable, doesn't need to be parked on-site). Station location: central, visible from stage, accessible to an ambulance if needed. Equipment: AED, first aid kit, blankets, sharps container. Staffed the full 12pm-6pm window, in place by 11:30am.

**Weather decision tree** (needs a named decision-maker, not just thresholds):
- Wind 25-30mph: secure banners/decorations, remove wind-sensitive stage items
- Wind 30-40mph: pause performance, move people away from stage canopies
- Wind 40mph+: evacuate stage area to solid structures/vehicles
- Lightning 8-10 miles: begin pause prep, move sensitive equipment
- Lightning 6 miles or closer: mandatory pause, move to indoor shelter/vehicles
- Resume only 30+ minutes after the last strike/thunder
- **The Site Lead makes this call** - Command Post monitors weather in real time and has a direct line to local fire/police if conditions worsen. Doc 1030's tent/rain-cover plan for AV gear (already recommended for equipment protection) is a separate, complementary decision, not the same as a people-safety pause.

**Lost child/person protocol:** report to nearest volunteer or info booth → gather description → radio to Command using discreet language → 2-3 volunteers search common areas with 5-minute check-ins → Command Post is the reunification point → escalate to Ellsworth Police (207-667-2168) if not found in 20-30 minutes → log reunification with two witness signatures.

**Evacuation:** Command Post signals via announcement/air horn, gate volunteers direct foot traffic to nearest exit, First Aid stands by at exits, each volunteer accounts for their zone. [FULL - sourced from festival medical/safety operations guides, cross-checked against Ellsworth's own municipal contact info]

### 4. Signage, parking, and traffic - sized correctly for 500 people, not over-built

**Signage** (10-12 signs total is sufficient at this scale): main entrance banner, one directional sign to stage, restroom directions (partner with downtown businesses/town offices for access - Ellsworth's downtown restrooms are walkable, no porta-potty-only plan needed for this specifically), First Aid station sign, Information booth sign, parking directional signs, food vendor zone sign, one large "you are here" map board at entrance, at least two marked emergency exit routes.

**Parking:** existing downtown street parking + 1-2 public lots within a 5-10 minute walk is genuinely sufficient - estimate ~60-70 cars (1 per 7-8 attendees, accounting for walkers/bikers/groups). No shuttle needed - the Maine Lobster Festival (12,000+ attendees) is the only regional comparable that needs one, and this event is roughly 1/24th that scale. No street closures needed unless the city specifically requests it for this event. **Confirmed directly by Zaal 2026-07-11:** the real setup is simpler than the benchmark estimate implied - one public parking lot plus free street parking right at the venue, not a multi-lot plan.

**Portable toilets:** 7 units minimum in 2 clusters (near stage/food, and at the opposite end of the parklet), one ADA unit per cluster, serviced once mid-event (~3pm), fully set up by 10:30am. [FULL - sourced from signage/wayfinding and sanitation planning guides, cross-checked against the Maine Lobster Festival as a regional scale comparable]

### 5. Volunteer check-in and day-of communication system

**Physical check-in:** a Volunteer HQ near the main entrance, opens 10:30am (1.5 hours before doors). Flow: sign-in → gear (shirt, badge, emergency-contact card, meal voucher) → shift/supervisor confirmation → 5-minute role briefing (emergency protocols, lost-child procedure, weather-pause protocol). Budget 2-3 minutes per volunteer to process.

**Day-of communication:** if radios are budgeted ($200-400 to rent 8-10 units), 4 channels - Operations/Main, Security/Gate, Medical, Volunteers/Guest Services - with a brief radio-etiquette protocol (identify yourself, wait for acknowledgment, keep it under 10 seconds). If radios aren't budgeted, 2 designated runners (bike or foot) carry messages between zones. Either way, one Command Post - a central tent with a whiteboard, master roster, emergency numbers, and backup phone - tracks the whole day. [FULL - sourced from volunteer coordination and communication-protocol guides for comparable small events]

### 7. Ellsworth's actual permit requirements, in the city's own words

City of Ellsworth Ordinance Chapter 14 (fetched directly): 45 days' minimum notice to the Police Chief (deadline Aug 19 for an Oct 3 event, confirming doc 1007's memory-sourced figure), a corporate surety bond from a Maine-authorized bonding company (~$200-500 for this scale, to guarantee grounds cleanup), and the Police/Fire Department may require paid personnel on-site at the applicant's expense (budget ~$300-500 for one officer if required, 11am-7pm). Whether ZAOstock qualifies for a city-sponsored exemption via the Art of Ellsworth umbrella - which doc 1007's earlier memory-sourced research flagged as a real possibility - is still the open question, not the deadline or the bond amount.
