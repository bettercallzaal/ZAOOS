## Key Decisions

| Recommendation | Why |
|---|---|
| **Post 3x/week (Mon/Wed/Fri), not literally daily** | Direct 2026 research on small-nonprofit social performance found daily posting measurably underperforms 3x/week for volunteer-run accounts - the algorithm and the audience both reward a post people actually stop for, not volume. A "daily photo series" that quietly becomes 3x/week with daily Stories as filler ships the same 84-day arc without burning out whoever's running it |
| **Do NOT post at standard work-day hours (9-10am)** | The external research's own suggested posting time (9-10am EST) conflicts with ZAO's existing standing rule against work-day posting times - the audience isn't scrolling at a desk at 9am. Post evenings instead, consistent with every other ZAO social cadence already running |
| **Don't lead with "peak fall foliage" as the hook - it doesn't actually land on Oct 3** | Direct research into Maine's own foliage-tracking data: the Acadia/Mount Desert Island region's historical peak color window is October 15-27 - after the festival, not during it. Oct 3 realistically gets early/first color, not peak. Use "the season is just turning" as the honest framing near the event, save true peak-foliage content for a post-event "come back in two weeks" push |
| **Real photos Zaal takes himself beat sourced/stock photos, and solve the licensing question entirely** | NPS's own Flickr photos are "many, but not all" public domain per their own stated policy - every single image would need individual rights-checking before use. ### 3. Daily posting is the wrong ask, backed by real 2026 data - not just a style preference

Multiple current sources on small-nonprofit and small-org social performance converge on the same number: **3-5 posts a week is the sustainable, effective ceiling** for a volunteer-run account; accounts that post daily without a real content pipeline behind them see lower per-post engagement and higher burnout/inconsistency risk than accounts that post 3x/week and never miss. One nonprofit social manager's framing, quoted directly in the research: "You want your posts to be remembered, not just scrolled past... better if they pause on your post 3-4x a week rather than posting 6-7x a week and them never actually reading what you put up." Given this event's crew is already stretched (per doc 1007/1030/1032's findings on unfilled roles), a Mon/Wed/Fri cadence with daily Stories as the lighter-weight filler delivers the same visible momentum without adding a load-bearing daily obligation to someone's plate. [FULL - HeyOrca, Buffer, Zeffy, Constant Contact, all fetched this session]
