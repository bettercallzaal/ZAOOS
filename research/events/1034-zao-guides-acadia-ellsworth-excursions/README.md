## ZAO Guide to Acadia National Park

### Getting in

Acadia's main entrance area (Mount Desert Island / Bar Harbor side) is about 25 miles / 30-40 minutes from Ellsworth via Route 3. The park has an entrance fee (standard NPS pass rules apply) and, separately, a **Cadillac Mountain Summit Road reservation** if you want to drive to the summit - required May 20 through October 25, 2026, $6/vehicle, booked on Recreation.gov. 30% of reservations release 90 days ahead (i.e., now, for an Oct 3 visit), the rest 2 days ahead. No reservation is needed if you hike or bike to the summit instead of driving.

**The free Island Explorer shuttle bus is running** through October 12, 2026, connecting Ellsworth-area hotels, Bar Harbor, and the main park destinations - genuinely useful for skipping the park's parking crunch, and worth mentioning since nothing currently on zaostock.com tells visitors it exists.

### What's actually open the first week of October

- Park Loop Road: open through December 1
- Schoodic Loop Road: open year-round
- Hulls Cove Visitor Center: open daily through October 31
- Seawall and Schoodic Woods Campgrounds: **close October 12**
- Blackwoods Campground: **closes October 19**
- Nature Center: closes on or after Columbus Day (Oct 14)

None of this affects the Oct 3 festival day itself, but it matters for anyone planning to stay through the following week.

### The five must-see spots (ranked, with real timing)

1. **Cadillac Mountain** - highest point on the US East Coast, 360-degree views. Go for golden hour/sunset around Oct 3 (the "first sunrise in the continental US" claim doesn't start until Oct 7). Reservation required if driving.
2. **Jordan Pond + the Bubbles** - the single most-photographed spot in the park; twin-peak reflection in the pond. Best early morning before wind picks up.
3. **Otter Cliffs + Boulder Beach** - dramatic granite cliffs that glow rust-red at sunrise; accessible via the flat, paved Ocean Path.
4. **Bass Harbor Head Light** - Maine's most-photographed lighthouse (it's literally been on a US quarter and an NPS stamp). Golden hour, either sunrise or sunset.
5. **Schoodic Peninsula** - the "quiet side" of Acadia, on the mainland rather than Mount Desert Island. Same dramatic pink-granite coastline, a fraction of the crowds.

### Weather, sunrise/sunset, and what to pack

Early October: daytime highs roughly 55-65°F, dropping to the 40s at night. October is Maine's wettest month historically - assume real odds of rain and bring a waterproof layer, not just a light jacket. Sunrise runs ~7:00-7:15am, sunset ~6:15-6:30pm in early October, both useful for planning excursion start times.

### Honest note on fall foliage

Peak color for the Acadia/Mount Desert Island region lands somewhere in the **October 10-27 window** (two independent research passes this session landed in slightly different but overlapping ranges within that window - treat it as "the second half of October," not a fixed date). Oct 3 will show early color starting on exposed high points and near water, but it is genuinely pre-peak. Say so honestly in any visitor-facing copy.

## Multi-Day Excursion Plan (for out-of-town artists/attendees staying 2-4 extra days)

Ranked for a mixed group of varying fitness - musicians and attendees, not a hiking club:

| # | Excursion | Difficulty | Time | Why it's on the list |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Ocean Path Trail** (Sand Beach to Otter Point, 4.4mi round trip) | Easy - flat, paved/well-maintained, no scrambling | 1.5-2 hrs | The single most inclusive real "I hiked in Acadia" experience. Anyone can do it in normal shoes |
| 2 | **Schoodic Peninsula loop drive + Ravens Nest overlook** | Easy - car-based, short optional walks | 2-3 hrs | Includes people who can't or don't want to hike at all - drivers, walkers, and non-walkers all get the same view. Quieter side of the park, dramatic sunset spot |
| 3 | **Bar Harbor lighthouse/wildlife boat cruise** | Very easy - sit on a boat | 2-3 hrs, ~$45-65/person | A genuinely different experience from hiking; seals, eagles, and 3+ lighthouses from the water. Good group activity when people want to sit down for a bit |
| 4 | **Great Head Trail loop** (1.8mi loop around Sand Beach) | Moderate - rocky headland, real trail feel, still short | 1-1.5 hrs | The middle option between Ocean Path (too easy for some) and Gorham (too much for others) |
| 5 | **Jordan Pond loop** (3.3mi, flat) | Easy | 1-1.5 hrs | Genuinely peaceful, good for anyone who wants nature without exertion - pairs well with popovers at the Jordan Pond House |
| 6 | **Gorham Mountain loop** (1.6mi, 400ft gain, granite scrambling) | Moderate-hard - real scrambling, not for anyone with balance/mobility concerns | 1.5-2 hrs | The opt-in for whoever in the group actually wants a real hike. Don't make this the group default |

Suggested framing for a 3-day extended stay: **Day 1 (arrival/rest)** - downtown Ellsworth, dinner at one of the new restaurant finds below. **Day 2** - Ocean Path Trail in the morning, Jordan Pond in the afternoon, Cadillac Mountain for sunset (reservation required). **Day 3** - Schoodic Peninsula loop drive, or the boat cruise for anyone who'd rather not do more walking.
