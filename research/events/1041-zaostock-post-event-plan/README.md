## Findings

### 1. The 48-hour window: thank-yous first, metrics later

Sourced best practice for small nonprofit/community events is unambiguous on sequencing: volunteer and sponsor thank-yous happen within 24-48 hours of the event ending, well before any metrics compilation. This isn't about politeness - it's about capturing goodwill while the event is still fresh in people's minds, and before volunteers who gave a full Saturday start wondering if their work was noticed. For ZAOstock specifically, this means: a same-week (ideally 48-hour) personal thank-you to Heart of Ellsworth, the confirmed artists (Fellenz, Dcoop), and the volunteer crews from doc 1032's staffing matrix - separate from and faster than the eventual sponsor metrics report. [FULL - per subagent research, cross-referenced against general nonprofit event-closeout guidance]

### 2. VOD + highlight reel + social clips: a realistic 2-3 week timeline, not a same-week one

Doc 1030 already plans the ATEM Mini Pro switcher + livestream setup that will produce raw footage. Turning raw festival footage into a published VOD, an edited highlight reel, and cut-down social clips realistically takes 2-3 weeks for a volunteer-run production, not the days a "check back soon" post might imply. Setting this expectation now - both internally and in any post-event messaging - avoids the credibility cost of promising fast turnaround and missing it. This directly feeds doc 1033's Week 12+ content plan, which currently ends at Oct 3 without a defined "what posts after the festival" arc. [FULL - per subagent research on small-event video production timelines]

### 3. What the promised "post-event metrics report" should actually contain

The live `/sponsor` page promises Virtual Partners: *"Post-event metrics report: livestream views, social reach, content views."* A realistic, gatherable version of that report for a first-year 500-person festival with a livestream:

- **Livestream views**: YouTube + Twitch analytics (both platforms provide this natively, no extra tooling needed)
- **Social reach**: hashtag impressions and tag mentions across the platforms ZAOstock actually posts to (per doc 1033's Firefly-based Mon/Wed/Fri cadence)
- **Content views**: views on the eventual recap post / highlight reel once published
- **Attendance**: a real headcount or estimate, not a guess
- **Sponsor visibility confirmation**: photos documenting the sponsor's actual logo placement (stage banner, printed lineup cards per the `/sponsor` page's own listed benefit) - sponsors want proof their benefit was delivered, not just abstract numbers

This is genuinely achievable with tools ZAOstock already has access to (native platform analytics) - the gap was never capability, it was that nobody had written down what "the report" means until now. [FULL - per subagent research, cross-referenced directly against the live `/sponsor` page's SPONSOR_TRACKS benefits list]

### 4. "Quarterly reports" for Ecosystem Partners is a real commitment that needs a real definition

The `/sponsor` page's Ecosystem Partners track promises "Year-round partnership with quarterly reports" and "Multi-year commitment with first-pick renewal." Taken literally for a single October event, this doesn't make sense - there's no quarterly cadence of content to report on a one-day festival. But doc 1031 already establishes ZAOstock as "Year 1 of a multi-year festival" with an active virtual-event ramp-up (WaveWarZ, COC Concertz, Spaces/talks) running April-September per the confirmed-facts memory. Read that way, "quarterly reports" is a real, deliverable promise - quarterly updates on the broader ZAO Festivals relationship (ZAOstock + ZAOville + the virtual programming), not four reports about one afternoon in Ellsworth. This distinction should be stated explicitly to any Ecosystem Partner, so the promise reads as ambitious-but-real rather than as a line that can't survive contact with an actual sponsor conversation. [FULL - cross-referenced against docs 1031 and the confirmed-facts memory's virtual ramp-up status]

### 5. Fiscal sponsorship close-out: a general pattern worth confirming against ZAOstock's actual terms

ZAOstock's funding runs through a fiscal sponsor structure (Fractured Atlas / New Media Commons, per the live `/sponsor` page's own "Public - tax-deductible" path). General best practice for fiscal-sponsorship close-out after an event: the fiscal sponsor typically handles donor tax receipts directly (not something ZAOstock needs to manage manually), but the sponsored project usually owes the fiscal sponsor a close-out report - expense breakdown, attendance/volunteer counts, an impact statement - within 30-60 days, which is also what determines timing on claiming any remaining held funds. **This is general nonprofit fiscal-sponsorship practice, not confirmed against Fractured Atlas's specific ZAOstock agreement** - the real terms should be checked directly rather than assumed from this general pattern. [PARTIAL - sourced to general fiscal-sponsorship practice, not to Fractured Atlas's actual agreement with ZAOstock, which nobody has checked]

### 6. What a good small-festival recap actually looks like: story and gratitude beat a numbers dump

Real published post-event recaps from comparable community festivals lead with story and gratitude, not a spreadsheet of metrics. They highlight volunteer count, performer count, and direct community quotes rather than revenue or attendance-as-the-headline. For ZAOstock's first year specifically - a "local builder, not a fly-in" narrative already established in the Zaal builder-profile onepager and doc 1031 - a recap post that centers Fellenz/Dcoop, the volunteer crew, and Heart of Ellsworth by name will land better than a stats-first post, even though the stats still belong in the separate sponsor report.
