# 1357 — ZAOstock Charity + Community Benefit Strategy (Jul 2026)

> **Why the charity component matters:** ZAOstock isn't just a music festival — it's a community event with a giving component built in. This distinction: (1) strengthens the Fisher grant application (Fisher looks for community benefit); (2) makes ZAOstock newsworthy to Maine local press ("local festival raises money for [charity]"); (3) gives ZAOstock attendees a reason to spend beyond tickets (donations); (4) adds a moral weight to the WaveWarZ loser-earns mechanic ("even the losing artist contributes to charity").
>
> **DECISION NEEDED:** Which specific charity/cause does ZAOstock 2026 support? This is the most urgent unresolved question in ZAOstock planning. Everything else in this doc follows from that choice.

## Part 1: Charity Partner Selection

### Criteria for the right charity

The ZAOstock charity partner should satisfy as many of these as possible:

| Criteria | Why it matters |
|----------|---------------|
| Maine-based (ideally Ellsworth/Hancock County) | Fisher grant language explicitly values local community benefit; local press is more interested |
| Music or youth arts focus | Alignment with ZAOstock's program = coherent narrative |
| Existing community trust | A charity people already donate to has built-in name recognition |
| Simple donation acceptance | Needs to be able to receive donations via QR code / cash / crypto |
| Nonprofit 501(c)(3) status | Fisher application may require a verified nonprofit partner |

## Part 4: Press + Marketing Angles for the Charity Component

### For Maine local press (highest-impact angle)

Maine local press (Ellsworth American, Bangor Daily News, etc.) covers local events that benefit local causes. The angle:

> **HEADLINE:** "Ellsworth Music Festival Raises Funds for [Charity]"
>
> "ZAOstock, a one-day outdoor music festival on October 3 in Ellsworth, will donate proceeds from ticket sales to [charity name]. The festival features 8 independent music artists selected through WaveWarZ, an online music platform..."

This framing makes ZAOstock a local news story, not just a crypto story. The charity is the community connection that makes it newsworthy to non-crypto audiences.

### For grant applications

Fisher Community Grant (doc 1349) community benefit section:
> "A portion of ZAOstock ticket proceeds and a dedicated add-on donation option will benefit [charity name], a Hancock County [nonprofit]. We are committed to ZAOstock being a community event, not just an entertainment event — the giving component is built into the ticket structure from day one."

Maine Arts Commission (doc 1349) additional community impact:
> "ZAOstock features a community giving component in partnership with [charity name], which serves [constituency] in Hancock County. Local audience engagement + local giving = ZAOstock's community benefit beyond the artistic programming."

### For press pitches to music/crypto outlets

For Decrypt, Bankless, or Green Pill who don't care about the local Maine angle:
> "ZAOstock runs on WaveWarZ — a blockchain music battle where even the losing artist earns. On October 3, the same loser-earns mechanic plays out in a new context: a WaveWarZ community battle will run during the festival intermission, with battle proceeds going to [charity]. The festival that pays losing artists is also the festival that donates to charity via the same blockchain rails."
