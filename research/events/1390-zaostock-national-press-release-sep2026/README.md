**The Economics That Set ZAOstock Apart**

WaveWarZ operates on a "loser-earns" model: both artists in a music battle earn payment regardless of who wins the community vote. To date, the platform has processed **1,245 artist battles** with **9.09 SOL paid directly to artists** and **524 SOL in total volume** — all distributed without label intermediaries and settled on Solana blockchain.

"Most music competition is winner-take-all," said Zaal Panthaki, founder of The ZAO. "WaveWarZ is built on the opposite premise: competing itself should have economic value. Artists who submit to battles earn whether they win or lose. ZAOstock is the live version of that model — the mid-show battle will pay both artists in front of the audience."

ZAOstock's mid-show WaveWarZ live battle will let festival attendees vote in real time via wavewarz.info, with on-chain settlement occurring immediately after the vote closes.

**Governance, Not Gatekeepers**

The ZAO has held **63+ consecutive weekly on-chain governance sessions** using Fractals of Infinity protocol, with three live smart contracts on Optimism Mainnet. All major ZAOstock decisions — from artist selection criteria to charity partner — have been made through on-chain community votes.

The organization has published **1,389 research documents** on DAO governance, music economics, and AI tooling at github.com/bettercallzaal/ZAOOS (CC-BY licensed), making it one of the most documented music communities in existence.
