## Why a Charity Partner Matters

ZAOstock's charity component serves two strategic purposes beyond doing good:

1. **Fisher grant narrative:** The Fisher grant values community benefit. A named charity partner + a committed donation amount strengthens the "community benefit" section of the Fisher grant application (doc 1436, Aug 15 deadline).

2. **Ticket sales and press:** "A portion of ZAOstock proceeds goes to [LOCAL CHARITY]" is a stronger hook for press coverage (Bangor Daily News, Ellsworth American) than "a music festival." Local media covers charities; local charities bring their own audiences to ZAOstock.

---

## Charity Selection Criteria

### Must-Have
- [ ] Based in Maine (ideally Hancock County / Ellsworth area)
- [ ] 501(c)(3) registered (required for Fisher grant co-benefit narrative)
- [ ] Public-facing charitable purpose relevant to music, arts, or community

### Nice-to-Have
- [ ] Has an existing audience they can invite to ZAOstock
- [ ] Willing to co-promote ZAOstock on their channels
- [ ] Staff or volunteers who could help day-of

### Avoid
- Political organizations
- Organizations with recent controversy
- Organizations with a conflicting event on Oct 3

## The Donation Structure

### Option A: Fixed Percentage of Ticket Sales
- **Structure:** 10% of net ticket revenue after Eventbrite fees
- **If tickets sell at target (200 @ avg $15):** ~$270 to charity
- **Stronger for Fisher grant:** Clear, auditable

### Option B: WaveWarZ Community Battle
- **Structure:** 1 WaveWarZ Community Battle at ZAOstock with 100% of proceeds to charity
- **Expected:** $50-500 depending on battle volume
- **Stronger for press:** "On-stage charity battle at ZAOstock"

### Option C: Both (Recommended)
- 5% of ticket sales + 1 Community Battle
- Total estimated donation: $135 (tickets) + $100 (battle) = ~$235 minimum
- For Fisher grant narrative: "ZAOstock integrates a live WaveWarZ charity battle and ticket donation, demonstrating the DAO's public goods model"
