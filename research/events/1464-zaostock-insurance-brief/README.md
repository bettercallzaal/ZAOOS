## Overview

ZAOstock requires event liability insurance before the City of Ellsworth will issue a Special Events Permit. This doc covers what coverage is required, who provides it in Maine, how to get a quote in under 30 minutes, and what certificate language the city needs.

---

## What the Permit Requires

Per Maine municipal standard for special events on public land (and consistent with Ellsworth's general permit terms):

| Requirement | Spec |
|---|---|
| Coverage type | Commercial General Liability (CGL) |
| Minimum limit | $1,000,000 per occurrence / $2,000,000 aggregate |
| Additional insured | City of Ellsworth named as additional insured |
| Certificate format | ACORD 25 (Certificate of Insurance) |
| Deadline | Certificate submitted with permit application OR before permit issuance |

## Alcohol Rider — If Applicable

If a beer garden or any alcohol service is added to ZAOstock:
- Standard CGL does NOT cover alcohol-related incidents (liquor liability exclusion)
- Requires a **Host Liquor Liability** endorsement (+$50–150 on most event policies)
- Maine requires a Special Amusement permit AND a temporary liquor license for any alcohol at public events (separate from the Special Events permit)
- **Recommendation:** Launch ZAOstock dry (no alcohol) to simplify permit and insurance — revisit for 2027
