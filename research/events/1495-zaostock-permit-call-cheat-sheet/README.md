## Questions to Ask (In Priority Order)

### Questions About the Venue

1. **"Is Foggy Bottom Trail available on October 3 for this kind of event?"**
   - If no: "What outdoor venue in Ellsworth would work for 500 people with a PA system?"
   - Listen for: Parklet, Knowlton Park, School grounds, or other city spaces

2. **"What are the setup and teardown time windows?"**
   - Target: setup from 7AM, teardown by 10PM

3. **"Is there power access at [VENUE NAME] for a PA system? Do you know the amperage available?"**
   - Target: 30A+ for PA (per doc 1461)
   - If no power: "Is there a generator permit process?"

4. **"Is there a noise ordinance or decibel limit we need to stay under?"**
   - Maine outdoor events: typically 85-90dB at property line
   - Note the limit if given

5. **"What's the parking situation nearby?"**
   - For 500 attendees, need some overflow parking guidance

### Questions About the Permit Process

6. **"What's the permit application process, fee, and timeline?"**
   - Note: you want the permit confirmed before July 25 if possible (insurance + Fisher grant)

7. **"Do we need a City of Ellsworth certificate of insurance?"**
   - Target: CGL $1M/$2M; additional insured = "City of Ellsworth, 1 City Hall Plaza, Ellsworth ME 04605"
   - (From doc 1464: standard requirement for most Maine city events)

8. **"Is there a first aid or EMS requirement for an event this size?"**
   - Fisher grant budget includes EMS costs (doc 1436)
   - If required: "Do you have preferred EMS vendors?"

### Questions About Partnerships (Ask If They Seem Engaged)

9. **"We're partnering with the Heart of Ellsworth (HoE) for this event. Does a partnership with a local organization help with the permit or the venue?"**
   - Listen for: any reduced fees, priority access, or city support

10. **"Is there an Art of Ellsworth event listing or local arts calendar we should be on?"**
    - Useful for MAC grant (Maine Arts Commission), media, and community awareness

11. **"Would the City be interested in being mentioned as a community partner in our press materials?"**
    - Cities often appreciate this for their own community reporting
