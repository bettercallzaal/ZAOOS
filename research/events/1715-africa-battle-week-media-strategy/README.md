## Why Africa Battle Week Is a Media Story

Africa Battle Week (Sep 22-26) has three distinct news angles:

**1. Music industry angle (Hypebot, Water & Music, Billboard Africa):**
African and diaspora artists competing in an automatic payout platform — losers earn immediately, no label involved. The "loser earns" mechanic applied to a culturally significant music week.

**2. Web3 / crypto angle (Bankless, Decrypt, CoinDesk Music):**
On-chain governance vote selected the charity. On-chain settlement proves the payout. Every SOL transfer is verifiable on Solscan. The entire event — from charity vote to artist payout — is transparent and immutable.

**3. African music / culture angle (Notjustok, TurnTable Charts, Afrobeats Intelligence, OkayAfrica):**
ZAO is highlighting African and diaspora artists on a global platform. Community members organized the event from within ZAO's governance structure. The charity payout goes to an Africa-focused music organization.

Each angle reaches a different audience. The goal is coverage from at least one outlet in each category.

## Pitch Angles by Target

### For Music Industry Media (Hypebot, Water & Music, Billboard Africa)

**Hook:** "African and diaspora artists competing in the first music battle week where the loser earns automatically — on Solana, in seconds."

**Lead with:**
- The loser-earns mechanic (music industry relevance)
- Africa Battle Week as a community-organized event, not a corporate marketing campaign
- Artists come from Afrobeats, Afropop, Afrohouse, diaspora hip-hop
- The charity payout: [charity name from Jul 24-25 vote] receives SOL on Sep 26

**Avoid:** "DAO," "ZOR," "Optimism" — explain as "fan community governance" and "automatic payout" instead.

### For Web3 / Crypto Media (Bankless, Decrypt, CoinDesk)

**Hook:** "A music DAO ran a community governance vote on Snapshot (157 voters, ERC-1155 strategy) to select a charity. Then ran 5 days of artist battles. Then sent the charity payment on-chain in one TX."

**Lead with:**
- Snapshot governance mechanics (ZOR ERC-1155, 157 voters)
- On-chain settlement: every artist payout and charity payment is a verifiable Solscan TX
- "This is what on-chain coordination looks like when it's applied to culture, not just DeFi"
- 100+ consecutive governance weeks with zero quorum failures = proven governance record

**Include:** OREC contract address on Optimism, ZOR contract, charity wallet + TX link (after Sep 26)

### For African Music / Culture Media (Notjustok, OkayAfrica, Afrobeats Intelligence)

**Hook:** "Five days of African and diaspora artists competing — and the artist who loses earns too."

**Lead with:**
- The artist roster (names, genres, where they're from)
- Community-organized: ZAO members proposed and approved this event, not a label
- Charity tie-in: [charity name] receives proceeds
- "ZAO is a music platform built to pay artists at every tier — not just the winners"

**Tone:** Warmer, culture-first. ZAO's connection to African music is genuine — Africa Battle Week came from within ZAO's community, not from a top-down campaign.
