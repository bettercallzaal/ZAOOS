## Interview Structure (BCZ Yapz — Hannah)

**Format:** 45-60 min conversational, 8-10 core questions, 2-3 spontaneous follow-ups, 5 min event pitch at the end. Record remote. Edit into short-form clips for distribution.

### Opening (5 min — warm her up)
1. "For people who've never been, walk us through your Blue Hill. What does it look like, smell like, sound like?"
2. "You lived in Spain and Central America — what did those places teach you about food that Maine hasn't yet figured out?"

### FarmDrop Model (15 min — core story)
3. "FarmDrop isn't a farmers' market and it isn't Instacart. What is it, and how do you explain it to your grandma?"
4. "You pay farmers *before* the harvest. Most businesses do it the opposite way. Why'd you flip that, and what changed when you did?"
5. "You went from 1 location in Blue Hill in 2016 to 6 regions today. What was the hardest region to crack, and what made it click?"

### Food for Health (15 min — the ask)
6. "Food for Health launched in 2025 right as SNAP was getting cut. What were you seeing on the ground that made you say 'we have to formalize this now'?"
7. "You're reinvesting 2% of FarmDrop sales into FFH and waiting on a $150K USDA SARE grant for June. If that grant hits, what does the program look like in 12 months?"
8. "Walk us through one family who's been through FFH. What changed for them?"
*(Hannah to bring one anonymized story ready.)*

### May 24 Event (10 min — the close)
9. "Tell me about SoulBenders at Halcyon Grange. Why is this the right band, the right room, and the right night for FarmDrop?"
10. "If someone watching this lives 3 hours from Blue Hill and can't make May 24, what are the three ways they can still support FFH right now?"

### Wildcard / Close (5 min)
- "What's Barley up to these days?" (Hannah's dog — humanizes her)
- "Finish this: *The reason Maine can feed itself better than any other state is ____.*"
