## Who they are

Kshitij Garg and Rishabh - Delhi-based, 3-4 years in Web3 (Rishabh deep in DeFi, having worked with several DeFi companies; Kshitij on communities + marketing for DeFi protocols and bridges). Last year they started **DDAN**, a pop-up-stage / community-stay business for major Web3 conferences:

- **ETHGlobal Delhi** - first pop-up stage, 50+ builders staying 10 days. Registered the company in India off the back of it.
- **India Blockchain Week (IBW)** - Bangalore flagship, every November - hosted 100+ builders, marketers, founders.
- **Solana Breakpoint 2** - tried global expansion; paused further global plans (Token2049 Dubai shelved) due to geopolitical/war situation, refocusing on the Indian market this year.

## Their DevCon India 2026 plan

- **3 properties booked (2 finalized), hosting 160+ people**, all 15-20 min from the DevCon venue:
  1. **Developer house** - for devs who want to stay and build on a project.
  2. **Rooftop / founders house** - founders, creators, devs; terrace for evening/night chill + branded nights; a podcast studio is being set up on-site.
  3. **Newcomer house** - students / people new to Web3 attending for the first time.
- Model = community-stay; until now free for attendees (they target people coming for the event who just need space). Now opening **sponsorship "tokens"** - co-host a property, pay a small fee to host a terrace event (they handle crowd + execution), guest spots on the on-site podcast.
- Also partnering with clubs/cafes to help companies curate + execute events during DevCon.
- Framing: "pop-up stay is the new culture in India" - Gen Z, extroverted, days-and-nights-together vs the "half-hour at the event" model.

## What Zaal + Sam bring (and the ZAO pitch)

- Zaal + Sam (candytoybox) co-founded **The ZAO** (on-chain music); WaveWarZ is the first incubated project - live-traded music battles on Solana where artists get paid as people trade. 5 spaces Mon-Fri 11am ET + 5 at 8:30pm ET, big battles Sundays.
- Reference: the 2024 Miami event - 50+ on-chain music communities, 20+ musicians, 100+ visual artists, an Airbnb with 16 teammates/artists, and a post-event cipher song with 10 artists = an inadvertent **artist residency**. Lesson: it is a loss-leader unless you pick a niche; the real value is the music network.
- Zaal's DevCon India plan: a crew of ~5 - media (the "Let's Talk About Ethereum" podcast, with 2 other founders, crowdfunded) + artists (named **Hurricane** and **Django** as willing if funded) + flex people. His aunt/uncle are 45 min from the venue. Personally going regardless; bringing others depends on funding/crowdfunding.
