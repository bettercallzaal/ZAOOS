> **Goal:** Catch-up where Zaal shares three threads - he's on the DevCon India music-stage team, he's launching a Farcaster builder-media series, and he floats turning the ZABAL Games build-a-thon into a proto-DAO - and civilmonkey gives advisory pushback.

## What happened

Three threads, plus a milestone:

**1. DevCon India music stage.** Zaal is on the team helping run the music stage for **DevCon India** - alongside Ethereum Foundation organizers and musicians ("no one's like me, which is great, so I add a lot of value"). He wants to get himself there and crowdfund to bring **Thy Revolution** (UK) and **Iman** (Zambia) - "with the three of us we could do some real good damage." His contacts so far are **Candy / Candyloo** and **Carlota** (Telegram only). civilmonkey's advice: apply in time for **scholarships** - they were offered last year and were closing around this time of year.

**2. Farcaster builder-media series.** Inspired by **GM Farcaster** (the de-facto premier Farcaster news network with little competition), Zaal is building a complementary lane - a **builder-focused media/interview series**, not a competitor. Plan: take the **ZABAL Games batches + agentic-bootcamp transcripts**, put them on his page, and make them **AI-queryable** so a builder joining in July can mine the value. Current guest cadence: Rizzle (Thu), Cassie (Fri), Sofa (today), Kenny (Mon), Adrian (end of month). civilmonkey: a deeper-conversation show is a real lane; provide **RSS podcast feeds** (like GM Farcaster does) - they age well and are searchable.

**3. ZABAL Games proto-DAO idea (exploratory).** Zaal's new idea: turn the ZABAL Games build-a-thon into a **mini proto-DAO** where contributors earn respect tokens for submissions (plus-one others rather than vote), and an **algorithm/agent scores contributions** against metrics + gives improvement feedback - smoothing the slow human-consensus friction of the Fractal Respect game. Specifically for **code**: a bot evaluates PRs' value-add, and **auditing** earns more than writing ("in the era of agentically-created code, auditing is the bottleneck, not writing" - e.g. 3 approvers get 5 points each, the author gets 1-2). Explicitly **NOT** changing the Monday Fractal - a separate thing for ZABAL Games. civilmonkey is **hesitant**: keep human consensus as the core; agents could offer optional pre-submission feedback, but algorithmic evaluation is inherently biased. He points to **DeepFunding** (@deep_funding on X) - AI evaluation of open-source GitHub repos for public goods - as prior art.

**Milestone:** Yesterday Zaal couldn't make the Monday Fractal, but it **still reached consensus and people earned respect** - he handed it off to **Iman**. civilmonkey: "huge fan of it working without you being there - that's huge." Zaal's next focus: improve those ZAO functions so it onboards more smoothly without him.
