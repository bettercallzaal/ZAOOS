## The COC Concertz answer (resolves Fellenz challenge 4)

This is what the call was for. Where COC Concertz framing/ownership lands:

- **It is a 50/50 joint venture / partnership** between The ZAO and the COC - "both teams coming together for that one thing that's in the middle of your brand and our brand" (Zaal). Thy Rev: "yeah, that's kind of what I'm thinking."
- **Thy Rev is indifferent on the naming.** Verbatim: "I'm indifferent, I don't give a shit who calls what." He has no attachment to who gets top billing - the Fellenz worry about The ZAO claiming COC's brand is not a worry from Thy Rev's side.
- **Zaal's explicit intent is the opposite of ownership-grab:** he built COC Concertz so the COC team has "a place in the ZAO that you own... this is my baby... I can point at it. But this wouldn't happen without you guys." And: "I would love for it to be separate of me so that you guys don't need me to succeed." Zaal wants COC to own it and for it to survive without him.
- **The Fellenz misunderstanding, explained:** Fellenz ("Flens") was asked to do a COC concert way back with **Mike Smart in the metaverse**, so he conflated that older thing with today's COC Concertz. That is the root of his confusion - not a real ownership conflict.
- **Current dependency:** Zaal has to be present for the **stream portion** because Thy Rev does not have a laptop yet. Once Thy Rev has a laptop, he manages that side and it becomes more independent of Zaal.

Net for the Fellenz reply: the "partnership, Thy Revolution leads, not ZAO-owned" framing is **confirmed and safe to publish** - Thy Rev is aligned and indifferent on naming, and Zaal's whole intent is COC ownership. The "(framing pending COC confirmation)" tag can come off.
