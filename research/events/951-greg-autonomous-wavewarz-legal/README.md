## Summary

Wave Wars is a live trading plus music competition platform with three founders (1.5 years development). Team operates smart contract battles with affiliate revenue-share (50/50 splits to community builders). Current concerns: personal liability protection, IP/trademark safeguarding for "Wave Wars" name and novel gamification mechanism, YouTube community strike flagging gambling-like content, choice between US LLC vs offshore entity (Cayman, Panama, Singapore, BVI).

Greg advised: risk-first entity planning (identify liability exposure before picking structure), IP requires separate global filings beyond entity formation, offshore jurisdictions raise litigation costs but platform rules (YouTube) trump jurisdiction, hybrid model (offshore holding + US pass-through) enables US bank lending while reducing tax friction. Autonomous offers 18+ formation jurisdictions.

## Memory-Worthy

**People:**
- Greg (lawyer at Autonomous) - IP/entity formation specialist, web3 experience (ApeSwap), Argentina-based, open-source mindset re: information sharing.

**Projects/Partners:**
- Autonomous (otomos.com) - Entity formation SaaS for crypto teams, 18+ jurisdictions, nominee director services. Greg works there.

**Strategy Insights:**
- Risk-first entity planning: do not assume LLC. Start with "what are we protecting?" (liability, IP, personal income, artists' claims) then pick structure.
- IP != entity: trademark/copyright filings are separate, costly, multi-jurisdictional work. Entity structure does NOT protect IP by itself.
- Offshore for friction, not safety: Cayman/BVI/Panama entities don't make Wave Wars legal, but raise litigation cost for plaintiffs. May be worth 5-10% of deal, not legal shield per se.
- Hybrid model (offshore holding + US pass-through) reduces tax friction while maintaining bank loan eligibility in US.
- Platform rules trump jurisdiction: YouTube's gambling policy applies regardless of where entity is formed. Real mitigation is content policy clarification, not structure.
