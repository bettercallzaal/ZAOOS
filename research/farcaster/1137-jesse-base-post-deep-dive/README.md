## Part 1: milob's Cast (The Technical Critique)

**Cast Hash:** 0x98bd95d414a157e12c9967925ce3d391b5710303  
**Author:** Milo Bowman (@milob, FID: 231807)  
**Bio:** Base Ambassador, Founder of Brighton Blockchain, Optimism delegate  
**Posted:** 2026-07-15/16 (exact timestamp: Farcaster epoch 174737601)

### Full Cast Text

> I've been repeatedly disappointed by crypto apps deciding to give up on whatever they were doing, when it seems like a few small tweaks could be all they need.
>
> Content coins correctly worked out that everyone wants to earn money. But they missed that very few want to spend money. For the content coin model to work, the money needs to come from somewhere else, and in web2.0, that's advertising (or, increasingly for X, a 'premium' subscription).
>
> The core idea - getting paid to create value on social media - is a great one. The tweak is in 'Where is the money coming from?'. Other users buying content coins was not it. But if Base app introduced ads, and used the ad revenue to buy content coins? Sadly it seems we won't know.
>
> Farcaster sold premium ('pro') subscriptions - but seemingly did nothing with the money! I wonder what would happen if they *pivoted the method not the mission*, and made the small tweak of distributing the pro subscription revenue to the best posts on the platform.

### What milob Is Saying

1. **The core idea is sound**: Paying creators for onchain value creation is valid, even elegant.
2. **The monetization was backwards**: Platforms tried to extract money from users via coins, instead of sourcing it from an ad layer or subscription revenue.
3. **The fix is not radical**: A single architectural change—redirecting subscription or ad revenue to creators—could have made creator coins viable.
4. **Base gave up too early**: Instead of pivoting the monetization model, they abandoned the entire thesis.

### Why This Matters

milob is a respected Base Ambassador and Optimism delegate. This is not a fringe take—it's a signal that the builder community sees the real failure as product design, not market reality. The implication: anyone who solves the monetization problem could own creator coins authentically.

## Part 2: Jesse Pollak's Admission (The Strategic Pivot)

**Date of Announcement:** 2026-07-15  
**Platform:** X / Farcaster (cross-posted)  
**Character:** Public, widely covered by major crypto news outlets

### Jesse's Core Statement

From Coinbase blog and X post:

> "I was definitively wrong" regarding his bet on onchain social applications driving crypto adoption.
>
> "On the app, my focus is on building Base into the blockchain for global finance. To that end, I've handed the Base app back to the Coinbase mothership, where my now good friend Cobie will be taking it from here."

### What Failed (Explicit Admission)

1. **Creator coins** - "disintegrated completely" in Q1 2026
2. **Farcaster bets** - Spending two years promoting Farcaster as the core social layer
3. **Mini apps** - Zora, native Farcaster integrations
4. **Cultural assumption** - That social applications would drive the next wave of crypto adoption

Pollak described the failure as "a punch in the face."

### What Base Is Shifting To (2026 Priorities)

1. **Trading** - Support for tokenized stocks, meme tokens, application tokens, onchain perps
2. **Payments** - Stablecoin infrastructure for consumers and businesses
3. **AI Agents** - Infrastructure for software-based economic systems requiring programmable digital money

### Leadership Change

Jordan Fish ("Cobie", founder of Echo, acquired by Coinbase for $375M) is taking over the Base app team. Pollak is moving to focus on Base as blockchain infrastructure.
