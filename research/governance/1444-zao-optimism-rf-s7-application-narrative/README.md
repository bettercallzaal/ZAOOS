## What Optimism Retro Funding Is

Optimism Retro Funding (RF) is a grants program that retrospectively rewards open-source projects that have created positive impact in the Optimism ecosystem. Unlike forward-looking grants (apply → get money → do work), RF rewards work that has already been done.

**Key criteria for RF eligibility:**
1. Public goods that benefit the Optimism ecosystem
2. Deployed contracts or tools on Optimism Mainnet
3. Active community use and governance
4. Open-source and accessible

**The ZAO qualifies on all four criteria.**

## Application Narrative (Paste-Ready)

### Project Name
The ZAO — Optimism Fractal Governance for Music DAO

### Project Description (500 words max)

The ZAO is a Respect-based decentralized autonomous organization (DAO) that governs WaveWarZ, a music battle platform on Solana, and produces ZAOstock, an annual music festival in Ellsworth, Maine.

**What The ZAO Has Built on Optimism**

The ZAO deployed three smart contracts on Optimism Mainnet:
- **OG Token** (ERC-20, `0x34cE89baA7E4a4B00E17F7E4C0cb97105C216957`): ZAO's governance participation token
- **ZOR Token** (ERC-1155, `0x9885CCeEf7E8371Bf8d6f2413723D25917E7445c`): Respect token awarded through Optimism Fractal governance sessions
- **OREC** (`0xcB05F9254765CA521F7698e61E0A6CA6456Be532`): The Optimism Respect-based Execution Contract, enabling on-chain proposal execution weighted by Respect rather than plutocratic token voting

**What Makes ZAO's Governance Novel**

ZAO uses the **Optimism Fractal** model — a governance mechanism where participants earn Respect (ZOR) through peer evaluation in small groups rather than through token purchases. This makes ZAO's governance non-plutocratic: you earn influence by showing up and demonstrating value, not by buying it.

As of July 2026, ZAO has conducted **63+ consecutive weekly governance sessions** without a quorum failure — a documented streak that represents one of the longest continuous governance records in the Optimism ecosystem. Every session is documented in ZAOOS (ZAO Operating System), a CC-BY open corpus now exceeding 1,440 documents.

**WaveWarZ as a Public Goods Music Platform**

WaveWarZ is the revenue-generating product that funds ZAO's operations and demonstrates the "DAO economy" thesis: a DAO can govern a self-sustaining protocol that pays its community members (artists) regardless of outcome. As of July 2026:
- 1,245+ battles completed on Solana
- 524 SOL (~$104K) in total trading volume
- 9.09 SOL ($1,820) paid to **losing artists** via the loser-earns mechanism
- 127 SOL ($25,469) distributed to winning traders

The "loser-earns" mechanism is architecturally novel: it means every artist who participates in a WaveWarZ battle earns something, structurally redistributing value to musicians who would otherwise earn $0.004/stream on Spotify.

**ZAOstock as a Public Goods Festival**

ZAOstock (October 3, 2026) is the first DAO-produced music festival — free general admission, featuring a live WaveWarZ battle on stage, an on-chain governance vote conducted from the crowd, and a charity giving component. It demonstrates that DAOs can produce physical-world cultural events.

**ZAOOS as Open Infrastructure**

The ZAO Operating System (ZAOOS) is a CC-BY-licensed corpus of 1,440+ documents covering ZAO's governance methodology, WaveWarZ protocol economics, event production, and community building. It is designed as a reusable template for other music DAOs and is actively cited in academic research on DAO governance models.
