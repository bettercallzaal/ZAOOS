## Why a Fractal, Not Just a Page

The strategic framing from Iman's brief is correct and worth stating explicitly:

**A regional page is a dead end.** It gives African artists and fans something to look at, but no stake in how it grows. The first instinct for any new market is "wait and see if it takes off." A governance structure inverts that: early contributors earn Respect — a verifiable on-chain record — that makes getting in early worth something. That Respect becomes their standing as the community scales.

**A Fractal node is a live community.** It runs the same Respect Game ZAO has been running for 100+ consecutive weeks. African contributors are recognized as full ZAO members — not a side channel.

**The replication template.** WaveWarzAfrica is Version 1 of the start-a-fractal playbook (doc 1232). What works here is documented and handed to the next community. Every Fractal expansion makes the ZAO's governance model more credible and more citable.

From the start-a-fractal playbook (doc 1232), Phase 0-2 for WaveWarzAfrica:

### Phase 0 — Prerequisites (now → Month 1)

Before any tooling:
- [ ] 6+ recurring African participants confirmed (Iman as host candidate)
- [ ] Mission statement written: "WaveWarzAfrica: African music creators earning recognition and prize money through peer governance"
- [ ] Host committed for Season 1 (12 sessions = ~3 months at weekly cadence)
- [ ] Discord channel or venue for sessions ready (can share ZAO main Discord initially)

### Phase 1 — Soft Launch: Respect Game (Sessions 1-12 = Season 1, ~Month 1-3)

No blockchain required. The game itself is the thing.

**Session format (60 min):**
1. Randomize into groups of 4-6
2. Each person: "Here's what I contributed to WaveWarzAfrica / the African music community since last time" (~4 min each)
3. Group reaches 2/3 consensus on ranking (1=lowest, 6=highest)
4. Record rankings in a spreadsheet: person, session number, rank
5. Fibonacci distribution: rank 6=110 pts, 5=68, 4=42, 3=26, 2=16, 1=10

**What to track:** attendance, contributions described, rankings. This is the Respect ledger.

**What to skip in Phase 1:** wallets, tokens, OREC, frapps.xyz. The game is the thing; tooling is Phase 2.

**WaveWarzAfrica-specific contribution categories:**
- Organizing or promoting battles in African markets
- Onboarding new African artists or fans
- Creating content about WaveWarz for African audiences
- Translating materials or facilitating in local languages
- Running local Zabal Games sessions or events

## Zabal Games Integration

Zabal Games is the lighter-weight entry point for non-artists — fans and newcomers who want to be part of WaveWarzAfrica before they're ready to submit to a battle.

**Why this matters for Fractal adoption:**
- Widens the on-ramp: not everyone joins as an artist first
- Creates a steady content stream between battle events
- Zabal Games participation = a contribution that earns Respect (playing, recruiting, running events)

## Plain-Language Explainer (Ready for Page Use)

Written for zero crypto or music-industry background. Can be published directly on the WaveWarzAfrica page.

**What is ZAO?**
A community built around helping musicians make money and build a following using blockchain technology — not relying on labels or streaming platforms alone.

**What is a Fractal?**
A self-governing group inside ZAO. Members earn recognition ("Respect") for what they actually contribute, and the group makes its own decisions about how it grows — while staying connected to the bigger ZAO network.

**What is WaveWarZ?**
A music battle competition — two artists go head-to-head with their best songs, fans vote, and the winner takes a real prize. Fans who backed the winner can win too.

**What does "onchain" mean?**
Votes, wins, and payouts are recorded on the blockchain — a public record that can't be quietly changed or hidden.

**What are Zabal Games?**
Games added to WaveWarzAfrica so fans and newcomers can be part of the community even if they're not competing as artists.

**Why should an African artist care?**
Get paid directly by fans, build a public record of your work and wins, gain real standing in the community, and get seen by a global audience — without a label deal first.

**Why should a fan care?**
Voting and backing can come with real rewards, and it's a way to be part of an artist's story from the beginning.
