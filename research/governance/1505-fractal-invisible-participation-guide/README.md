# 1505 — Invisible Participation: Onboarding Non-Technical Members to the ZAO Fractal

> From doc 1227 (Eden learnings): "make participation invisible to non-technical users." This doc is the concrete playbook for doing that. Who to use it with, what to say, what steps to skip, what to never mention first.

---

## The Core Principle

A music artist joining the ZAO Fractal does not need to understand:
- What a DAO is
- What Optimism is
- What an ERC-1155 token is
- What "soulbound" means
- What OREC or ORDAO stands for

They need to understand:
1. Show up Monday 6pm EST
2. Tell the group what you did for ZAO this week
3. The group decides who did the most — and that determines who gets a say in what happens next

Everything else is infrastructure. It should be invisible.

---

## Who This Is For

This guide is for whoever is running ZAO Fractal onboarding:
- Zaal doing a 1:1 pitch call with a new artist
- ZOE managing a new member's first week
- A community liaison onboarding a WaveWarZ Africa batch

If someone is asking "what is a wallet?" or "I don't really understand crypto" — this is the guide to use.

---

## Phase 0: The Pitch (Before They Say Yes)

**What to say:**
> "Every Monday we do a 90-minute session where the group votes on who contributed the most that week. Whoever contributes most gets more say in decisions. You don't need to understand crypto — you just need to show up and talk about what you did."

**What NOT to say:**
- Don't mention ZOR, OREC, ORDAO, Respect1155 — these come later or never
- Don't explain blockchain
- Don't say "DAO governance protocol"
- Don't lead with the token

**If they ask about the token:**
> "There's a token you accumulate over time just by participating — ZOR. It tracks who's been most active and gives them more voting weight. You don't have to do anything to get it — it happens automatically when you participate."

**If they ask about wallets:**
> "You'll need a wallet eventually to claim your tokens, but you can participate in sessions for weeks without one. We'll set that up together when you're ready."

---

## Phase 1: First Session (Week 1)

**Before the session:**
Send them one message:
> "Join the Zoom at [link] Monday at 6pm EST. You'll share for 3-4 minutes about what you did for ZAO this week. Anything counts — music, outreach, artwork, just showing up. See you there!"

No pre-reading required. No setup required. No wallet required.

**During the session:**
- Seat them in a group of 4-6 (if running multiple groups)
- Coach them if needed: "Tell us about one thing you did for ZAO or WaveWarZ this week"
- Don't draw attention to the blockchain component — just run the Respect Game as a conversation
- After the session, ZOE or Zaal submits results to frapps.xyz — this happens in the background

**After the session (within 24h):**
Send them:
> "Great first session! You participated in Fractal #[N]. Your contributions were heard and recognized. You've started building your Respect score, which is how ZAO tracks who shapes the future of the community. Next session is Monday [date]."

No mention of on-chain status. No wallet prompt. Just: you did it, it counted.

---

## Phase 2: Weeks 2-6 (Building Rhythm)

**Goal:** Get them to 6 sessions without ever feeling lost.

**What to communicate:**
- Weekly reminder: "Fractal tomorrow 6pm — what did you do for ZAO this week?"
- Monthly: "You've been to [N] sessions this season. Your Respect is growing."

**What to defer:**
- Wallet setup: defer until they ask or until week 4-6
- ZOR token: mention only if they bring it up or as a reward milestone
- frapps.xyz: don't link it until they're comfortable

**When they ask "how do I check my score?":**
> "Your Fractal stats are in [link to ZAO app leaderboard tab]. No wallet needed — just sign in with Farcaster."

If they're not on Farcaster: don't force it. Have Zaal or ZOE manually check their score for them and send a screenshot. Friction at week 3 kills retention.

---

## Phase 3: Wallet Setup (When They're Ready, ~Week 6-12)

**The signal they're ready:** They ask "how do I get my tokens?" or "how do I actually use my Respect?"

**The one-time setup:**
1. Install Coinbase Wallet (simplest for ZAO/OP ecosystem) or Rainbow
2. Fund with a tiny amount of ETH on Optimism (ZOE or Zaal can send $2-5 for gas if they don't know how)
3. Check their ZOR balance on Optimism
4. Explain: "This is your proof of contribution. It doesn't have a price you can sell it for — it's not that kind of token. It's your reputation in the DAO."

**What NOT to do:**
- Don't make them set up a wallet before their first session
- Don't pressure wallet setup at any point — it's optional
- Don't ask them to connect a wallet to participate in sessions

## The Consent Ladder

Not everyone will go all the way to wallet. That's fine. There are meaningful levels of participation:

| Level | What they do | What they get | Wallet required? |
|-------|-------------|----------------|------------------|
| **Observer** | Joins sessions, listens | Community belonging | No |
| **Participant** | Shows up, shares contributions | Respect score, voting voice | No (Farcaster login only) |
| **Contributor** | Regular attendance, Season badge | Named in season history, higher Respect | No |
| **Stakeholder** | Wallet setup, ZOR claimed | On-chain governance weight, OP network presence | Yes |
| **Council member** | Highest Respect holders in a season | Fractal council seats, proposal rights | Yes |

A non-technical artist can be a meaningful **Contributor** without ever touching a wallet. The belonging, the voice, and the recognition are real regardless of on-chain status.

## Common Moments Where People Drop Off

**Week 0 (before first session):** They feel confused or intimidated.
- Fix: single clear message, no jargon, no setup required

**Week 1 (first session):** They don't know how to participate.
- Fix: brief prep call or message, "tell us what you did"

**Week 2 (deciding to come back):** They didn't feel like it mattered.
- Fix: DM after week 1 confirming they participated and their contribution was heard

**Week 4 (plateau):** Sessions feel repetitive.
- Fix: season framing ("you're halfway to a Season badge"), name what they've built

**Week 8 (wallet prompt):** They feel forced.
- Fix: never force. Bring it up only when they ask.

## What ZOE Can Automate

ZOE should handle these touchpoints without Zaal:
- **Pre-session reminder** (Telegram/DM, 24h before): "Fractal tomorrow at 6pm. What did you do for ZAO this week?"
- **Post-session confirmation** (within 2h): "You participated in Fractal #[N]. Your Respect is building."
- **4-session milestone**: "You've been to 4 sessions. You're in the middle of [Season name]. Keep going."
- **Season end** (after session 12): "Season [N] complete. You attended [X] of 12 sessions. Here's your season badge."

The human (Zaal or liaison) handles: first-session coaching, wallet setup, escalated questions, relationship maintenance.
