## Who This Is For

A community leader who has said "yes" to running a Fractal. They've seen the pitch (doc 1502), they understand participation can be invisible to non-technical members (doc 1505), and now they need the concrete operational steps to launch.

**Audience:** Community managers, DAO operators, music collective leads, creative org founders — not Zaal.

---

## Phase 0: Pre-Launch (1–2 weeks before Session 1)

### Decisions to make before anything else

| Decision | Options | Recommendation |
|----------|---------|----------------|
| Platform | Zoom, Discord Stage, Twitter Spaces, IRL | Video call for the first 8 sessions; switch later |
| Cadence | Weekly, biweekly | Weekly — the streak is the product |
| Day + time | Any | Match when your community already shows up; Monday 6pm ET is ZAO's slot |
| Wallet requirement | Required from day 1, or optional initially | Optional — introduce wallets at Session 4–8 (doc 1505 consent ladder) |
| Who runs it | You alone, or rotating | You for Sessions 1–3; introduce co-facilitators at Session 4 |

### Checklist: Week before Session 1

- [ ] Pick your platform and create a recurring calendar invite
- [ ] Announce to your community: "We're running a Respect Game starting [DATE]. Here's what it is: [2-sentence explanation]"
- [ ] Identify 4–8 people who will definitely attend Session 1 (quality > quantity)
- [ ] Read doc 1505 (invisible participation) — know which members need wallet-free entry
- [ ] Contact ZAO if you want to be part of the ZAO Fractal network (optional but recommended for on-chain Respect)

**2-sentence community announcement (paste and edit):**
> "Starting [DATE], we're running a weekly Respect Game — a 60-minute call where we recognize who contributed most this week and give them a voice in how we make decisions. No tokens, no voting by wealth — just contribution. Join us [DAY] at [TIME]."

## Phase 1: Sessions 1–3 (Weeks 1–3)

### Session 1: The Demo

Session 1's only job is to make it feel good. Not to explain the mechanism. Not to set up wallets.

**Script for opening (1 minute):**
> "Welcome to our first Respect Game. Here's all you need to know: in a few minutes we'll split into small groups, each group will share what they did for [community name] this week, then rank each other by who contributed most. That's it. The ranking feeds into how we make decisions together."

**Session 1 structure (45 minutes):**

| Time | What |
|------|------|
| 0–5 min | Welcome. Explain the one rule: "rank each other, 1st to last" |
| 5–10 min | Everyone shares their contribution in 30 seconds |
| 10–25 min | Breakout groups (3–4 people). Each group ranks. |
| 25–35 min | Bring groups back. Collect rankings verbally or in chat. |
| 35–40 min | Announce the top-ranked contributor of the week. Celebrate them briefly. |
| 40–45 min | "See you next week." |

**What to skip in Session 1:**
- OREC / ORDAO / contracts — skip entirely
- Wallet addresses — skip entirely
- Governance implications — skip entirely
- What "Respect" is technically — describe it as "recognition points"

**After Session 1:**
- [ ] Send a message to attendees: "That was Session 1 of your Fractal. Same time next week."
- [ ] Log the rankings somewhere simple (Google Sheet, Notion, DM) — even if not on-chain yet

### Session 2: The Habit

Session 2 is about showing up again. The only goal is attendance ≥ 75% of Session 1.

**Opening:**
> "Welcome back to Session [2] of our Respect Game. This is our streak. Last week, [Name] earned the most recognition. This week, let's do it again."

**New element to introduce:**
- One question to the group: "Was there anything last week where you felt someone's contribution wasn't recognized? How can we do better this time?"

**After Session 2:**
- [ ] If attendance ≥ 4: you have a real community — continue
- [ ] If attendance < 4: individually reach out to Session 1 attendees before Session 3

### Session 3: The Foundation

By Session 3, you have a streak. A small one, but real.

**New elements to introduce:**
- Share that rankings will eventually go on-chain (mention it casually, not urgently)
- Ask: "Who else should be in this room?" — invite one person each week

**After Session 3:**
- [ ] Decide: introduce wallets at Session 4 or Session 8? (See consent ladder, doc 1505)
- [ ] If connecting to ZAO Fractal network: reach out to Zaal with your first 3 rankings

## Phase 2: Sessions 4–12 (Weeks 4–12, Season 1)

### When to introduce wallets

Use the consent ladder from doc 1505:

| Stage | When | What You Say |
|-------|------|--------------|
| Observer → Participant | Session 1 | "Just show up" |
| Participant → Contributor | Session 2–3 | "Share what you did" |
| Contributor → Stakeholder | Session 4–8 | "Your scores are going on-chain — here's how to set up a wallet" |
| Stakeholder → Council | Session 8–12 | "You can now vote on proposals" |

**Script for introducing wallets (use at Session 4 or later):**
> "Starting today, your Respect scores will be recorded on-chain — meaning they're permanent and verifiable. This means you'll need a wallet. It takes 5 minutes to set one up. I'll help anyone who needs it after the session. You can still participate without a wallet — we'll record your score and add it when you're ready."

### When to introduce on-chain Respect (OREC)

Only after:
- [ ] All active participants who want a wallet have one
- [ ] You've tested submitting to OREC on a test wallet
- [ ] You have a fallback if the on-chain submission fails (off-chain ledger)

### Season boundary (Session 12)

At Session 12, you've completed Season 1. Mark it:

- [ ] Name your Season 1 retroactively (e.g., "The Founding Season")
- [ ] Announce Season 2's theme (what will the community focus on for the next 12 sessions?)
- [ ] Celebrate: share the total Respect distributed, the top contributor, the most-improved
- [ ] Give people the chance to re-opt-in: "Season 2 starts next week. Who's in?"

## Common Failure Modes (and How to Avoid Them)

| Failure | Cause | Fix |
|---------|-------|-----|
| Session 2 attendance drops 50%+ | Session 1 felt clinical, not human | Lead with a celebration of Session 1's top contributor at the start of Session 2 |
| "I don't know what to rank on" | Contribution types aren't clear | Before Session 2, send examples: "contributions include: posts, intros, code, organizing, showing up consistently" |
| Wallet pressure drives people away | Wallets introduced too early | Follow the consent ladder — never pressure before Session 4 |
| "This feels like extra work" | Framing is wrong | Reframe: "This is 45 minutes/week to recognize people who already show up" |
| Attendance plateaus at 4–5 | Not inviting | Add "invite one person" as a standing ask every week |
| Facilitator burnout | One person runs everything | Introduce co-facilitators at Session 4; rotate the "week in review" role |

## Connecting to the ZAO Fractal Network

If your community wants to be part of the ZAO Fractal ecosystem (shared Respect, network effects, co-hosted sessions):

1. Run at least 3 sessions independently first
2. DM Zaal on Farcaster or Telegram with: (a) your community name, (b) your session cadence, (c) your first 3 session summaries
3. ZAO will help you set up OREC on-chain submission, connect to ORDAO, and promote your node

**What being a network node means:**
- Your Respect scores are on the same chain (Optimism) as ZAO's
- Your members can co-participate in ZAO sessions
- ZAO can co-host milestone sessions (Session 10, 25, 50)
- Your community appears in the ZAO Fractal directory
