# 1542 — ZAO Fractal Contribution Rubric

> **Who this is for:** Anyone who wants to rank higher in the Fractal's weekly peer review — and anyone facilitating breakout groups who needs language for what "contribution" means.

---

## The Core Question the Group Is Answering

In every breakout session, your group is answering one question:

> **"Who contributed most to The ZAO this week, relative to each other?"**

Not total volume. Not seniority. Not loudest voice. **Relative contribution** — given what each person did this week, how does it stack up against the others in this group?

---

## What "Contribution" Means (Fractal Definition)

Contribution = anything that advances The ZAO's mission, moves a project forward, or strengthens the community. It is intentionally broad because ZAO is a multi-surface ecosystem. The rubric below gives concrete examples across role types, but it is not exhaustive.

The ranking is peer judgment, not a formula. Groups deliberate and reach consensus — no formula replaces that. This rubric helps calibrate what peers are likely to value.

---

## Contribution by Role Type

### Musician / Artist

| Contribution | Likely impact |
|---|---|
| Performed live at a WaveWarZ, COC Concertz, or ZAOstock event | High — visible, on-chain provable |
| Submitted tracks to ZAO playlists or ZABAL curriculum | Medium-high |
| Onboarded another artist to WaveWarZ or ZAO Fractal | High — direct growth action |
| Mentored a ZABAL learner | Medium-high |
| Promoted ZAO on social (quantified reach) | Medium |
| Attended Fractal sessions + arrived prepared | Medium (baseline) |
| Shared music without measurable community impact | Low-medium |

### Builder / Developer

| Contribution | Likely impact |
|---|---|
| Shipped a PR that merged this week | High — concrete, verifiable |
| Unblocked another member's PR or build | High — multiplier effect |
| Fixed a live bug or incident | High — protective, time-sensitive |
| Wrote a research doc that others reference | Medium-high |
| Reviewed code, tested PRs | Medium |
| Reported a bug with a reproduction path | Medium |
| Started something but didn't ship | Low (mention it but peers will note incomplete) |

### Connector / Community Builder

| Contribution | Likely impact |
|---|---|
| Brought in a new member who attended this week | High — direct growth |
| Made an intro that led to a partnership | High — relationship capital |
| Ran a community call or co-working session | Medium-high |
| Facilitated a prior Fractal session | Medium-high |
| Moderated Discord / caught and resolved a conflict | Medium |
| Showed up consistently and helped new members | Medium (cumulative) |
| Posted in Discord without driving outcomes | Low |

### Researcher / Strategist

| Contribution | Likely impact |
|---|---|
| Wrote a research doc that directly informed a decision | High |
| Synthesized external research into ZAO-applicable findings | Medium-high |
| Produced a grant application or external submission | High |
| Updated canonical facts or docs before they were cited incorrectly | Medium-high |
| Identified a missed opportunity or risk that the team acted on | Medium-high |
| Research that sat unused | Low |

## What Peers Actually Weight (Observed Patterns)

Based on 100+ ZAO Fractal sessions, these factors recur in breakout group deliberations:

**1. Did it ship?** Completed, merged, live > in-progress. Peers can see it, verify it, use it.

**2. Did it multiply?** Work that enabled others to go faster ranks above solo work of equal effort.

**3. Did it show up in public?** A published piece, a public event, a merged PR — verifiable by anyone — gets more trust than claims of invisible effort.

**4. Did it solve something urgent?** Unblocking a release, fixing a live bug, or responding to a time-sensitive external opportunity ranks above steady-state work.

**5. Is it consistent?** Members who show up EVERY week and contribute accumulate trust. Sporadic bursts followed by gaps are ranked lower than steady medium contributions.

**6. Is the claim proportionate?** A member who says "I answered questions in Discord" in a group where another member "merged 3 PRs and ran a community call" will not rank first, regardless of effort.

---

## What Does NOT Rank High

| Pattern | Why it ranks low |
|---|---|
| "I was busy with other things" | ZAO contribution is ZAO-specific |
| Vague effort ("I worked on stuff") | Can't be verified or weighted |
| Attendance alone | Presence is a prerequisite, not a contribution |
| Planning without execution | Plans without shipped artifacts |
| Large effort on abandoned work | Output matters; intentions don't score |
| Social media presence alone | Unless it drove quantifiable ZAO outcomes |

## Tips for New Members (Sessions 1–6)

New members often rank at the bottom of their groups in early sessions — not because their work isn't valued, but because the group doesn't know them yet and their ZAO contribution is still small. This is normal and expected.

To rank higher in your first 6 sessions:

1. **Do one concrete thing for ZAO before the session.** One PR, one doc, one artist intro, one post that tags ZAO. Something you can name and point to.
2. **Be specific in the breakout.** "I submitted a track to the ZABAL curriculum and got feedback from Iman" beats "I was active in music spaces."
3. **Ask follow-up questions during deliberation.** Shows engagement; peers notice.
4. **Accept your rank gracefully.** Members who dispute their ranking or argue for points lose social trust. The game is peer-decided; accept and improve next week.
5. **Come back next week.** Consistency is weighted. Session 2 starts to build the pattern.

---

## In the Breakout: Language for Facilitators

When a group gets stuck on how to weigh contributions:

> "Let's anchor on verifiable impact this week. What did each of us finish, ship, or move forward that The ZAO can point to?"

When someone claims high contribution for invisible work:

> "That sounds meaningful. Can you help us connect the specific action to something the group or community experienced?"

When two members seem equal:

> "If we had to pick one to invite to the next external opportunity as a ZAO representative, which would it be? That's often a useful tiebreaker."

---

## Why There's No Formula

The Respect Game explicitly avoids a point formula because:

1. **Rigid rubrics get gamed.** Members optimize for the rubric, not the mission.
2. **Different phases need different things.** "Most valuable" in Week 2 of a new node (onboarding, trust-building) is different from Week 50 (shipping, external growth).
3. **Peer judgment is the point.** The deliberation IS the governance exercise — the act of reaching consensus is what builds the trust network.

The rubric above calibrates expectations, not replaces judgment.
