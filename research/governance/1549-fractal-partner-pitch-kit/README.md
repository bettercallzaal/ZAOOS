# 1549 — ZAO Fractal External Partnership Pitch Kit

> **Who this is for:** ZAO members (especially connectors and community builders) pitching other communities on starting a Fractal node, joining WaveWarZ Africa Fractal, or partnering on governance coordination.

---

## The Core Pitch (60 Seconds)

> "The ZAO runs the longest unbroken Respect Game DAO in Web3 — 100+ consecutive weekly sessions without a single miss. Every Monday, our members rank each other's contributions. The highest contributors earn ZOR tokens that give them voting weight in our on-chain governance. No crypto knowledge required to start. No capital required to participate. We're expanding to WaveWarZ Africa and looking for community leaders who want to be the node anchors there. You'd bring the community; we bring the governance infrastructure, on-chain records, and ZAO network access."

**Use this pitch:** DMs, intro calls, conferences, Telegram/Discord intros.

## The One-Page Pitch (Email / Deck Slide)

**Subject line:** Bring governance to your community (ZAO Fractal node partnership)

---

**What ZAO's Fractal is:**

The ZAO Fractal is a weekly governance session where members rank each other's contributions and earn Respect (ZOR tokens) based on their rankings. It runs on Optimism Mainnet — 100+ consecutive sessions, $0 capital to participate, zero quorum failures in 2+ years.

**What a node partnership means:**

Your community runs its own weekly Fractal session. Your members earn their own ZOR Respect, recorded on Optimism. You stay autonomous — your own weekly call, your own community rules — while gaining access to ZAO's governance infrastructure, cross-community reputation, and WaveWarZ platform integration.

**What you get:**

- On-chain Respect record for every member from day one
- Access to ZAO's governance infrastructure (OREC contract, ZOR token, ORDAO)
- WaveWarZ Africa Fractal integration: your artists can compete in WaveWarZ battles
- ZAO network: connections to other Fractal nodes, ZABAL education program, COC Concertz circuit
- Technical support: ZOE handles session setup, reminders, on-chain submissions

**What we're asking:**

One community leader to be the node anchor. Commit to running or co-running weekly sessions. Bring 10–20 founding members who want to participate in contribution-ranked governance.

**Timeline:** First session in 2 weeks from handshake. Onboarding guide: [Doc 1523]

## Target Community Profile

The ideal partner community for a new Fractal node:

| Criterion | Why it matters |
|-----------|----------------|
| 15–100 active members | Small enough for breakout groups; large enough for meaningful ranking |
| Meets weekly (or could) | Fractal requires weekly sessions — existing weekly touchpoint makes adoption easier |
| Work-focused (not social-only) | Contribution ranking requires real work to rank; social-only communities have nothing to rank |
| Creator/builder orientation | Artists, musicians, developers, community builders — people who produce verifiable output |
| Africa / diaspora connection (priority) | WaveWarZ Africa Fractal is the active expansion pipeline (doc 1474) |
| Leader with skin in the game | Node anchor must want to run this, not just be interested — weekly commitment required |

**Communities that have worked well:** Music communities, artist collectives, creator DAOs, music tech builder groups.

**Communities that don't fit:** Pure token communities (expect plutocratic governance), social clubs (no contribution to rank), communities without weekly meeting culture.

## Common Objections + Responses

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "We don't know crypto / Web3" | You don't need to. The weekly session is a Zoom call. Members discuss contributions and rank each other. ZOE handles the on-chain parts in the background. Your members will learn Web3 by doing, not by studying. |
| "We already have a governance structure" | Fractal doesn't replace your existing governance — it adds a contribution-ranking layer. Many communities use it for resource allocation decisions, not daily ops. |
| "What if nobody shows up?" | The first 6 sessions are hardest. Doc 1523 has a week-by-week script. Typical pattern: 10–15 founding members → 20–30 by week 8. One no-show session is fine; the streak belongs to your node, not ZAO's overall count. |
| "How do ZOR tokens connect to real decisions?" | ZOR holders vote on community battle charity selection, artist lineup for governance-determined shows, and ZAOstock direction. ORDAO is the on-chain voting mechanism. More ZOR = more voting weight. |
| "Is this expensive to run?" | $0 for participants. On-chain transaction costs (~$0.50–$2 per session submission) are handled by ZAO during pilot. |
| "What does ZAO get out of this?" | Network effects: more nodes = more governance data = stronger case for Optimism Retro Funding. Your community benefits from ZAO's infrastructure; ZAO's infrastructure gets stronger from more nodes. Non-zero-sum. |
| "What if we want to leave?" | Your on-chain Respect records are yours — they live on Optimism Mainnet at addresses your members own. The data is portable. ZAO doesn't hold your community's keys. |

## Qualifying a Partner Community (5-Question Check)

Before investing time in an onboarding conversation, ask these 5 questions:

1. **Do you have a weekly meeting you'd be willing to adapt?** *(If not, would you start one?)*
2. **Who is the person who would run the weekly Fractal session?** *(Need a named individual, not "the community")*
3. **How many members would you expect to show up in week 1?** *(Minimum viable: 4 people)*
4. **What kind of work does your community do that would show up as contributions to rank?** *(Music, building, community organizing, education — anything verifiable)*
5. **Are you open to your members creating Optimism wallets to receive ZOR?** *(This is the on-chain piece — doesn't have to happen in week 1 but must happen by week 6)*

**Pass = all 5 answered honestly, minimum answers met. Fail = any hard no.**

## The Onboarding Path After "Yes"

Once a partner says yes:

| Step | Timeline | Who |
|------|----------|-----|
| Confirm node anchor person | Same day | ZAO connector + partner |
| Share doc 1523 (new-node launch checklist) | Day 1 | ZAO connector |
| Schedule session 1 date/time | Day 2–3 | Node anchor |
| ZOE adds node to session reminders | Day 3 | ZOE |

| Session 1 — ZAO member observes + supports | Week 2 | ZAO connector |
| OREC submission for session 1 | Session 1 end | ZOE + ZAO |
| Independent operation | Session 3+ | Node anchor owns |

**First 3 sessions are supported.** Session 4 onward the node is self-sufficient, with ZOE as ongoing support.
