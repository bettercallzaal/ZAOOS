### Opening

We have missed zero sessions.

One hundred weeks of showing up every Thursday, sitting in a Telegram voice channel or Juke room, and playing the Fractal Democracy game. One hundred weeks of recording the results on-chain on Optimism Mainnet. One hundred weeks of ZOR tokens flowing to the people who contributed most.

The DAO world is full of impressive whitepapers. It is not full of DAOs that actually run governance every week for two years straight.

ZAO Fractal is one of them.

---

### Part 1 — What the Fractal Is

Fractal Democracy is a consensus protocol built by the Optimystics team. The mechanics are elegant: participants form small groups of 3-6, each person speaks for 3 minutes about what they contributed since the last session, and groups vote on who demonstrated the most value. Top-voted participants advance to a "Respect table." The whole thing resolves in 60–90 minutes.

What makes it unusual is what it produces: not just a governance score, but a social ritual. Every week, the same group of people shows up to say what they've been building, hear what others have been building, and recognize each other for it.

The on-chain record is the OREC contract: `0xcB05F9254765CA521F7698e61E0A6CA6456Be532` on Optimism Mainnet. Every session is a proposal. Every proposal is a record. 100+ records now.

The token is ZOR — an ERC-1155 soulbound token that accumulates in your wallet each session you attend. You can't buy it. You earn it by showing up and contributing. 157 wallets hold ZOR right now.

### Part 3 — What "100 Weeks" Actually Means

The number that matters isn't 100. It's zero.

Zero missed sessions. Zero quorum failures. Zero weeks where nobody showed up.

That's rare for any organization — let alone a decentralized one with no employees, no office, no mandatory attendance, and no cash payments for showing up. People come to ZAO Fractal sessions because they want to.

Here's what 100 weeks of showing up built:

- **157 ZOR holders** across 3 countries
- **100+ on-chain OREC proposals** recorded on Optimism Mainnet
- **2 scoring eras**: the OG era (sessions 1–73, ERC-20 Respect token) and the ORDAO era (session 74+, ZOR ERC-1155)
- **8 named seasons** of governance, retroactively mapped
- **1 WaveWarZ MAIN battle** now tied directly to Fractal Democracy (artists are nominated by ZOR holders)
- **1 Africa expansion** in progress: WaveWarZ Africa node targeting its first governance cohort in 2026

The streak is cited in our Optimism Retro Funding Round 7 application as evidence of sustainable on-chain governance. It's referenced in our DAOstar registry submission as our primary DAO credential. It's what makes ZAO Fractal a case study rather than a project.

### Part 4 — The Real Metric: Belonging

We track sessions, OREC submissions, ZOR balances. Those matter.

But the metric that explains why the streak exists is one you can't put on-chain: belonging.

Every Thursday, a group of people shows up who feel like they are building something together. The Fractal is the ritual that holds that feeling. You can miss three weeks and come back and your place is still there. You can be a music artist, a developer, a photographer, a connector — contribution comes in every form. No one gets excluded for not having code commits or token balances.

That's rare in crypto. Most governance systems are designed for people who already understand governance. The ZAO Fractal is designed for people who want to belong to something.

[See doc 1577 for a full analysis of the belonging-first design principles. This isn't accidental — every structural choice in the Fractal, from the 3-minute speaking format to the tiered ZOR earning curve, was made to lower the barrier to feeling at home.]
