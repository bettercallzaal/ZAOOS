# 1640 — ZAO Fractal Pilot Program: Framework for Partner Communities

> **Who this is for:** Community builders who want to run Fractal Democracy governance for their own group and connect to the ZAO ecosystem. Also useful for Zaal when evaluating partnership requests for Fractal expansion.

---

## What a Fractal Pilot Is

A Fractal Pilot is a 12-week test run of ZAO's Fractal Democracy governance model inside a partner community. At the end of 12 weeks (one season), the pilot community decides whether to continue as a sustained Fractal node or conclude.

**What a Fractal Pilot is:**
- A weekly governance session using the ORDAO/Optimystics protocol
- On-chain using ZAO's existing OREC contract and ZOR token infrastructure
- Facilitated by ZAO (weeks 1-4) and then independently (weeks 5-12)
- Free to run (no cost to pilot communities)
- Optional: the pilot community can continue as a ZAO ecosystem partner indefinitely

**What a Fractal Pilot is NOT:**
- A fork of ZAO's brand or platform (you don't need to call it "ZAO Fractal")
- A requirement to hold ZOR tokens for your own community (ZOR is ZAO's token; your pilot can use its own token or just governance weight)
- A commitment to join the ZAO ecosystem permanently
- A code project — no development required from the partner community

---

## Why ZAO Offers This

ZAO's 100+ consecutive weeks of Fractal Democracy prove the model works. The research library has 1,600+ docs about how it works, why it works, and how to extend it. The value of proving it works at ONE more node is less than the value of ZAO being the organization that TEACHES Fractal governance to partner communities.

A ZAO-piloted partner community:
- Expands ZAO's governance network (more OREC submissions = stronger OP RF evidence)
- Creates a pipeline for ZOR token holders from new communities
- Proves "ZAO runs the Fractal model" across cultures and geographies
- Builds the case for ZAO as a governance-as-a-service entity (future revenue)

## Pilot Phases

### Phase 0: Pre-Pilot Qualification (2 weeks)

**Who runs it:** Zaal + community facilitator  
**Goal:** Confirm the community is ready and a good fit

**Qualification criteria:**
- [ ] The community has a consistent weekly gathering of 6-12 people (in-person or online)
- [ ] The community has a shared purpose (music, art, tech, civic, athletics, etc.)
- [ ] At least one person is willing to be the community facilitator
- [ ] The community can commit 60-90 min per week for 12 weeks
- [ ] The community leader has met with Zaal at least once (intro call)

**Disqualifying factors:**
- Community exists primarily as a speculative token project (the governance must come before the token)
- No shared purpose beyond "DAO"
- Fewer than 6 committed participants available weekly

**Deliverable:** Signed/agreed pilot agreement (informal — a Telegram message from community leader saying "we're in" is sufficient)

---

### Phase 1: Foundation (Weeks 1-4)

**Who runs it:** Zaal facilitates the first 2 sessions, community facilitator takes over weeks 3-4  
**Goal:** Get the game format into muscle memory; establish baseline

**Week-by-week:**

| Week | What happens |
|---|---|
| Week 1 (Session 1) | Zaal or ZAO facilitator runs the session. Explain Fractal Democracy, form groups, play one full round. Q&A at end. |
| Week 2 (Session 2) | Zaal co-facilitates. Community facilitator handles group formation and timing. |
| Week 3 (Session 3) | Community facilitator runs solo. Zaal available on Telegram if questions arise. |
| Week 4 (Session 4) | Community facilitator runs solo. First session with contribution log submitted in advance (doc 1561 format). |

**ZAO provides:**
- New Member Welcome Packet (doc 1573) — send to all participants before Session 1
- Session Guide (doc 1475) — facilitator reference
- Facilitator Guide (doc 1565) — facilitator preparation
- Pre-session log template (doc 1561) — members submit contributions in advance
- ZOR distribution for attendees (if Zaal submits to OREC on their behalf)

**ZAO does NOT provide:**
- A Telegram group (use the community's existing channel)
- A Juke account (use Telegram voice or any voice channel)

---

### Phase 2: Independence (Weeks 5-10)

**Who runs it:** Community facilitator runs all sessions  
**Goal:** Sustainable weekly cadence, community owns the game

**Zaal's role during Phase 2:**
- Weekly check-in message (Telegram) with the facilitator: "How'd it go? Any issues?"
- Answer specific questions but does NOT attend sessions
- Submits to OREC on ZAO's behalf IF the pilot community wants on-chain recording
- Not required: the pilot can run off-chain (no OREC submission) and still be a valid pilot

**Milestones to hit by end of Phase 2:**
- [ ] 6+ participants attending consistently (not just the first-timers)
- [ ] Facilitator comfortable with the 60-minute format
- [ ] Community has discussed at least one real governance question (not just the game mechanics)

---

### Phase 3: Graduation Decision (Weeks 11-12)

**Who runs it:** Community facilitator + Zaal (one joint call, week 11)  
**Goal:** Decide: (a) continue as a ZAO ecosystem partner, (b) continue independently, or (c) conclude

**Joint call agenda:**
1. What worked? What didn't?
2. Did the game produce real governance decisions? (Or just attendance?)
3. Does the community want to continue?
4. If yes: ZAO ecosystem partnership (ZOR integration, listed as ZAO node, co-promotion)
5. If independent continuation: ZAO shares all open-source resources, no obligation
6. If concluding: debrief, write a short "lessons learned" doc for ZAO research library

**Deliverable:** Season 1 recap (12 sessions, attendance, governance decisions made, next steps)

---

## What "ZAO Ecosystem Partner" Means (Post-Graduation)

If a pilot community chooses to continue as a ZAO partner:

| ZAO provides | Partner community provides |
|---|---|
| ZOR token integration (participants earn ZOR by attending) | Weekly sessions, self-facilitated |
| OREC on-chain submission after each session | Session attendance list + scores |
| Listed on ZAO's node map (doc 1474 and future site) | Monthly update to Zaal on attendance/health |
| Access to ZAO research library and all facilitator resources | Co-promotion of ZAO events (seasonal) |
| Invitation to seasonal ZAO gatherings (ZAOstock, etc.) | Invitation to Zaal for one guest session per season |

The ZAO ecosystem partnership is perpetual and can be ended by either party at any time.

## How to Apply

To apply for a Fractal Pilot:
1. DM Zaal on Telegram or email zaal@thezao.com
2. Subject: "Fractal Pilot — [Your community name]"
3. Include: community name, size, weekly meeting format, your role

Zaal will respond within 48 hours with a qualification call invite or more questions.

**What makes a great pilot application:**
- Already meeting weekly (any format)
- Led by someone with facilitator experience (or willingness to learn)
- Community has a clear shared purpose
- Leader can commit 12 weeks personally
