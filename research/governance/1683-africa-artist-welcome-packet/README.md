# 1683 — Welcome to ZAO Fractal: A Guide for WaveWarZ Africa Artists

> **This is your welcome packet.** You've been invited to join ZAO Fractal for Season 9 — the WaveWarZ Africa season. Here's everything you need to know before your first session.

---

## What You Just Said Yes To

You're joining a governance community that has run every single week for 100+ consecutive weeks. Not once has a session been cancelled, postponed, or skipped. That record exists because the people inside it find it genuinely worth showing up for.

Season 9 (August–November 2026) is the first ZAO Fractal season built around WaveWarZ Africa. You're not late arrivals — you're the reason this season exists.

---

## The 60-Second Explanation

**ZAO Fractal is a weekly meeting where contribution is scored — but not by a boss.** It's scored by your peers.

Every session: you join a group of 6. Each person ranks everyone else in their group based on one question: "Who contributed most to the ZAO ecosystem this week?" Then the rankings aggregate into a score called **Respect**. The more you show up and contribute, the more Respect you earn. Respect is recorded on the Optimism blockchain as **ZOR tokens**.

ZOR is not a cryptocurrency you buy. It's a record of your contribution. And it gives you a voice in decisions that affect the community.

---

## Why This Matters for You as an Africa WaveWarZ Artist

**Your battles count as contribution.** When you battle in WaveWarZ, you're contributing to the ZAO music ecosystem. Sessions 97-108 (Season 9) are built around the WaveWarZ Africa moment — your battles, your music, and your audience are what makes this season run.

**Your vote matters.** ZOR holders vote on fund allocation. The Africa Battle Week charity vote (Jul 24-25, 2026) already let 157 ZOR holders decide which African music charity gets 100% of a battle's proceeds. As Season 9 participants, you'll earn ZOR and have that same voice in future votes.

**Your network grows.** ZAO Fractal participants include producers, DJs, music executives, technologists, and artists from across the US and beyond. Sessions are a weekly peer network meeting, not just a governance exercise.

---

## What a Session Looks Like (Your First Six Sessions)

**Session 1:** You show up, observe, and introduce yourself. No ranking pressure. Watch how the breakout groups work. You'll be in a group of 6; each person shares what they contributed to the ZAO ecosystem in the past week, then the group ranks each other's contribution in a poll.

**Sessions 2-3:** You start contributing — a WaveWarZ clip, a social post about ZAO, a connection you made, feedback on something. Anything that advanced ZAO's goals counts.

**Sessions 4-6:** Your contributions accumulate. By session 6, you'll have enough Respect to understand what ZOR gives you. At session 5-6, you'll typically receive your first ZOR.

**After session 6:** You're a ZAO Fractal participant. ZOR unlocks governance voting, proposal submission, ZAOstock participation (IRL + remote), and recognition as a ZAO contributor.

---

## The Three Things to Know About ZOR

1. **You earn ZOR; you don't buy it.** Contribution in sessions = ZOR. No shortcut.
2. **ZOR is on-chain.** Every token is recorded on Optimism Mainnet. It's a permanent, verifiable record of your contribution to ZAO.
3. **ZOR = voice.** Governance votes, charity allocation, proposal voting — all ZOR-gated. Your participation in sessions is how you get a vote in the community's decisions.

## Questions You're Probably Asking

**"Do I have to attend every week?"**
No. But ZOR is earned by attending. Miss a session = no ZOR for that week. The streak record (100+ weeks) is ZAO's — not a requirement on you. Show up when you can; contribute when you're there.

**"Is there any money involved?"**
ZOR itself has no guaranteed monetary value. The Africa Battle Week charity vote directed SOL to a charity — that SOL came from WaveWarZ battle settlements, not from ZOR itself. ZAO is exploring funded contribution requests for Season 10 (sessions where completing specific work earns USDC), but that's Season 10.

**"What language are sessions in?"**
English. Breakout groups are open to any language within the group if all members agree, but the main session runs in English. We're working on multilingual facilitator options for Season 9.

**"What if I already have a wallet but I'm not sure it's on Optimism?"**
DM Zaal — he'll verify and help you bridge if needed. Do not send a wallet address on the wrong network; ZOR on Ethereum mainnet cannot be transferred to Optimism.

**"What does 'contributing to ZAO' mean for an Africa artist?"**
Anything that advances ZAO's goals: a WaveWarZ battle, a post about ZAO or the Fractal to your audience, recruiting another Africa artist, creating music content for ZAO channels, giving feedback on ZAO products, attending ZAO events (even remote). If you did something that made ZAO better or bigger, it counts.
