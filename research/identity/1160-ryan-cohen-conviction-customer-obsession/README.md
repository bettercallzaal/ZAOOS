## 1. Grounded Facts

**Chewy (2011-2018):**
- Ryan Cohen co-founded Chewy (originally "MrChewy") in 2011 at age 25 alongside a co-founder he met in an online chat room discussing website design.
- Initial concept: online jewelry store. Pivoted to pet supplies after an epiphany about the category — larger market, intense customer loyalty, local pet store experience not yet replicated online.
- Bootstrapped for 2 years; rejected by 100+ VC firms before raising capital.
- **April 2017:** PetSmart acquired Chewy for **$3.35 billion** — the largest e-commerce acquisition in history at the time.
- Post-acquisition: Cohen remained CEO, operated Chewy as an independent unit.
- **2018:** $3.5 billion revenue; 66% of sales from customers on auto-replenishment. Cohen stepped down as CEO in March 2018.
- **June 2019:** Chewy IPO at $8.7 billion valuation — 2.6x the 2017 purchase price. Chewy eventually became more valuable than its parent company.

**GameStop (2020-present):**
- **August 2020:** RC Ventures LLC disclosed ~9% stake in GameStop — Cohen became largest individual shareholder.
- **November 2020:** Public letter to GameStop board urging strategic review and digital transformation.
- **June 2021:** Elected chairman of the board at Annual Shareholder Meeting.
- **Current role:** Chairman, CEO, and President — **volunteering at zero salary**; compensation is pure stock ownership.

**The GameStop Investment Thesis (2020):**
- Physical stores survive only if excellent; Cohen saw opportunity in digital transformation of gaming retail.
- Gaming community = passionate, identity-connected enthusiasts who pay a premium for culture and community, not just product.
- Strategic bet: online-first model serving gaming culture (collectibles, trading cards, merchandise, social connection) against Amazon's operational efficiency model.
- Competitive positioning: niche differentiation through relationships > operational efficiency.

## 2. Customer Obsession at Chewy: The Specifics

Chewy didn't win on price or logistics (Amazon has both). It won on **emotional connection**. This is the model:

**Handwritten cards at scale:**
- Hundreds of staff card-writers; 5 million handwritten holiday cards sent annually.
- Welcome notes for new customers, sympathy cards when pets pass away.
- Philosophy: bring the human element to e-commerce; optimize for lifetime relationship, not single transaction.

**Custom pet portraits:**
- Customers send photos of their pets; Chewy hires artists to create personalized oil paintings.
- Mailed to customers at no charge, as unprompted surprises.

**24/7 US-based customer service:**
- Answer any call within 6 seconds, 24/7.
- Expertise on specific topics (gluten-free pet diets, specific toothpaste brands, etc.).
- If a customer needed same-day delivery that Chewy couldn't fulfill: Chewy told them to buy locally and **reimbursed them**.

**The framing:**
Cohen reframed customers as "pet parents" — emotionally investing the relationship before a transaction ever happened.

**Competitive moat vs. Amazon:**
- Amazon model: operational efficiency, scale, logistics, price.
- Chewy model: emotional connection, community, personalization, owner mentality, customer lifetime value.
- Cohen succeeded by NOT competing directly. Niche focus + relational depth > operational optimization.

## 4. Operating Playbook (6 Principles)

| # | Principle | Evidence |
|---|-----------|----------|
| 1 | **Extreme customer obsession** | 5M handwritten cards, custom pet portraits, reimbursing competitors' same-day orders. Optimize for lifetime relationship, not transaction. |
| 2 | **Conviction investing — go all in** | ~9% concentrated position in GameStop. Rejected by 100+ VCs; bootstrapped Chewy anyway. No diversification when the thesis is clear. |
| 3 | **Skin in the game, zero salary** | GameStop role: chairman/CEO/president at zero salary. Compensation = pure stock performance. If shareholders lose, he loses. |
| 4 | **Strategic silence + action over words** | No earnings guidance, no public strategy telegraphing, no lofty promises. "Judge us by our actions." Gives competition zero signal. |
| 5 | **Long-term over quarterly** | Reinvested Chewy's profits into growth for years before profitability. GameStop: 2-year transformation, not 90-day turnaround. Capital discipline over short-termism. |
| 6 | **Niche differentiation > scale optimization** | Chewy vs. Amazon: relationships, not logistics. GameStop vs. Amazon: gaming culture, not product fulfillment. Win a market others don't care to win. |

**The meta-principle:**
Cohen's differentiation from traditional PE/activist investing: he operates like an owner-founder, not a fee-collecting institutional investor. He puts his own money in, takes no salary, and executes quietly. The risk alignment is real.

## 6. The ZAO Map

How Cohen's principles translate to The ZAO / ZABAL / Sparkz:

| Cohen Principle | ZAO Application |
|-----------------|-----------------|
| **Extreme customer obsession** | The ZAO member experience is the product. Every member should feel like Chewy's "pet parents" — seen, cared for, valued beyond the transaction. ZOE's personal DMs, the Fractal's 4-minute speaking equity, the handwritten-style community touches. |
| **Conviction + concentrated bets** | The ZAO bets are: Sparkz (music token readiness), ZAOstock (creator festivals), ZABAL Games (web3 gaming). Each deserves full execution, not half-measures. Don't diversify before proving the core. |
| **Skin in the game** | Zaal takes no VC salary model. ORDAO Respect tokens: contributors earn influence proportional to work, not capital invested. The token is the skin-in-the-game mechanism for the whole community. |
| **Strategic silence** | Build in public but don't telegraph the roadmap to competitors. Ship demos and PRs; announce when live, not when planned. |
| **Long-term** | The ZAO's 188 members are not a growth metric — they're year-1 of a 10-year community. Sparkz is year-1 of artist economics that compounds. |
| **Niche differentiation** | ZAO doesn't try to be Spotify, Sound.xyz, or Twitter for music. It's the artist community for serious music creators who want economic ownership. The narrower the niche, the deeper the loyalty. |

**The core lesson:**
Cohen proved that **obsessive care at small scale beats efficient service at large scale**. Chewy didn't beat Amazon. It served a niche (pet parents) so obsessively that it became irreplaceable. The ZAO's 188 members need to feel that level of care — that's the moat before the scale.
