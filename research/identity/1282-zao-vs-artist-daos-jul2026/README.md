## The Landscape: 5 Categories of Artist DAOs

Music and creator DAOs fall into roughly five models:

| Model | What it is | Examples |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| **Social token DAO** | Members buy a token to access a community | Friends With Benefits ($FWB) |
| **Label DAO** | Collective that signs artists, shares revenue | Songcamp, Mirror DAO |
| **Platform DAO** | Builds a product owned/governed by community | Audius ($AUDIO), Sound.xyz |
| **IP licensing DAO** | Collectively owns IP and licenses it | Royal, Decent |
| **Builder DAO** | Runs governance + product incubation | The ZAO |

The ZAO is closest to the "Builder DAO" model — it runs governance AND ships a live product (WaveWarZ) — but it's also distinct from pure builder DAOs because its members are predominantly artists, not just developers.

## Peer Comparison

### Friends With Benefits (FWB)

**What it is:** The largest social token DAO. Members purchase $FWB tokens to access a gated community of artists, writers, technologists, and cultural figures.

**Model:** Token-gated community. Revenue from token sales + events (FWB Fest).

**Size (approximate):** ~3,000-5,000 token holders at peak (2021); significantly contracted since bear market 2022-23.

**Governance:** Token-weighted voting via Snapshot.

**Key difference from ZAO:**
- FWB requires financial entry (purchasing $FWB tokens, historically $75-1,000+)
- ZAO requires contribution entry (attend Fractal sessions, earn Respect)
- FWB governance is token-weighted (wealth = votes); ZAO governance is Respect-weighted (peer-ranked contribution = votes)
- FWB has contracted significantly since the bear market; ZAO has grown continuously
- FWB built a social space; ZAO built a music product (WaveWarZ) AND a social governance system

**Verified ZAO advantage:** The ZAO's governance record (100+ consecutive weeks, 63 on-chain weeks) is verifiable. FWB's governance record does not have an equivalent on-chain track record.

---

### Songcamp

**What it is:** A series of web3 music production "camps" where musicians, artists, and technologists collaborate to create music albums and release them onchain.

**Model:** Project-based cohorts. Each camp runs for a defined period, produces music, and releases as an NFT album.

**Notable projects:** Chaos (Camp 2), Elektra (Camp 3), etc.

**Governance:** Camp-specific, not a continuous ongoing DAO governance system.

**Key difference from ZAO:**
- Songcamp is project-cohort-based: camps start, run, end. The ZAO is continuous (100+ weeks without break).
- Songcamp focuses on creation (making music together); The ZAO focuses on economic enablement (helping artists own and trade their IP onchain via WaveWarZ)
- Songcamp does not have a live revenue-generating product with onchain settlement; WaveWarZ does
- The ZAO has verified artist payouts ($9.07 SOL directly to artists, onchain); Songcamp distributes NFT sale proceeds to participants

---

### Water & Music

**What it is:** A research DAO focused on the intersection of music and technology. Publishes research reports, hosts community discussions, and educates the music industry about Web3.

**Model:** Membership DAO (NFT-gated access to research and community). Revenue from NFT sales.

**Key difference from ZAO:**
- Water & Music is a RESEARCH organization; The ZAO is a BUILDER organization
- Water & Music analyzes what other platforms are doing; The ZAO builds WaveWarZ and operates it
- Water & Music does not have governance that generates on-chain tokens
- The ZAO's community research (docs 1260, 1214, 1079) is internal knowledge generation for product development, not the primary product

The ZAO is more accurately compared to the organizations Water & Music WRITES ABOUT than to Water & Music itself.

---

### Audius ($AUDIO)

**What it is:** A decentralized music streaming protocol with its own Layer 1 chain. Artists upload music, listeners stream, and the $AUDIO token governs the protocol.

**Model:** Platform DAO. Token holders vote on protocol governance.

**Size:** Millions of registered users (streaming platform scale).

**Key difference from ZAO:**
- Audius is a platform play; The ZAO is a community play
- Audius has millions of passive listeners; The ZAO has 188 active contributors
- Audius governance is theoretical (large passive token holder base, low engagement); ZAO governance is operational (100+ consecutive weekly sessions, peer-ranked)
- WaveWarZ USES Audius as a source for artist music — the two are complementary

### Eden Fractal

**What it is:** The originating organization of the Fractal Democracy governance model that The ZAO adopted. Ran weekly fractal sessions on the EOS blockchain.

**Current status:** INACTIVE as of 2026. SSL certificate expired; site returning 502 errors (verified July 2026).

**Significance:** The ZAO is now the most active Fractal Democracy organization globally. With Eden Fractal inactive, The ZAO carries the model forward — on Optimism, with a live product.

**Key difference from ZAO:**
- Eden Fractal was governance-only; The ZAO couples governance with a product (WaveWarZ)
- Eden Fractal ran on EOS; The ZAO runs on Optimism mainnet (more widely accessible, higher trust)
- Eden Fractal is gone; The ZAO is still running

## What The ZAO Is NOT

To be honest about comparisons:

- **Not at FWB's cultural reach** — FWB had broader cultural press coverage at its peak; The ZAO is less known outside Web3 music
- **Not at Audius's scale** — Audius has millions of users; The ZAO has 188 active members
- **Not a research organization** — Unlike Water & Music, The ZAO does not publish research for the music industry; it builds for artists
- **Not yet institutionally funded** — FWB received VC investment; The ZAO has not taken VC money

The ZAO's differentiation is not in scale — it's in the combination of verified governance + live product + onchain artist economics + continuous operation.
