## Part 2: The Maine Angle (What Makes ZAOstock a Maine Story)

Maine local press does NOT care about blockchain, DAOs, or Web3. They care about:
- Local events that bring people to the community (economic impact)
- Local artists and musicians being highlighted
- Local charities receiving donations
- Unique/unusual stories from the community

ZAOstock has all four. The pitch is:

**The Maine story (not the Web3 story):**
> "Ellsworth is hosting its first community music festival on October 3rd, organized by The ZAO (ZTalent Artist Organization). Eight independent artists — selected through an online competition with hundreds of votes — will perform at [venue]. Proceeds from ticket sales will benefit [charity name]. The event is expected to draw 200-300 local attendees plus virtual viewers from across the country via livestream."

**Do NOT lead with:** blockchain, DAOs, Solana, NFTs, governance tokens, WaveWarZ "loser-earns mechanic"

**DO lead with:** local event, 8 artists, local charity, Ellsworth ME, 200+ expected attendees

**Mention Web3 only if asked:** "The artist selection process used an online platform called WaveWarZ, where fans voted on head-to-head song competitions. The 8 artists with the most votes over 12 months earned their ZAOstock slot." This is understandable without any crypto knowledge.
