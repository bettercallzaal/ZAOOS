## Why Green Pill

Green Pill is Kevin Owocki's podcast on crypto for public goods, regenerative finance, and impact projects. ~50K listeners. Audience: Gitcoin ecosystem, Optimism grant recipients, regen DAO founders, impact-oriented builders. This is the only major crypto podcast that explicitly frames Web3 as a mechanism for public good — which is precisely ZAO's governance and WaveWarZ's economics angle.

**What makes ZAO a Green Pill episode:**

1. **Redistributive economics.** WaveWarZ pays artists for LOSING. The loser gets 1.73% of the battle's buy-side SOL pool — a structural income floor for competing musicians. This is a public goods framing: the platform funds participants who would earn $0 on Spotify. Green Pill audience immediately understands "redistribution" and "floor income."

2. **DAO-run music.** ZAO has run 63+ consecutive Fractal/Respect-based governance sessions without token plutocracy. No one can buy influence with capital alone; governance weight comes from "showed up and contributed." This directly addresses Owocki's critique of token-weighted voting.

3. **ZAOstock = public goods IRL.** A music festival run by a DAO, with a charity component, held in a rural Maine community. The festival's proceeds include a donation to [charity] (doc 1384). "ZAO runs the first public-goods music festival governed by a DAO" — this is a Green Pill episode premise, not a sidebar.

4. **Optimism ecosystem.** ZAO's governance contracts live on Optimism Mainnet. ZAO applied (or plans to apply) for Optimism Retro Funding (doc 1311). Green Pill has a strong Optimism/OP connection via Owocki's relationship with the Optimism Foundation.
