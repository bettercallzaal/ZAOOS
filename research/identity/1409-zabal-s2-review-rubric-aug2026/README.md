## Purpose

ZABAL S2 applications open Aug 1. Zaal reviews applications Aug 22-29 and sends acceptance/rejection emails by Sep 1 kickoff (doc 1392 timeline).

This document tells Zaal HOW to evaluate applications — what makes a strong builder, what makes a strong musician, what red flags to look for, and how to break ties when slots are full.

**Key constraint:** 10 Builder spots + 10 Musician spots = 20 total. Two fixed cohort sizes. No overflow waitlist unless Zaal explicitly creates one.

## Part 1: Reviewer Setup (Do Before Aug 22)

1. Export all applications from the form platform (Google Forms CSV or equivalent)
2. Create a review spreadsheet with columns: Name, X Handle, Track (Builder/Musician), Score A, Score B, Score C, Score D, Score E, Total Score, Red Flags, Decision (Accept/Reject/Waitlist), Notes
3. Review all applications twice — once skimming for red flags, once scoring seriously
4. Make final decisions on Aug 29. Send acceptances by Aug 31, rejections by Sep 1.

**Time budget:** Block 3-4 hours for Aug 22-24 (first pass). Block 2-3 hours for Aug 27-29 (final decisions). Total: ~6-7 hours.

## Part 2: Builder Track Scoring Rubric (10 spots)

Each criterion scored 1-5. Max total: 25 points.

### Criterion B1 — GitHub Quality (5 pts)

**5:** Active GitHub (5+ repos, recent commits, clear project history). Can see what they've built.  
**4:** Moderate GitHub (2-4 repos, some commits, one notable project visible)  
**3:** Light GitHub (1-2 repos, mostly forks, or repos are private — linked but can't confirm)  
**2:** GitHub link dead or empty (no public repos). BUT they explained a reasonable why.  
**1:** No GitHub provided, or link is broken with no explanation

**Key:** Look for COMMIT HISTORY, not just repo count. Builders who commit frequently > builders with one big repo they haven't touched in 6 months.

---

### Criterion B2 — Problem Clarity (5 pts)

Question: "What problem are you trying to solve, and who has this problem?"

**5:** Specific problem, specific audience, concrete pain point. Shows they've talked to real people with this problem. Example: "Independent music producers on Audius can't see which of their tracks performs best in battle formats. There are 30K+ Audius artists who could use this."  
**4:** Clear problem, somewhat specific audience. Missing proof that they've talked to anyone, but the reasoning is sound.  
**3:** Vague problem ("music discovery is broken") or very generic audience ("musicians"). The what is there but the who is underdeveloped.  
**2:** The "problem" is really a feature wishlist, not a problem someone has.  
**1:** No real problem identified. Wants to build something because it sounds cool.

---

### Criterion B3 — Season 2 Goal (5 pts)

Question: "What do you want to ship by November 30?"

**5:** Specific, scoped, shippable goal. "A working WaveWarZ analytics dashboard that shows per-artist battle performance, publicly deployed." Not vague. Has a definition of done.  
**4:** Mostly specific. Clear output, slightly broad scope. "Build a tool to help artists optimize their WaveWarZ strategy" — close, just needs more specificity.  
**3:** Ambitious but unscoped. "Build a full WaveWarZ data platform" in 3 months = not realistic. OR very conservative scope that feels like they could do it alone without ZABAL.  
**2:** No clear goal. "Explore what I can build." Not a goal.  
**1:** Goal is outside WaveWarZ/ZAO ecosystem entirely.

---

### Criterion B4 — ZAO/WaveWarZ Fit (5 pts)

**5:** Has used WaveWarZ or COC Concertz. Has been in ZAO Fractal sessions. Understands the ecosystem from the inside. Can cite something specific about ZAO.  
**4:** Deep understanding of WaveWarZ mechanics even if not yet a user. Has researched ZAOOS. Shows genuine interest in the ZAO/WaveWarZ ecosystem specifically.  
**3:** General Web3 builder who is interested in the ZAO pitch but hasn't engaged yet.  
**2:** Mostly interested in the stipend or resume line. Low ecosystem engagement.  
**1:** Applied to the wrong program. Has no connection to ZAO/WaveWarZ/music.

---

### Criterion B5 — Communication Quality (5 pts)

Judge solely by how they wrote the application.

**5:** Clear, specific, free of jargon. Reads like a person who can explain technical work to non-technical people. Good sign for a collaborative DAO environment.  
**4:** Mostly clear. Minor jargon or vague sentences but the substance is there.  
**3:** Hard to follow. Makes you re-read sentences. But you can tell there's something there.  
**2:** Very hard to understand. Either jargon-heavy or vague to the point of opacity.  
**1:** Unreadable or looks like ChatGPT wrote the whole thing with no personal voice.

---

### Builder Track Red Flags (auto-lower score by 2)

- GitHub URL 404s or links to GitHub.com (not a profile)
- "I want to build [X] with AI" where X is not connected to WaveWarZ or ZAO
- Application looks identical to other applications (may be submitting to multiple programs simultaneously with the same text)
- No evidence of having shipped ANYTHING before
- Proposes something that already exists in ZAOOS (check first before flagging)

## Part 3: Musician Track Scoring Rubric (10 spots)

Each criterion scored 1-5. Max total: 25 points.

### Criterion M1 — Music Quality (5 pts)

**5:** Strong original tracks. Genre versatility OR deep mastery of one genre. Music clearly at a level that could compete in WaveWarZ MAIN battles. You listen and think "this person belongs on the COC Concertz stage."  
**4:** Good tracks. Not quite MAIN-event ready yet, but clear potential and distinguishable voice.  
**3:** Decent music. Could pass in quick battles. Needs development to be COC-stage ready.  
**2:** Basic or unfinished tracks. Rough demos where it's hard to tell the vision.  
**1:** No music provided or links are broken.

**Note:** Don't discriminate by genre. Electronic, hip-hop, indie, afrobeat, classical — all valid. Judge on craft within the genre.

---

### Criterion M2 — WaveWarZ History (5 pts)

**5:** Has competed in WaveWarZ. Has a battle record. Can speak to the experience from the inside ("I won 3 battles and my loser earnings helped cover my Audius subscription").  
**4:** Actively follows WaveWarZ. Watched battles. Has Audius profile with tracks already uploadable.  
**3:** Knows about WaveWarZ from research. No direct experience but has done homework.  
**2:** Vague awareness. "I know it's a music battle thing on Solana."  
**1:** No apparent knowledge of WaveWarZ at all.

---

### Criterion M3 — Onchain Presence (5 pts)

**5:** Has a Phantom or Solana wallet. Has used crypto for music before. May have Coinflow experience or have received onchain payments. Shows they understand the mechanics.  
**4:** Has a wallet, maybe hasn't used it much for music. Comfortable with the concept.  
**3:** Has heard of Solana/crypto but no wallet yet. But clearly willing to learn — mentions specific thing they need to learn.  
**2:** No wallet, no knowledge, somewhat skeptical but open. Will need significant onboarding.  
**1:** No wallet, and actively dismissive of crypto component. Risk of dropout when they have to use Phantom.

**Important:** Don't hard-reject for lack of wallet. ZABAL S2 includes onboarding support. But note the onboarding gap in your review.

---

### Criterion M4 — ZAOstock / COC Ambition (5 pts)

**5:** Has clear vision for what they want to perform at ZAOstock and/or COC. Mentions ZABAL → COC → ZAOstock pipeline explicitly. Sees this as a career opportunity, not just a 3-month program.  
**4:** Interested in performing. Open to the COC and ZAOstock path but hasn't thought through specifics.  
**3:** Interested in the program. Performance goal is secondary or not mentioned.  
**2:** Applied for the music education/community aspects, not performance opportunities.  
**1:** Doesn't want to perform publicly. (Not necessarily a rejection — but note that the musician track culminates in a COC performance.)

---

### Criterion M5 — Communication + Authenticity (5 pts)

**5:** Reads like a real musician talking about their music. Personal voice. Can explain what's unique about their sound in one sentence. Would be interesting to interview for ZAOOS.  
**4:** Good personal voice. Clear about their music and goals.  
**3:** Generic application. "I love music and want to grow as an artist." Could be anyone.  
**2:** Very short responses. You can tell they're talented from the music links but they didn't engage with the application seriously.  
**1:** Generic + no music quality. Both the text and the music fail to show what makes this person worth 10 Musician spots.

---

### Musician Track Red Flags (auto-lower score by 2)

- Music links return 404s or go to private SoundCloud
- Music is clearly AI-generated with no human creative input (different from AI-assisted, which is fine)
- Mentions they need ZABAL to "get started making music" — musician track assumes you're already making music
- Adversarial attitudes toward crypto / WaveWarZ in the application text

## Part 4: Decision Matrix

### First pass (Aug 22-24): Categorize all applications

| Category | Criteria | Action |
|----------|----------|--------|
| Clear Yes | Score ≥ 20/25 OR score ≥ 17/25 with no red flags | Tentatively accept |
| Borderline | Score 13-19/25 | Review more carefully in second pass |
| Clear No | Score < 13/25 OR has 2+ red flags | Reject |
| Red Flag | Any application that feels actively suspicious (spam, duplicate, misaligned) | Reject immediately |

### Second pass (Aug 27-29): Fill the 10 slots

**If you have >10 Clear Yeses in a track:**
- Use Criterion B4/M2 (ZAO/WaveWarZ ecosystem engagement) as the tiebreaker
- Second tiebreaker: diversity of skills/genres (avoid 10 builders all building analytics; avoid 10 musicians all doing the same genre)
- Third tiebreaker: interview (if time allows — 15-min Zoom call with 3-5 borderline candidates)

**If you have <10 Clear Yeses in a track:**
- Promote the best Borderlines until you have 10
- Minimum acceptable score for a spot: 13/25
- If you can't fill to 10: reduce cohort size rather than accepting below 13/25 threshold. 8 strong participants > 10 weak ones. Announce the difference in the acceptance email.

### Waitlist (optional)

Create a waitlist of 3-5 per track (next-ranked Borderlines). Send waitlist notification by Sep 1. Activate only if an accepted participant drops out before Sep 7 kickoff.
