## Core Talking Points (Memorize These)

### Opening Hook (30 seconds)
```
"We built a music platform where the artist who loses the battle still gets paid. 
That single decision — loser earns — is the thread that connects everything we do.

In two years: 1,245 battles, $1,820 distributed to losing artists, 63 consecutive 
weekly governance sessions. Zero missed. Not one."
```

### The Loser-Earns Mechanic (explain in 90 seconds)
```
On WaveWarZ, two artists submit original tracks. The community votes on WaveWarZ. 
Traders bet on the outcome with SOL.

When the vote closes, here's what happens:
- Winning side: traders split the losing pool. They win real money.
- Losing artist: gets 1.73% of the losing pool. Not zero. They earn something.

One losing battle appearance = roughly $35 at current volume. 
That's the same as 11,667 Spotify streams.

We're not saying every artist gets rich. We're saying every artist who participates 
has proven something — their music moved people enough to bet on it — and they 
should be compensated for that.
```

### The DAO / Governance Story
```
The ZAO has run 63 consecutive weekly governance sessions on Optimism Mainnet. 
Zero quorum failures. Zero weeks missed.

We don't vote with tokens you buy. We use Fractal Respect — a peer-ranking system 
where you show up, rank your peers' contributions for that week, and earn non-transferable 
ZOR tokens based on the community's assessment.

No plutocracy. No whale votes. The people who show up are the people with power.

That's the boring, important version of what a DAO actually looks like.
```

### Why This Is "Regenerative Music"
```
Kevin Owocki's framing for Green Pill is regenerative finance — using crypto to create 
positive sum outcomes rather than zero sum speculation.

WaveWarZ is regenerative music. Traditional music battles are zero sum: one artist wins, 
one loses, the losing artist gets nothing and is demoralized. Our model is different: 
the competition still happens, the market still decides, but the losing artist extracts 
real value from the event. The musicians don't cannibalize each other's earnings. 
Both walk away with something.

Traders also benefit. Community governance also benefits. It's a multi-player win.

We're proving that you can build a sustainable creative institution using onchain mechanics 
without making it speculation-first.
```

ZAOstock is a free outdoor music festival. No ticket required. And we're running

It's a DAO hosting a public festival. With a WaveWarZ battle. With a live onchain vote.

### The ZAOOS Story (for podcast listeners who are builders)

We've documented everything. 1,460+ documents — all CC-BY, all public, all on GitHub.

## Expected Questions + Prepared Answers

### "How do you sustain this economically?"
```
WaveWarZ takes 3% of every battle pool as a protocol fee. That's roughly $3,100 in 
revenue since launch across 1,245 battles.

That doesn't fund staff. We don't have staff. ZOE, our AI operations agent, handles 
social media, scheduling, and community management. The whole system costs $215-440/month 
to run — and we've documented every line of that budget.

We're applying for arts grants (Fisher Foundation, OP Retro Funding) for ZAOstock specifically. 
Long term: ZAOstock ticket sales, WaveWarZ protocol fees, and sponsorship are the three legs of the stool.
```

### "How is this different from other DAOs?"
```
Most DAOs are governance experiments that lack a clear product. We have a product — 
WaveWarZ — with real volume, real payouts, and real artists.

Most music platforms treat competition as a monoculture — winner takes all. We've 
built something where losing has positive economic value.

And most DAOs stop. We've had 63 consecutive weekly sessions. That consistency IS the proof.
```

### "What's the Optimism connection?"
```
Our governance contracts are on Optimism Mainnet: the ZOR token, the OREC executor, 
the OG Respect token. We're applying for Optimism Retro Funding in Season 7.

The argument for OP: we've built a composable governance pattern — Fractal Respect 
on Optimism — that any other DAO can deploy. The OREC contract is open source. 
We're public infrastructure, not a closed product.
```

### "What would you do with more funding?"
```
Immediately: cover ZAOstock production costs (sound, insurance, artist fees) without 
relying entirely on ticket sales.

Medium term: build the ZABAL program at scale. ZABAL is our 13-week cohort for 
musicians and builders. Season 2 starts September 1. A micro-grant for each 
completing participant changes the incentive structure meaningfully.

Long term: Africa Battle Week (Sep 22-26) is our first international expansion — 
pairing US WaveWarZ artists with West African musicians. That needs artist travel stipends.

We're not trying to raise a round. We're trying to get through October 3 and document 
what a small, consistent, regenerative music DAO actually looks like in the real world.
```

### "What should listeners do right now?"
```
If you make music: try WaveWarZ. One battle. wavewarz.info. Lose on purpose and 
tell me you don't feel differently about losing afterward.

If you build: the ZAOOS is all CC-BY on GitHub. Fork the governance model. 
Improve the loser-earns mechanic. We want it to spread.

If you want to be there in person: ZAOstock is October 3, Ellsworth, Maine. 
Free. Come. There will be a live onchain vote from the audience.
```
