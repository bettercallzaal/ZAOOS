# Steal Like an Artist — 2026-04-01

**North star:** How does ZAO OS become the operating system for a decentralized music label?
**Five pillars:** Artist Development · Distribution · Revenue · Governance · Culture

---

## 1. Vault.fm — Phone Number as Identity, Artist Owns the List

**What it does:** Vault.fm (launched by James Blake's team, became Sound.xyz's spiritual successor in Jan 2025) inverts the platform-fan relationship with one blunt design decision: fans subscribe via phone number, not email. There's no algorithm, no feed, no discovery layer. When an artist drops something — a demo, a vault track, a voice note — subscribers get an SMS. Artist keeps the phone list when they leave. Platform is unreleased-first: the entire premise is music that would otherwise live on a hard drive. Each artist gets a private group chat for subscribers only. Price: $5/month flat per artist.

**Pillar served:** Culture + Distribution

**ZAO adaptation:** ZAO has XMTP for private messaging and Farcaster for public posts, but no direct "artist to superfan" channel. Adapt this as a **ZAO Vault** feature: artists can create a private XMTP group (existing: `src/components/messages/`) for token-gated subscribers. The gate = holding N Respect tokens or a ZAO NFT (existing gating: `src/lib/gates/`). Drop unreleased tracks to that group directly via the music player queue (`src/providers/audio/PlayerProvider.tsx`). Artists retain their subscriber list exportable as a wallet address CSV.

**Related research:** `research/013-chat-messaging/` (XMTP), `research/012-gating/` (token gates)

## 2. Stem — Recoupment Rules Before the Split Waterfall

**What it does:** Stem's most concrete 2025 UX addition is **Recoupment Rules**: a collaborator (manager, producer, investor) who fronted costs creates a rule that auto-deducts a defined dollar amount from streaming revenue *before* the split percentage waterfall fires. The rule is visible to all stakeholders in their dashboards. Every split arrangement also requires individual collaborator sign-off — a "consent gate" — before any payout releases. This is non-skippable. Result: no surprise deductions, no trust disputes, on-chain-style accounting in a Web2 wrapper. (Note: Stem was acquired by Concord in March 2025 for ~$50M+ but continues operating independently.)

**Pillar served:** Revenue + Governance

**ZAO adaptation:** ZAO's governance system (proposals, Respect-weighted voting) is social but doesn't touch money flows. Add **Respect-Weighted Revenue Splits** as a governance proposal outcome: when a ZAO community release is approved and earns streaming revenue, the Supabase proposals table (`src/lib/db/`) stores a `revenue_split` JSON field. A new API route (`/api/revenue/splits/route.ts`) can compute the waterfall: recoup production costs first, then distribute to contributors by Respect token weight. Each contributor must sign off via an XMTP message confirmation before disbursement. This bridges governance and revenue — proposals become executable financial instruments.

**Related research:** `research/029-artist-revenue-ip-rights/` (IP + splits), `research/004-respect-tokens/` (Respect weighting)

## 3. Are.na — Slow Curation as Community Identity

**What it does:** Are.na (no major 2025 rebrand — still deliberately slow, no algorithmic feed) maintains its core pattern: users build "channels" (boards) by saving URLs, images, text, and files. Crucially, anyone can connect *their* block to *your* channel with attribution — creating a distributed web of curated taste. The 2025 thesis that emerges from their continued existence: **human curation velocity is the product**. The slower, more intentional the save, the more signal it carries. Are.na Premium added better API access and private channels but the UX philosophy hasn't changed: friction is a feature.

**Pillar served:** Artist Development + Culture

**ZAO adaptation:** ZAO's social feed (Farcaster casts) is real-time and high-velocity. Add a **ZAO Board** feature — a slow, intentional curation layer. Artists and fans can create named boards (e.g., "Influences for my next EP," "ZAO Community Sounds") and save casts, tracks, links, and images to them. Any member can "connect" a track from the music player to a public board. Boards are indexed in Supabase and surfaced on artist profiles. Implementation: new `src/components/boards/` directory, `src/app/api/boards/` routes, referenced from `src/app/(auth)/social/` and artist profile pages.

**Related research:** `research/003-music-integration/` (music discovery), `research/005-zao-identity/` (artist identity), `research/015-mvp-spec/` (feature prioritization)

**Highest-leverage next build: Revenue (score: 2)**

The governance system is world-class and generates proposals — but proposals don't yet produce money. The single highest-leverage move is wiring governance to revenue: implement Respect-Weighted Revenue Splits (from the Stem pattern above) so that approved proposals become financial instruments, not just social signals. This unlocks the "label" in decentralized music label — the community makes decisions *and* gets paid together.
