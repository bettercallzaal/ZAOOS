## 1. DistroKid — Private Splits with Sequenced Recoupment

**What it does:** DistroKid's Splits feature has a specific design decision worth stealing: **collaborators can only see their own percentage, not anyone else's**. Privacy is the default. On top of this, the Recoupments mechanic lets you designate one collaborator to receive 100% of earnings until a fixed dollar amount is recovered (e.g., the producer who paid $800 for recording gets whole first), then the percentage waterfall fires. Multiple recoupment amounts can be stacked in sequence. No manual tracking. No disputes. The entire flow is set up by the uploader before distribution — a one-time configuration that becomes the financial contract for the release lifecycle.

**Pillar served:** Revenue

**ZAO adaptation:** Yesterday's entry covered Stem's recoupment UX (more governance-focused, requires sign-off consent gates). DistroKid's insight is different: **privacy-by-default on splits**. When ZAO implements `src/lib/revenue/splitEngine.ts`, collaborator dashboards (`src/components/revenue/SplitDashboard.tsx`) should show each member only their own Respect-weighted percentage — not the full waterfall. The uploader/artist sets recoupment amounts before a community release goes live, stored in the `proposals` table's `revenue_split` JSON field. This removes a major trust barrier to collaboration within a community where Respect scores are public.

**Related research:** `research/029-artist-revenue-ip-rights/` (splits + IP), `research/004-respect-tokens/` (Respect weighting)

## 2. Substack — Livestream → AI Auto-Clip → YouTube Shorts (Zero-Touch)

**What it does:** Substack opened livestreaming to all publishers on January 14, 2025. The specific UX mechanic that's worth studying: when a stream ends, Substack's AI automatically generates highlight clips and — if your YouTube account is connected — **the top 2 clips upload directly to YouTube Shorts with no extra action required**. The stream also triggers an immediate email to your entire subscriber list, bypassing algorithmic distribution entirely. Updated in July 2025: desktop support, AI-powered pause removal (dynamic editing), one-tap cross-post to Instagram, TikTok, X, and LinkedIn. The core thesis: creator goes live once, content fans out to 6+ platforms automatically.

**Pillar served:** Distribution + Culture

**ZAO adaptation:** ZAO has Spaces (Stream.io live audio rooms) with recording and transcription already live. The missing piece: **post-stream auto-distribution**. When a ZAO Space ends, the Stream.io webhook (`src/app/api/stream/webhook/route.ts`) receives the recording URL. Add a new step: trigger `src/lib/publish/` to auto-clip the transcript into a highlight cast for Farcaster + short-form posts for Bluesky and X. The artist doesn't touch anything — the Space ends and ZAO distributes. This turns every live session into a cross-platform content event without any extra work from the musician.

**Related research:** `research/027-spaces-audio-video/` (Spaces/Stream.io), `research/023-cross-platform-publishing/` (Farcaster/Bluesky/X pipeline)

## 3. Catalog — On-Chain Shares: Co-Ownership of a Single Edition

**What it does:** Catalog (catalog.works) maintains the single-edition NFT model (one record = one owner at a time) but in 2024 migrated to Base and introduced **Shares**: artists can allocate fractional co-ownership of their record's secondary royalty stream to collaborators *at mint time*. A producer, engineer, or featured artist receives a Share — an ERC-1155 token representing their cut of every future secondary sale, forever, on-chain. The collector owns the record; the collaborators own Shares of its resale economics. No off-chain agreement, no platform middleman. When the collector resells, royalty splits execute at the contract level across all Share holders.

**Pillar served:** Revenue + Artist Development

**ZAO adaptation:** ZAO's governance system approves community releases (Respect threshold + 7-day vote). After approval, the release should mint on Catalog (or a similar ERC-1155 framework) with **ZAO community Shares** automatically allocated to all Respect holders who voted yes — their Respect weight determines their Share percentage. This turns a governance vote into a permanent on-chain revenue stake. Implementation: extend `src/lib/zounz/contracts.ts` to include a Shares contract, add a `mint-with-shares` action to the proposal outcome handler in `src/app/api/governance/proposals/`. This is the most direct path to making ZAO governance financially meaningful.

**Related research:** `research/004-respect-tokens/` (Respect tokens), `research/029-artist-revenue-ip-rights/` (on-chain royalties), `research/031-zounz-dao/` (contracts on Base)

## Decentralized Music Label Gap Analysis

| Pillar | Score (0–10) | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| **Artist Development** | 4 | Profile pages exist. No structured tools: no release pipeline, no collaborator matching, no feedback channels. Catalog Shares point toward a "credit on-chain" primitive. |
| **Distribution** | 4 | Cross-posting to Farcaster/Bluesky/X works for governance. Substack's zero-touch auto-clip pattern shows ZAO Spaces could become a distribution engine at zero extra cost. |
| **Revenue** | 2 | Still the weakest pillar. DistroKid + Catalog patterns both point the same direction: set splits at creation time, execute on-chain, hide individual percentages for trust. |
| **Governance** | 7 | Three-tier system is world-class and live. The gap is that votes don't produce money or on-chain ownership yet. |
| **Culture** | 6 | Music player, binaural beats, radio, ZAO identity — strong. Spaces adds live culture. Post-stream auto-distribution would close the gap further. |

**Highest-leverage next build: Revenue (score: 2) — still the critical gap.**

Two days of research converge on the same conclusion from different angles: governance is strong, culture is strong, but there is no financial infrastructure. The specific highest-leverage move (updated today): combine DistroKid's privacy-default split model + Catalog's on-chain Shares + Stem's consent gates into a single ZAO Revenue Engine. A community release that gets approved through governance should automatically:
1. Set Respect-weighted splits (private per contributor)
2. Mint with on-chain Shares on Base
3. Require each collaborator to sign off before any payout executes

**Three files that unlock this:**
- `src/lib/revenue/splitEngine.ts` — weighted split computation with recoupment and privacy
- `src/app/api/revenue/splits/route.ts` — REST endpoint for split setup + sign-off
- `src/lib/zounz/sharesContract.ts` — Base contract interaction for on-chain Shares minting
