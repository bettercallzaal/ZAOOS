# Steal Like an Artist — 2026-04-03

**North star:** How does ZAO OS become the operating system for a decentralized music label?
**Apps studied:** Sound.xyz · Linear · Mirror.xyz

---

## 1. Sound.xyz — The Death of the Marketplace Feed (2025)

**What they did:** On January 15, 2025, Sound.xyz entered full maintenance mode — no new uploads, no new artist onboarding. The team's replacement, Vault.fm, is a deliberate anti-marketplace: no public feed, no algorithmic discovery, no NFT listings. Fans can only access a Vault artist's content if they have opted in via SMS or email. The entire discovery surface is removed. You don't browse. You subscribe, or you miss it.

**Pillar:** Culture + Distribution

**The lesson:** Sound.xyz ran the NFT music marketplace experiment to completion and concluded the model doesn't work. The future isn't a marketplace — it's a private channel between artist and opted-in fan. The opt-in is the product.

**ZAO adaptation:** ZAO already has gating (Respect token threshold) but the gate is binary: you're in or you're out. Sound's lesson is about *tiers within the in-group*. The adaptation: add a lightweight "inner circle" subscription layer on top of the existing gate. Artists could offer a ZAO subscriber tier — SMS/email or Farcaster DM (XMTP) — for first-access drops: unreleased tracks, stems, early votes. Implementation: extend `src/lib/gates/` with a subscription tier model, add a "Subscribe" action in `src/components/social/` that writes to a `subscriptions` Supabase table, and surface first-access content in the radio queue via `src/lib/music/curationWeight.ts`.

## 2. Linear — Customizable Mobile Bottom Toolbar (2025–2026)

**What they did:** Linear shipped a mobile redesign in October 2025, replacing top-nav with a persistent bottom toolbar rendered with a custom frosted glass material. The tab bar expands beyond iOS's standard 5-item cap to accommodate Linear-specific navigation (Pulse, My Issues, pinned projects). In January 2026 they went further: users can fully reorder toolbar items and pin specific projects, initiatives, or documents directly to the tab bar — making the nav surface personal to each user's workflow.

**Pillar:** Artist Development (tooling/workflow)

**The lesson:** Navigation is workflow. The items you surface in your bottom nav tell users what matters. Making it customizable is a trust signal: "we know power users have different priorities." For a community tool like ZAO, members have wildly different entry points — some live in Radio, some in Governance, some in Spaces.

**ZAO adaptation:** The current nav is fixed (hardcoded in `community.config.ts` under nav pillars). Add user-level nav personalization: let members pin up to 5 nav items from the full pillar list. Store preferences in Supabase (`user_preferences` table, `nav_order` JSON column). Read in `src/middleware.ts` or a layout-level hook. On mobile, render the bottom bar from the user's pinned order rather than the global config. Start small: just allow reordering, no custom external links.

**Related research:** [Doc 014 — Project Structure](../014-project-structure/), [Doc 015 — MVP Spec](../015-mvp-spec/)

## 3. Mirror.xyz — Subscribe to Mint (2025)

**What they did:** Mirror's "Subscribe to Mint" gates NFT minting behind an email subscription. The interaction flow: a reader arrives at a post, sees a "Mint" button, clicks it — and is prompted to subscribe to the creator's publication *before* the mint completes. The subscription is the prerequisite. The resulting NFT is on-chain proof of subscription. One creator used this to convert 330,000+ newsletter subscribers through a single NFT badge tied to an annual review post. Mirror's team has since migrated to Paragraph, but the mechanic carried over.

**Pillar:** Revenue + Culture

**The lesson:** The mint is the call to action, but the subscription is the durable asset. Flipping the order (subscribe → then mint, not mint → then subscribe) dramatically increases subscription conversion because the mint incentive pulls users through the friction. The NFT becomes a badge of being an early subscriber.

**ZAO adaptation:** ZAO's governance proposals can already publish on-chain (via Respect threshold + auto-post to Farcaster/Bluesky/X). The adaptation: when a ZAO community proposal passes its 7-day vote and crosses 1,000 Respect, mint a commemorative "Participated" NFT to every address that voted. Voting becomes proof-of-participation on-chain. Implementation: extend `src/app/api/proposals/` with a post-vote mint trigger, use the existing `src/lib/zounz/contracts.ts` for the NFT layer (or a lightweight ERC-1155 on Base), and add a "You voted — claim your badge" CTA in `src/components/governance/`.

## Decentralized Music Label Gap Analysis

| Pillar | Score | Status |
|--------|-------|--------|
| **Artist Development** | 4/10 | ZAO has identity, radio, and social. Missing: artist dashboard (analytics, streaming stats, fan CRM). Linear's lesson: surfacing the right data for each artist's workflow. |
| **Distribution** | 3/10 | Cross-platform publishing (Farcaster/Bluesky/X) exists. Missing: music distribution pipeline to DSPs (Spotify, Apple Music). Sound.xyz's lesson: the opt-in channel matters more than the public feed. |
| **Revenue** | 2/10 | Respect-weighted curation exists; 0xSplits scaffolded. Missing: actual payment rails — fan-to-artist, stream-to-split, NFT minting. Consistent bottom scorer. |
| **Governance** | 6/10 | Three-tier governance (ZOUNZ on-chain + Snapshot + community proposals) is the strongest pillar. Mirror's lesson: make participation tangible with on-chain proof. |
| **Culture** | 5/10 | Farcaster social, Spaces, binaural beats, governance culture. Missing: exclusive inner circles, artist-to-fan intimacy layer, subscription tiers. |

**Highest-leverage next build:** Revenue (2/10). The Mirror "Subscribe to Mint" adaptation is the lowest-friction entry point: tie voting badges to existing governance flow (no new payment infrastructure needed), then use that NFT infrastructure to bootstrap a proper minting/revenue-split pipeline. The gate already exists — now attach value to passing through it.
