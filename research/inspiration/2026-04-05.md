## 1. Bandcamp — Bandcamp Clubs (September 2025)

**What they did:** Bandcamp launched a curator-led subscription service at $13/month. Each "Club" gives subscribers one record permanently added to their library ("subscribe-to-own"), a live listening party, and an artist interview. Four clubs at launch — jazz, electronic, heavy, global dance — each with a named human curator, no algorithm. The critical design decision: discovery and ownership collapse into a single subscription action. Fans don't stream; they accumulate a curated collection while directly funding artists.

**Pillar:** Artist Development + Revenue

**The lesson:** Human curation as a product. The curator's reputation is the trust signal — no algorithmic intermediary. Each subscription is also a micro-patronage act: the artist gets direct revenue *and* a permanent listener who owns the work.

**ZAO adaptation:** ZAO has 188 members who are active listeners and curators. The pattern maps directly: a ZAO member (high-Respect) can run a "ZAO Club" — a recurring subscription (via Superfluid or 0xSplits stream) that delivers one ZAO artist track per month as a permanent Bandcamp/Catalog hold, plus an exclusive Spaces listening party. Implementation: a `ZaoClub` component in `src/components/music/`, a subscription record in Supabase, and a curator profile in the admin section. Related: [Doc 029 — Artist Revenue & IP Rights](../029-artist-revenue-ip-rights/), [Doc 143 — 0xSplits Revenue Distribution](../143-0xsplits-revenue-distribution/).

## 2. Discord — Video Quests on Mobile (June 2025)

**What they did:** Discord expanded its "Sponsored Quests" rewarded-ad format to mobile — its first mobile ad product. Users see an opt-in Quest bar at the bottom of the screen; tapping plays a full-screen video; dismissing opts out entirely. Rewards are in-game loot or cosmetics. The key design constraint: the ad is only interruptive if the user consciously engages. Non-participating users see nothing beyond the passive Quest bar.

**Pillar:** Revenue + Culture

**The lesson:** Attention as an opt-in currency. Discord solved the monetization-without-ruining-UX problem by making ad engagement a deliberate, rewarded action — not a tax on the experience. The reward must be something the user already wants (cosmetics, loot).

**ZAO adaptation:** ZAO has a natural attention currency: Respect tokens. The pattern: artists can sponsor "ZAO Quests" — e.g., "Listen to 3 minutes of [track] → earn 5 Respect." The sponsor (artist or label partner) pays in USDC; the listener earns Respect; ZAO takes a small protocol fee. This creates a self-sustaining discovery loop: artists pay for attention, listeners earn governance weight. Implementation: a `QuestBar` component in `src/components/music/`, a `quests` table in Supabase, and a payout hook in `src/app/api/quests/`. Related: [Doc 012 — Gating](../012-gating/), [Doc 100 — Governance Architecture](../100-governance/).

## 3. Figma — Figma Buzz (May 2025, Config 2025)

**What they did:** Figma Buzz is a dedicated workspace for brand/marketing teams to produce on-brand assets at scale — social media, campaigns, event materials — using designer-locked templates. Non-designers can edit content within guardrails (text, images) but cannot break brand rules. A built-in approval workflow lets teams request and grant sign-off directly inside Buzz before assets ship.

**Pillar:** Distribution + Culture

**The lesson:** Locked templates + lightweight approvals = brand consistency at community scale. The insight is that the bottleneck in content creation is not creativity — it's guardrails and sign-off. Buzz removes the designer-as-gatekeeper without removing brand coherence.

**ZAO adaptation:** ZAO publishes governance proposals across Farcaster, Bluesky, and X. The current flow has no visual template system — every post is freeform. Buzz's pattern suggests: a set of locked ZAO brand templates (navy + gold, logo placement, typography) for cross-platform posts, with a lightweight "approve before publish" step for admin FIDs. Implementation: extend `src/lib/publish/normalize.ts` with a `templateEngine.ts` that applies brand tokens to post content, add an approval flag to the `posts` Supabase table, and surface a one-click "Approve & Publish" button in `src/app/(auth)/admin/`. Related: [Doc 063 — Cross-Platform Publishing](../063-cross-platform-publishing/).
