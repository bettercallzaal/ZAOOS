## What These Three Have in Common

Nobody would put these together, but all three share the same hidden structure: **behavior leaves a structured residue that unlocks access**.

- **Hindsight** retains facts → builds mental models → forms opinions. Memory compounds. Past interactions shape future responses.
- **Hats Protocol** grants roles based on eligibility logic. Behavior (holding a token, passing a condition) earns a non-transferable hat. Hats can be auto-revoked when conditions no longer hold.
- **WaveWarZ** tracks battle performance — wins, losses, volume staked, weekly momentum. Artists build a verifiable track record over time. That record attracts recruiting attention and fan staking.

The pattern: **time-stamped behavior creates layered credentials that gate access to the next level**.

## The Emergent Feature

### ZAO Artist Credential Oracle

A living, auto-updating credential system for ZAO artists. Behavior accumulates across multiple signals, memory computes reputation, and on-chain roles are granted automatically when thresholds are crossed.

**What it does:**
- Aggregates 4 signals per artist: (1) Respect score, (2) radio play count on ZAO, (3) WaveWarZ W/L record + volume, (4) governance participation
- A Hindsight memory bank (`bank: artist_{fid}`) tracks these signals over time, forming a "Mental Model" of each artist's trajectory
- When a signal combination crosses a threshold, a Hats Protocol role is automatically granted on-chain — no admin action required
- Roles stack: Emerging → Established → Featured → ZAO Core Artist — each with different platform privileges (submission priority, Spaces hosting rights, revenue split weight)

**User story:**
> As an independent hip-hop artist in the ZAO community, I submit tracks, participate in governance votes, and compete in WaveWarZ battles. After 90 days, my Respect score crosses 500, my tracks average 40 plays/week, and I've won 60% of my battles. ZAO OS automatically grants me the "Established Artist" hat — my submissions now appear in the weekly featured queue, I can host Spaces without approval, and my Respect weight in governance increases from 1x to 1.5x. I didn't have to ask anyone.

## Why This Moves ZAO Toward Decentralized Music Label

Traditional labels use internal A&R scorecards. The scores are opaque, subjective, and controlled by a few people. This feature makes the scorecard:
- **Transparent** (on-chain hats, public signals)
- **Automatic** (no gatekeeper, eligibility module handles it)
- **Composable** (any app can read Hats Protocol roles)
- **Evolutionary** (Hindsight memory means the model improves as more data accumulates)

A decentralized music label needs decentralized A&R. This is it.
