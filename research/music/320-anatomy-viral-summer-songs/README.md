## Key Decisions / Recommendations

| Decision | Recommendation |
|----------|----------------|
| **BPM** | USE 115-120 BPM - the scientifically proven sweet spot for summer hits (matches human heart rate during light activity, 117 BPM average for viral TikTok songs) |
| **Key** | USE major key (C major, G major, or D major) - summer hits overwhelmingly use major keys for the uplifting, optimistic feel |
| **Song structure** | USE hook-first structure: chorus or hook within first 15 seconds. TikTok virality depends on a memorable moment in the first 10-20 seconds |
| **Duration** | USE 2:30-3:30 for the main version + a 15-second "TikTok cut" of the chorus. Average viral track is 3 minutes, but the snippet matters more |
| **Lyric themes** | USE anticipation + place-specificity + invitation. "Come to Maine" as the central call. Summer anthems work through anticipation of good times, not just describing them |
| **Production** | USE genre fusion: electronic + indie + hints of lo-fi or house. Genre-blending is the #1 trait of modern summer chart-toppers |
| **Hook construction** | USE an unusual interval leap within a familiar melodic shape (rise then fall). Simple enough to hum, weird enough to stick |
| **Distribution** | USE multi-platform strategy: TikTok 15-second hook clip first, then full track on streaming, then ZAO OS community plays. 84% of Billboard Global 200 entries in 2024 went viral on TikTok first |
| **AI platform** | USE Suno v5 for the vocal version (best AI vocals, lyrics tags), ACE-Step for the raw/indie version, ElevenMusic for voice-cloned version (per Doc 319) |

### The Earworm Formula (APA Research)

The American Psychological Association published research identifying what makes melodies stick:

1. **Faster-than-normal beat** - earworms have higher tempo than average pop songs
2. **Generic overall melodic shape** - similar contour to nursery rhymes (rise then fall)
3. **Unusual intervals within that shape** - unexpected leaps or pitch changes that surprise the brain
4. **Repetition** - the hook repeats 3-4x in the song minimum
5. **Simplicity** - hummable within 2 listens

Examples: "Bad Romance" (Lady Gaga), "Can't Get You Out of My Head" (Kylie Minogue), "Moves Like Jagger" (Maroon 5) - all follow this pattern.

### Why People Play the Same Song All Summer (Psychology)

| Mechanism | How It Works | Application to "Summer of 26" |
|-----------|-------------|-------------------------------|
| **Dopamine hit** | Summer anthems trigger reward pathways; brain associates song with warm weather + freedom | Pair the song with ZAO Stock promo videos, beach imagery, Maine scenery |
| **Mere exposure effect** | Familiarity breeds preference - hate it at first, love it by week 3 | Release 2+ months before ZAO Stock (July-August), let it marinate |
| **Temporal anchoring** | Songs become timestamps for entire seasons of memories | Make it THE soundtrack of the ZAO Stock build-up |
| **Anticipatory emotion** | Summer songs work through anticipation, not just description | Lyrics should build excitement for what's COMING, not what already happened |
| **Nostalgia encoding** | Music memories are the strongest type of memory | First ZAO IRL fest = unforgettable association |

### What They ALL Share

1. **A single unforgettable moment** - the bridge scream, the hook drop, the lyric everyone quotes
2. **Cultural phrase potential** - "Hot Girl Summer" became language before it was a song
3. **Visual association** - every one has imagery people can recreate (dances, memes, aesthetic edits)
4. **Genre-bending** - none of them are "pure" anything
5. **Emotional anticipation** - they make you feel like good times are ahead
