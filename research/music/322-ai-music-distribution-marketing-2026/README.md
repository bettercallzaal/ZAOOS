# Doc 322: AI Music Distribution & Marketing for Independent Artists (2026)

**Date:** 2026-04-11
**Topic:** How independent artists distribute and market AI-generated music commercially
**Context:** A musician making original songs using AI tools (ElevenLabs, Suno, ACE-Step) with his own voice clone and original lyrics wants to distribute them commercially.

---

## 1. Distribution Platforms: Who Accepts AI-Generated Music?

### DistroKid - BEST OPTION FOR AI MUSIC
- **Policy:** Accepts AI-generated music with mandatory disclosure
- Every upload includes a mandatory disclosure step where you must indicate AI involvement
- Does not limit how many AI tracks you can upload
- AI-disclosed tracks are delivered to Spotify, Apple Music, Amazon Music, and 150+ platforms the same as human-produced tracks
- If their system identifies AI characteristics but the disclosure box was NOT checked, the track is flagged for review
- Confirmed undisclosed AI content is removed; repeat offenders risk suspension
- **Pricing:** Flat yearly fee ($22.99/year unlimited uploads)
- **Verdict:** Most AI-friendly major distributor

### UnitedMasters - ALLOWS AI
- Does not explicitly limit distribution of music created using AI tools
- No specific AI disclosure requirement documented yet
- **Pricing:** Free tier available, Select ($5.99/mo) and Exclusive ($14.99/mo) tiers

### RouteNote - ALLOWS AI
- Allows AI tracks according to 2026 data
- Free tier available (15% commission)

### Amuse - UNCLEAR
- No explicit AI policy documented
- Revamped pricing mid-2025: Artist ($23.99/yr), Artist Plus ($39.99/yr), Professional ($59.99/yr)

### TuneCore - RESTRICTIVE
- Supports GenAI only where underlying models rely on fully licensed datasets
- Will NOT distribute works that are 100% AI-generated
- AI-assisted tracks (where you wrote lyrics, provided voice, made creative decisions) may be accepted
- **Key risk:** If you used Suno (which faced copyright litigation), TuneCore may reject

### CD Baby - REJECTS AI
- "You will not be able to distribute AI-generated content"
- Even if the AI generator permits commercial use, CD Baby won't distribute it
- Cites inability to ensure unique sounds or rights compliance
- **Verdict:** Do not use for AI music

### Ditto Music - BLOCKS AI
- Has been blocking AI-generated releases from reaching Meta, TikTok, Apple Music, Amazon Music
- Takes a blanket stance calling it "AI-generated" and refusing delivery
- Even when lyrics are original and vocals use your own voice as base persona

### Bandcamp - BANS AI
- As of January 13, 2026, AI-generated music is no longer welcome
- "We want musicians to keep making music, and for fans to have confidence that the music they find on Bandcamp was created by humans"

### Unchained Music - PRO-HUMAN
- Markets itself with a "pro-human AI policy"
- Bundles marketing tools and editorial pitching

### RECOMMENDATION
**Use DistroKid as primary distributor.** It's the most transparent and AI-friendly. Consider UnitedMasters as backup. Avoid CD Baby, Ditto, and Bandcamp entirely for AI content.

## 2. Streaming Platform Policies (Spotify, Apple Music, YouTube Music)

### Spotify (2026)
- Allows AI-generated music with disclosure
- Enforces formal AI policy demanding: disclosure, ownership proof, ethical sourcing of training data
- AI music that impersonates artists is strictly prohibited
- Launched "Artist Profile Protection" - artists can review/reject releases before they appear under their name
- Rolling out spam filter to identify mass-uploading bad actors
- Adopted DDEX standard for AI disclosure (September 2025)
- Tracks labeled in credits showing AI involvement in vocals, instrumentation, or post-production
- Does NOT currently label AI content to listeners (unlike YouTube, Meta, TikTok)
- **Key fact:** 60,000+ AI tracks uploaded daily across all platforms

### Apple Music (2026)
- Launched voluntary "Transparency Tags" in March 2026
- Tags indicate AI involvement in four areas: artwork, tracks, compositions, music videos
- Currently voluntary for distributors to declare
- Does not reject AI music outright

### YouTube Music (2026)
- Expanding Content ID with AI-specific audio scanning
- Requires disclosure for AI-generated audio in video metadata and visual indicators
- Tag videos under "AI-generated" category when prompted
- Most mature AI labeling system among platforms

### Deezer
- Most aggressive against AI: 28% of uploads are AI but only 0.5% of streams
- Active AI detection with reduced recommendation exposure for fully AI-generated tracks
- Has "Slop Tracker" revealing how AI music impacts streaming revenue

## 3. Algorithmic Promotion vs. Suppression

### The Reality
- Spotify has NOT implemented significant algorithmic suppression of properly disclosed AI music (unlike Deezer)
- AI-generated tracks comprise over 25% of some users' Discover Weekly playlists
- Spotify expanding personalization may actually help niche AI tracks find audiences
- The spam filter targets mass-uploaders and manipulation, not individual quality AI releases

### What Gets Suppressed
- Bulk spam uploads (dozens of tracks per day)
- Tracks that trigger impersonation detection
- Content from accounts flagged for manipulation
- Undisclosed AI content caught by detection

### What Gets Through
- Properly disclosed AI-assisted tracks with genuine artistic merit
- Consistent release schedule (not spamming)
- Tracks with real engagement signals (saves, replays, shares)
- Well-produced, post-processed AI output

## 4. Labeling & Disclosure Best Practices

### Mandatory Requirements (2026)
- **DistroKid:** Check the AI disclosure box during upload (mandatory field)
- **Spotify:** DDEX metadata standard indicating where/how AI contributed
- **YouTube:** Metadata disclosure + visual indicators for AI content
- **Apple Music:** Transparency Tags (voluntary but recommended)
- **EU AI Act (August 2026):** Mandates labeling for ALL AI-generated content on streaming platforms

### Recommended Credit Format
```
Composed by [Artist Name], AI-assisted production using [Tool Name] (2026)
```
or
```
Written and performed by [Artist Name]. Production assisted by ElevenLabs/Suno/ACE-Step.
```

### Metadata Best Practices
1. Embed AI tool name, version, and dataset reference in file metadata
2. Specify what AI generated: vocals, instrumentation, post-production
3. Use consistent terminology: "AI-assisted" (not vague "co-generated")
4. Keep attribution logs documenting which AI models contributed
5. Confirm AI music model did not infringe third-party copyrights

### What NOT to Do
- Never claim "featuring [Famous Artist]" or "in the style of [Artist Name]"
- Never upload without checking the AI disclosure box
- Never use someone else's voice clone without licensing
- Never market AI output as purely human-made

### Platform Choice
- **TikTok:** Strongest discovery platform - 75% of users discover new music here
- **Instagram Reels:** Strong secondary platform, better for building engaged followers
- **YouTube Shorts:** Best monetization rates among short-form platforms

### Content Strategy
- Engineer a catchy, emotionally charged 10-20 second snippet from each song
- 80/20 rule: 80% entertainment/value content, 20% direct promotion
- Post 3-5x per week minimum on primary platform
- Emotional, mood-driven content outperforms dance routines in 2026
- Aesthetic edits and introspective videos around songs work well

2. Generate in Suno, pick best version
3. Upload through DistroKid/TuneCore
4. Run 6+ faceless YouTube Shorts/Instagram Reels channels by niche
5. Find trending video clips, pair with your music as background
6. Post 2-4 Shorts daily first 2 months, scale to 6-8/day
7. Run $5/day Facebook Ads targeting low-cost countries for YouTube visibility
8. Takes 3-4 months to warm up channels
9. Took 150 uploads before finding what worked
10. One lofi + anime clip made $1,000 in one month

### Key Tactics
- Trending hashtags, titles, and emotionally resonant scenes
- K-drama, anime, K-pop, cartoon niches perform well
- Volume over virality - compound results through consistency
- YouTube Shorts pays best, then Facebook Reels, then TikTok

## 9. Copyright Registration for AI-Assisted Music

### US Copyright Office Stance (January 2025 Ruling)
- **Purely AI-generated:** NOT copyrightable. "Prompts alone do not provide sufficient human control"
- **AI-assisted with human authorship:** CAN be copyrighted when it "embodies meaningful human authorship"
- Key factors for copyrightability:
  - Did you write original lyrics? (YES = strong claim)
  - Did you make specific creative decisions about arrangement/structure? (YES = helps)
  - Did you use your own voice clone? (Strengthens claim significantly)
  - Did you use AI as a tool in a larger creative process? (YES = strongest position)

### For Your Specific Case (Voice Clone + Original Lyrics + AI Production)
- **Original lyrics:** Copyrightable as literary work regardless of AI involvement
- **Voice clone of YOUR OWN voice:** Strengthens authorship claim
- **AI-generated instrumental/arrangement:** May not be independently copyrightable
- **Combined work:** Likely registrable if you can document meaningful human creative decisions
- **Best practice:** Register the lyrics separately as literary works, then register the sound recording emphasizing your creative contribution

### Practical Steps
1. Document every creative decision (lyrics, arrangement choices, vocal direction)
2. Keep records of AI tools used and your input at each stage
3. Register with US Copyright Office disclosing AI assistance
4. Register with a PRO (ASCAP/BMI/SESAC) for publishing royalties
5. Note: entirely AI-created music may face PRO registration obstacles

## 10. Making AI Music Sound "Human" Enough

### Why Tracks Get Rejected
- Most rejections are NOT because AI was detected
- Rejections happen because creators submit unfinished AI output directly
- The review evaluates: audio quality, credits, artwork, release behavior, overall coherence

### Post-Production Checklist
1. **Never go straight from generator to distributor** - always post-produce
2. Edit arrangement, timing, fades, sonic balance
3. Revise lyrics and song structure where needed
4. Normalize loudness (LUFS standards for streaming)
5. Check dynamic range and spectrum balance
6. Mix and master (or use AI mastering tools like LANDR, then review)
7. Create professional artwork (not AI slop)
8. Write coherent credits and metadata

### Production Quality Signals
- Proper intro/outro (not abrupt starts/ends)
- Natural-sounding transitions between sections
- Consistent vocal quality throughout
- Genre-appropriate mixing (e.g., bass-heavy for hip-hop)
- No digital artifacts or glitches (unless intentional)

### Using ACE-Step Effectively
- ACE-Step 1.5 (January 2026): open-source, MIT license, free for commercial use
- Trained on royalty-free non-copyrighted material (legal advantage)
- Outperforms Suno v5 on SongEval benchmark
- Supports: text-to-music (10s to 10min), cover generation, audio repainting, vocal-to-BGM
- LoRA fine-tuning for personalized style
- Runs on consumer hardware (<4GB VRAM)
- **Key advantage:** Full local control, no platform risk, no licensing questions

## 11. Playlist Pitching for AI Music

### Can AI Music Get on Editorial Playlists?
- No specific policy banning AI music from editorial playlists
- ~1 in 5 pitched songs get added to at least one editorial playlist
- Tracks pitched 14+ days early see ~2x editorial consideration rate vs. 7-day minimum
- Quality and engagement signals matter more than origin

### Strategy
1. Submit through Spotify for Artists at least 14 days before release
2. Fill out EVERY metadata field carefully
3. Focus on building engagement signals: saves, replays, follows
4. Don't rely solely on editorial - build multi-layered approach
5. Curator outreach to 20-50 relevant independent playlists per release
6. Use precision-targeted Meta ads ($5-10/day) sending right listeners to songs

### What Works Better Than Playlist Pitching
- Meta ads targeting specific demographics/interests that match your genre
- Building organic TikTok/Reels presence with song snippets
- Growing a genuine listener base that triggers algorithmic recommendations
- Consistent releases building momentum over time

## 12. Handling the AI Music Stigma

### The 2026 Landscape
- Stigma has evolved from hostility to cautious acceptance
- Transparency frameworks have matured
- "AI-assisted" is less stigmatized than "AI-generated"
- Your voice + your lyrics + AI production = most defensible position

### Framing Strategies
1. **Lead with your human contribution:** "I write every lyric, I trained my own voice model"
2. **Position AI as a tool:** "AI is my production partner, like a DAW or synthesizer"
3. **Be transparent early:** Mention AI in your artist bio and social posts
4. **Show the process:** Behind-the-scenes content of your creative workflow
5. **Emphasize what's yours:** Original lyrics, voice, creative direction, arrangement decisions

### What Successful Artists Do
- Grimes: "Here's my voice, make music with it" (radical openness)
- Holly Herndon: Governance + community + transparency
- Reddit practitioners: Don't disclose at all (risky but common)
- Best middle ground: Transparent about tools, emphasis on human creativity

### Language Guide
| Instead of... | Say... |
|---------------|--------|
| "AI-generated music" | "AI-assisted production" |
| "Made by AI" | "Produced with AI tools" |
| "AI song" | "Original song with AI production" |
| "AI vocals" | "My voice clone" or "voice model trained on my recordings" |
