## The 5 Structural Differences

### 1. Prediction Market, Not Sales

Every other onchain music platform is built on the NFT sale model: artist mints, collector buys, transaction ends. WaveWarZ replaces the transaction with a *race* — two songs enter, audiences bet on which wins, and the odds shift in real time as more bets flow in.

This means:
- An artist's song can generate revenue across dozens of battles (not just one primary mint)
- Volume compounds: a song that wins builds reputation → more battles → more volume
- The "floor" is not a static NFT price — it moves every time the song enters a new battle

### 2. The Loser Earns — No Zero-Sum Outcomes

Traditional prediction markets (Polymarket, Manifold) destroy the losing side. WaveWarZ is built differently: the losing artist and losing bettors receive a partial return, funded by a fee split across all participants.

**Why this matters for music:**
- Artists don't avoid battling because losing feels catastrophic
- High-quality songs can build a following even through losses (GodclouD: 8-0 undefeated; but artists with 30% win rates still earn meaningful SOL)
- The loser-earns mechanic is WaveWarZ's patent-defensible moat against general prediction market platforms entering music

### 3. Instant SOL Settlement

Spotify pays monthly, after deducting label cuts and distributor fees, on a minimum threshold. Sound.xyz and Catalog pay on the Ethereum network, which can cost $5–50 in gas for a single transaction.

WaveWarZ settles in SOL. At current fees, a WaveWarZ payout transaction costs $0.01. It lands in the artist's wallet in seconds, not months.

For independent artists in emerging markets — where $9.07 SOL total platform payouts represents real income — this timing difference matters.

### 4. Community Governance via Fractal DAO

Sound.xyz, Catalog, and Royal are corporate platforms. Artists use them, but have no vote in platform direction.

WaveWarZ is governed by The ZAO — a decentralized artist collective running weekly Fractal governance sessions on Optimism mainnet. Artists who are ZAO members can influence battle formats, fee structures, and platform direction through the Respect-weighted voting system.

This is the only onchain music platform with a live, verifiable, on-chain governance track record (157 unique Respect holders, 505 transactions).

### 5. Community Benefit Mode

WaveWarZ has a built-in charity battle format: when a battle is flagged as a benefit battle, the winning pool is redirected to a nonprofit instead of the trader prize pool. Two rounds have run, raising $1,497 for HuRya.

No other onchain music platform has a native charity mechanism verified onchain.

## Why WaveWarZ Is Not Competing with Spotify

A common framing error: "WaveWarZ wants to replace streaming." It doesn't.

WaveWarZ is a **participation layer** on top of streaming culture. Artists release music to Audius, Spotify, or SoundCloud — then they bring those songs into WaveWarZ to battle. WaveWarZ doesn't replace the discovery and listening layer; it adds a prediction market engagement layer on top.

The right mental model:
- Spotify = where you discover and listen to music
- WaveWarZ = where you bet on which music wins

This also means WaveWarZ is not in competition with Audius, Sound.xyz, or Catalog. It's additive. A song can be on all of them simultaneously.
