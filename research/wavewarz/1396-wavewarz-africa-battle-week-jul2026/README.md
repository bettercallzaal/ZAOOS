## What Africa Battle Week Is

**Format:** 7 consecutive days of WaveWarZ battles where both slots are filled by African music artists (any genre, any country).

**Who can battle:**
- African-born artists, regardless of current residence
- Artists making African-origin genre music (Afrobeats, amapiano, highlife, Afropop, Afro-house, Afrofusion, gqom)
- Diaspora artists with African heritage who identify with the scene

**Battle structure:** Identical to standard WaveWarZ — MAIN event format, community or quick battles, loser-earns economics apply. No rule changes for Africa Battle Week; the model speaks for itself.

**Partner:** RAM (Rhythm and Music) — referenced in doc 1355 as ZABAL S2 musician-track recruiting source. Confirm RAM as Africa Battle Week co-organizer (first DM: identify ZAO's Africa music contact, frame as co-promotion).

## Why This Works for WaveWarZ

**The loser-earns model is uniquely powerful in the African context:**
- Most African music platforms are streaming-only (low payout)
- African independent artists are highly motivated by any guaranteed payment per performance
- WaveWarZ's $1.31 loser-average is more significant in markets where streaming pennies are even smaller
- Battle-based competition culture (freestyle rap, dancing, music competition shows like Nigerian Idol, Coke Studio Africa) is native to the market

**Onchain access is increasing:**
- Nigeria leads global P2P crypto volume (Chainalysis 2023)
- Ghana, Kenya, South Africa all in top 20 global crypto adoption
- Solana (WaveWarZ's chain) has growing Africa-native developer + creator communities
