## What WaveWarZ Is (Plain English, 3 Paragraphs)

WaveWarZ is a music battle platform where two artists compete head-to-head and the audience bets on who they think will win. The audience bet is in SOL (Solana cryptocurrency), and the platform uses those bets like a real-time poll — the side with more SOL wins.

Here's what makes WaveWarZ different: **the losing artist gets paid.** When a battle closes, the losing artist receives a share of the SOL bet against them — roughly 1.73% of the losing pool. So every artist who participates earns something, regardless of whether they win or lose. That's structurally different from Spotify, YouTube, or any streaming platform.

As of July 2026, 1,245+ battles have been completed on WaveWarZ with over 524 SOL (~$104K) in total trading volume.

## What Winning and Losing Actually Means

| Outcome | Artist gets |
|---------|-------------|
| Win | Recognition, visibility, leaderboard position, trading volume |
| Lose | **Loser-earns payout** — a share of the SOL bet against you |

There is no financial penalty for losing. The worst case: you earn less than you would have if you won.

**The math:** If 10 SOL is bet against you and you lose, you receive approximately 0.173 SOL (~$34.60 at $200/SOL). If 100 SOL is bet against you and you lose, you receive approximately 1.73 SOL (~$346). Higher volume battles = higher payouts for both the winning trader side AND the losing artist.

## Battle Formats

### Quick Battle (easiest to enter)
- Any registered artist can create a Quick Battle at any time
- You challenge another registered artist
- The battle runs until it closes (time or volume threshold)
- Audience bets during the open window

**Best for:** Artists new to WaveWarZ who want to try the platform with low stakes.

### MAIN Battle (curated)
- Scheduled as part of a MAIN event (typically 3-5 battles in one show)
- Selected by ZAO/WaveWarZ; not self-service
- Higher audience + higher volume than Quick Battles
- Artists for MAIN events are recruited by ZAO team

**Best for:** Artists with an existing audience or catalog; higher expected earnings.

### Community Battle (charity)
- Portion of battle proceeds goes to a nonprofit
- ZAO selects charity; artists know in advance
- Same mechanism as Quick/MAIN; different narrative framing

**Best for:** Artists who want to participate in giving campaigns.

## How to Get Started

### Step 1: Set Up a Solana Wallet

You need a Solana wallet to receive your WaveWarZ earnings.

**Recommended:** Phantom Wallet (phantom.com)
- Download the browser extension or mobile app
- Create a new wallet and save your seed phrase securely
- Your wallet address will look like: `AbcDe123...` (base-58 encoded)

**Note:** WaveWarZ payouts go directly to your wallet address — no intermediary, no "withdraw" button to click. You can verify payouts on Solana Explorer.

### Step 2: Register on WaveWarZ

Go to **wavewarz.info** and register as an artist.

You'll need:
- Artist name
- Your Solana wallet address (from Step 1)
- Genre(s) you perform in
- At least 1 track link (SoundCloud, Spotify, YouTube, etc.)

### Step 3: Enter Your First Quick Battle

Once registered, you can enter a Quick Battle immediately.

- From your dashboard, click "Enter Quick Battle"
- Select an opponent (or request a matchup with a specific artist)
- Set the battle window (how long the battle is open for bets)
- Share your battle link with your audience — more bets = higher payout for both sides

**Best practice:** Post your battle link on X, Farcaster, Instagram, and your Discord. The more your fans bet on you, the higher the SOL pool — and the higher the loser-earns payout even if you lose.

## How Payouts Work

After a battle closes:
1. The winning side's traders claim their winnings
2. **The losing artist automatically receives their loser-earns payout to their Solana wallet**
3. Winning artists don't automatically receive a payout (the winning side is the trader side) — but winning increases your leaderboard rank and visibility

**Timeline:** Payouts are processed on-chain automatically within minutes of battle close. You can verify at solscan.io using your wallet address.

## Do I Need Cryptocurrency to Participate as an Artist?

**No.** You need a Solana wallet to receive payouts, but you don't need to buy any cryptocurrency. You earn SOL just by battling.

However, your fans and supporters need SOL to bet on your battles. The more crypto-native your audience, the higher your expected earnings.

## Artist FAQ

**Q: What happens if no one bets on my battle?**
A: Battles require minimum volume to close. A battle with very low total bets will result in a very small (or zero) payout. Quick Battles with no volume simply don't pay out much. MAIN events have a baseline audience, which is why they typically have higher volume.

**Q: Can I battle the same artist twice?**
A: Yes. Rematches are allowed and common on WaveWarZ.

**Q: What genres work best on WaveWarZ?**
A: Based on existing battles, hip-hop and R&B have the highest volume. However, WaveWarZ is genre-agnostic. Africa Battle Week (Sep 22-26, 2026) will be the first international expansion, featuring African music genres.

**Q: How do I get invited to a MAIN event?**
A: MAIN events are curated by the ZAO team. Contact @bettercallzaal on X or Farcaster to express interest, or fill in your profile completely at wavewarz.info — ZAO scouts the platform for emerging artists.

**Q: Is my loser-earns payout taxable?**
A: This depends on your jurisdiction. In the US, cryptocurrency received as income (including from battle payouts) is taxable. Consult a tax professional. WaveWarZ payouts are on-chain and verifiable — you can export your transaction history from Solana Explorer.

**Q: Can I withdraw my earnings?**
A: Yes — SOL in your Phantom wallet can be converted to USD via any cryptocurrency exchange (Coinbase, Kraken, etc.). WaveWarZ does not hold your earnings; they go directly to your wallet.
