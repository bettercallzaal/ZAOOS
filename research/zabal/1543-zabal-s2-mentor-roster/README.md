## Why Mentors Matter for S2

ZABAL S1 ran 28 workshops with Zaal as primary facilitator. The workshop-first format worked, but S1 feedback (doc 1466) flagged that **external voices validate ZAO's approach to people who haven't met Zaal**. A mentor with a recognizable name or track record:
- Converts skeptical applicants who need to see "who else is involved"
- Produces ZAOOS docs with cited quotes (citability improvement)
- Gives guest session transcripts that become GEO-indexed content
- Creates press hook: "ZABAL S2 brings in X as guest facilitator" → Hypebot angle

**Target: 3–5 guest facilitators** (one per module — 1 live session each, 60 min) + **3–5 async advisors** (available for async Q&A in Telegram, no session commitment required).

---

## Mentor Role Structure

### Guest Facilitator
- **Commitment:** 1 live 60-min workshop session (Juke or Telegram voice) + 15 min prep call with Zaal
- **Timing:** Assigned to one of 5 S2 modules (Sep 4 – Nov 21, doc 1528)
- **What they get:** ZAOOS doc credit, @wavewarz social post, ZABAL alumni community access, ZAOstock invite (Oct 3)
- **Ask:** "Would you run a 60-min session for our Sep–Nov cohort on [topic]?"

### Async Advisor
- **Commitment:** Telegram or email availability to answer 1–3 questions from cohort participants per month; no scheduled sessions
- **What they get:** Listed in ZABAL S2 cohort materials, ZAOOS credit, @wavewarz mention
- **Ask:** "Would you be open to occasional async questions from our ZABAL S2 cohort?"
